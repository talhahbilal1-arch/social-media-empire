The 5 Compound Lifts Every Man Should Master

Workouts | 8 min read

If you're a man over 35 looking to build real, functional strength while maximizing your time in the gym, compound lifts are your best friend. These multi-joint movements recruit multiple muscle groups simultaneously, making them incredibly efficient for building muscle, burning fat, and boosting testosterone.

Unlike isolation exercises that target single muscles, compound movements mirror real-world activities and build the kind of practical strength you need as you age. Here are the five essential compound lifts every man should master.

1. The Squat - King of All Exercises

Muscles Worked

Quadriceps, glutes, hamstrings, core, lower back

Why It Matters After 35

The squat builds the leg strength essential for maintaining mobility and independence as you age. It also triggers significant testosterone and growth hormone release.

Key Form Tips

  • Keep your chest up and core braced throughout the movement
  • Push your knees out in line with your toes
  • Go as deep as your mobility allows while maintaining a neutral spine
  • Drive through your heels on the way up
Pro Tip: If mobility is an issue, start with goblet squats. Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell at your chest - this helps counterbalance and allows you to squat deeper with better form.

2. The Deadlift - Total Body Power

Muscles Worked

Entire posterior chain: hamstrings, glutes, lower back, upper back, traps, forearms

Why It Matters After 35

The deadlift strengthens your entire back side, which is crucial for posture and preventing the back pain that plagues many men in their 40s and beyond.

Key Form Tips

  • Start with the bar over mid-foot, shins nearly touching
  • Keep your back flat - never round your lower back
  • Push the floor away rather than pulling the bar up
  • Keep the bar close to your body throughout

3. The Bench Press - Upper Body Foundation

Muscles Worked

Chest, front deltoids, triceps

Why It Matters After 35

Beyond building an impressive chest, the bench press maintains pushing strength essential for daily activities and sports.

Key Form Tips

  • Retract your shoulder blades and create an arch in your upper back
  • Lower the bar to your lower chest/nipple line
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor and drive through them
  • Don't bounce the bar off your chest

4. The Overhead Press - Shoulder Strength & Stability

Muscles Worked

Shoulders, triceps, upper chest, core

Why It Matters After 35

Shoulder injuries become more common as we age. The overhead press builds the strength and stability to protect these vulnerable joints.

Key Form Tips

  • Start with the bar at shoulder height, elbows slightly in front
  • Brace your core and squeeze your glutes
  • Press straight up, moving your head back slightly to clear the bar
  • Lock out fully at the top

5. The Barbell Row - Back Builder

Muscles Worked

Lats, rhomboids, rear deltoids, biceps, lower back

Why It Matters After 35

Counteracts the forward-hunched posture from desk work. A strong back is essential for a healthy spine and athletic performance.

Key Form Tips

  • Hinge at the hips with a slight knee bend
  • Keep your back flat and core engaged
  • Pull to your lower chest/upper stomach
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top

Programming These Lifts

For men over 35, I recommend the following approach:

  1. Frequency: Hit each lift 2x per week for optimal muscle protein synthesis
  2. Sets/Reps: 3-4 sets of 6-8 reps for strength, 3 sets of 8-12 for hypertrophy
  3. Rest: Take 2-3 minutes between heavy sets - recovery matters more as we age
  4. Progression: Add weight only when you can complete all reps with perfect form
Recovery Note: At 35+, your recovery ability isn't what it was at 25. Listen to your body, prioritize sleep, and don't be afraid to take an extra rest day when needed.

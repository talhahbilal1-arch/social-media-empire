## 1. Algorithm Ranking Signals (2026)

What Pinterest actually rewards, ranked by impact:

1. **Domain quality** — custom domains with consistent content (we have this)
2. **Keyword optimization** — Pinterest is a search engine first, social network second
3. **Pin quality score** — high-res images, proper aspect ratio, text readability
4. **Save rate** — the #1 engagement signal. Saves >>> likes. A save = "I want this later" = highest intent
5. **Board-to-pin semantic match** — "Best Cooling Sheets for Night Sweats" board >>> "Health Stuff" board
6. **Fresh pin priority** — 3-5 different designs per article URL, staggered over days
7. **Visual dwell time + closeups** — people zooming in = quality signal to algorithm
8. **Click-through rate** — clicks to your site prove the content delivers on the promise

Key insight: Pinterest distributes pins that perform well in the first few hours. Early saves and clicks trigger wider distribution. Timing matters.

## 2. Pin Design Formula

### Dimensions & Format
- **Size:** 1000 x 1500px (2:3 ratio) — non-negotiable
- **Format:** PNG or JPEG, under 10MB
- **Orientation:** Always vertical — horizontal gets crushed in the feed

### Text Overlay Rules
- **Word count:** 3-8 words MAX on the pin image. Details go in description.
- **Font:** Bold sans-serif (Inter, Montserrat, DM Sans). No thin scripts. No cursive.
- **Font size:** 40-100pt minimum. Must be readable on a phone screen.
- **Position:** Title in the top 1/3 (never gets cut off in feed). CTA at bottom if space allows.
- **Coverage:** Text should cover no more than 40% of the image area

### Color Strategy
Bright/saturated colors outperform muted tones in the feed. High contrast = readable = clickable.

**Brand-specific palettes:**

| Brand | Background/Overlay | Accent | Text Color | Vibe |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| FitOver35 | Dark navy (#1a1a2e) | Red (#e94560) | White (#FFFFFF) | Bold, masculine, authority |
| DailyDealDarling | Warm cream (#FAF9F6) | Gold (#D4AF37) | Dark (#1A1A1A) | Elegant, editorial, trust |
| Menopause Planner | Sage (#A5A58D) | Rose (#DDBEA9) | White (#FFFFFF) | Calming, supportive, warm |

**What NOT to do:**
- No pure white backgrounds (invisible in feed)
- No low-contrast text (light gray on white = death)
- No cluttered designs with 5 different fonts
- No tiny text that requires zooming

### Image Selection
- Lifestyle photos (person using/doing the thing) > product-only shots
- Pexels/Unsplash free stock is fine — the text overlay is what matters
- Food, fitness action shots, cozy home scenes perform best in our niches

## 3. Title Formulas (8 Proven Structures)

Use these EXACT structures. Rotate through them for variety.

### Formula 1: Number + Adjective + Result
`[Number] [Adjective] Ways to [Result]`

- FO35: "7 Proven Ways to Build Muscle After 40"
- DDD: "9 Genius Amazon Finds Under $25"
- Meno: "5 Natural Ways to Stop Hot Flashes"

### Formula 2: How To Without Pain Point
`How to [Result] Without [Pain Point]`

- FO35: "How to Lose Belly Fat Without Cardio"
- DDD: "How to Organize Your Kitchen Without Spending $100"
- Meno: "How to Sleep Through Menopause Without Medication"

### Formula 3: The Definitive Guide
`The [Adjective] Guide to [Topic]`

- FO35: "The Complete Guide to Home Gym Setup Under $500"
- DDD: "The Ultimate Guide to Self-Care on a Budget"
- Meno: "The Honest Guide to HRT in 2026"

### Formula 4: Audience + Constraint
`[Result] for [Audience] Who [Constraint]`

- FO35: "Strength Training for Dads Who Have 30 Minutes"
- DDD: "Self-Care Products for Moms Who Never Have Time"
- Meno: "Supplements for Women Who Can't Take HRT"

### Formula 5: Stop / Do This Instead
`Stop [Bad Thing] — Do This Instead`

- FO35: "Stop Doing Crunches — Do This for Abs Instead"
- DDD: "Stop Buying Full-Price — Save 40% With This Trick"
- Meno: "Stop Ignoring Brain Fog — Try These 3 Fixes"

### Formula 6: Things You Didn't Know
`[Number] Things You Didn't Know About [Topic]`

- FO35: "5 Things You Didn't Know About Creatine After 35"
- DDD: "8 Things You Didn't Know About Amazon Subscribe & Save"
- Meno: "6 Things Nobody Tells You About Perimenopause"

### Formula 7: Best Products (Year)
`Best [Products] for [Specific Need] (2026)`

- FO35: "Best Protein Powder for Men Over 40 (2026)"
- DDD: "Best Kitchen Gadgets Under $25 (2026)"
- Meno: "Best Cooling Sheets for Night Sweats (2026)"

### Formula 8: Versus / Comparison
`[Thing] vs [Thing]: Which Is Better for [Audience]?`

- FO35: "Creatine vs Pre-Workout: Which After 35?"
- DDD: "Dyson vs Dupe: Which Air Wrap Is Worth It?"
- Meno: "HRT vs Natural Remedies: What Actually Works?"

### Title Rules
- First 30-40 characters are what shows in feed — front-load the hook
- Odd numbers convert better than even (7 > 8, 5 > 6)
- Include one long-tail keyword naturally
- Max 100 characters total (aim for 60-80)

## 5. Posting Strategy

### Volume & Timing
- **Daily:** 5-10 pins/day (current ~7/day is in the sweet spot)
- **Best times:** Evening 8-11 PM + early morning 6-8 AM
- **Spread:** Throughout the day, not all at once
- **Current Make.com schedule:** Already doing this well — no changes needed

### Fresh Pin Strategy (this is the growth lever)
For every article URL, create 3-5 different pin designs:
1. **Variant A:** Number-led title ("7 Ways to...")
2. **Variant B:** Question-led title ("Can You Build Muscle After 40?")
3. **Variant C:** Different image, same title angle
4. **Variant D:** Different color overlay
5. **Variant E:** "Best of" / product-roundup angle

Stagger releases: Mon → Thu → following Mon. Never post all variants the same day.

### Board Strategy
- Each pin goes to ONE highly-specific board
- Board names must be keyword-rich:
  - GOOD: "Protein Supplements for Men Over 35"
  - BAD: "Fitness Stuff"
  - GOOD: "Menopause Sleep Solutions"
  - BAD: "Health"
- 10-15 boards per brand is the sweet spot
- Board descriptions: 50-500 characters with keywords

## 7. What to A/B Test First

Priority experiments, in order:

### Test 1: Title Style (Week 1-2)
- **Group A:** 10 pins with number-led titles ("7 Ways to...")
- **Group B:** 10 pins with question-led titles ("Can You...?")
- **Measure:** CTR after 14 days
- **Hypothesis:** Numbers will win for product/guide content; questions may win for emotional/health content

### Test 2: Color Overlay (Week 3-4)
- **Group A:** Dark overlay + white text (current FO35 style)
- **Group B:** Bright/saturated overlay + dark text
- **Same article, different designs**
- **Measure:** Save rate + CTR

### Test 3: Image Type (Week 5-6)
- **Group A:** Lifestyle photo (person using product / doing exercise)
- **Group B:** Product-only or infographic-style
- **Measure:** Closeup rate + save rate

### How to measure
Pinterest Analytics → Last 30 days → Sort by outbound clicks. Compare Group A vs Group B pins. Need at least 1,000 impressions per pin before results are meaningful.

# Chapter 11: The Macro Calculator (Worked Example)

This chapter walks through the exact math for one person so you can replicate it for yourself. No apps required, just a calculator and the formulas from Chapter 5.

## Meet the Example

- Age: 42
- Weight: 195 pounds (88.6 kg)
- Height: 5 feet 10 inches (177.8 cm)
- Occupation: desk job, mostly seated
- Training: 3 days per week (following the Chapter 4 program)
- Daily steps: approximately 6,000
- Goal: lose 15 pounds of fat while preserving muscle

## Step 1: Calculate Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation for men:

BMR = (10 x weight in kg) + (6.25 x height in cm) - (5 x age) + 5

BMR = (10 x 88.6) + (6.25 x 177.8) - (5 x 42) + 5

BMR = 886 + 1,111.25 - 210 + 5

**BMR = 1,792 calories/day**

This is what his body burns at complete rest.

## Step 2: Apply Activity Multiplier

| Activity Level | Multiplier |
|---|---|
| Sedentary (desk job, under 5,000 steps) | 1.2 |
| Lightly active (desk job, 5,000 to 7,500 steps, 3x training) | 1.375 |
| Moderately active (active job or 7,500 to 10,000 steps, 3 to 4x training) | 1.55 |
| Very active (physical job, 10,000+ steps, 4 to 5x training) | 1.725 |

Our example has a desk job, 6,000 steps, and trains 3 days per week. That puts him at "Lightly active" with a multiplier of 1.375.

TDEE = BMR x Activity Multiplier

TDEE = 1,792 x 1.375

**TDEE = 2,464 calories/day (maintenance)**

## Step 3: Set the Deficit

For fat loss while preserving muscle, subtract 400 calories from maintenance. This creates a moderate deficit that allows recovery from training.

Target intake = 2,464 - 400

**Target intake = 2,064 calories/day**

At this deficit, expect to lose approximately 0.7 to 0.9 pounds per week. Patience is required. At 15 pounds to lose, this takes roughly 17 to 21 weeks. If you want to accelerate, increase daily steps to 8,000 to 10,000 before cutting more food.

## Step 4: Calculate Macros

### Protein (the anchor)

1 gram per pound of bodyweight.

Protein = 195 grams = 780 calories (4 calories per gram)

### Fat (the floor)

0.4 grams per pound of bodyweight (minimum for hormonal health).

Fat = 78 grams = 702 calories (9 calories per gram)

### Carbs (the remainder)

Remaining calories = 2,064 - 780 - 702 = 582 calories

Carbs = 582 / 4 = 145 grams

## The Final Numbers

| Macro | Grams | Calories | % of Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Protein | 195g | 780 | 38% |
| Fat | 78g | 702 | 34% |
| Carbs | 145g | 582 | 28% |
| **Total** | | **2,064** | **100%** |

## How to Distribute Through the Day

| Meal | Protein | Carbs | Fat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Breakfast (7 AM) | 45g | 35g | 20g |
| Lunch (12 PM) | 50g | 40g | 20g |
| Pre-workout snack (4 PM) | 20g | 30g | 8g |
| Dinner (7 PM) | 50g | 30g | 25g |
| Evening snack (9 PM) | 30g | 10g | 5g |
| **Total** | **195g** | **145g** | **78g** |

Note: these are targets, not mandates. Hitting within 10 grams on protein and within 15 grams on carbs and fat is close enough. Precision matters less than consistency.

## What Changes as He Loses Weight

Every 10 pounds lost, recalculate. At 185 pounds:

- New BMR: 1,737
- New TDEE: 2,388
- New target: 1,988 calories
- Protein stays at 185g (tracks current weight)
- Recalculate fat and carbs with the leftover

This recalculation prevents the dreaded "plateau" that is usually just a shrinking deficit from a shrinking body.

## Common Calculation Errors

1. Using "very active" multiplier when you sit 10 hours a day (the 3 training sessions do not make a desk job "very active")
2. Not counting liquid calories (a daily latte with whole milk adds 150 to 200 calories)
3. Forgetting cooking oils (one tablespoon of olive oil is 120 calories and 14 grams of fat)
4. Double-counting exercise calories from a fitness tracker (your TDEE multiplier already accounts for training; do not subtract again)

## The Problem

Generic fitness programs break men over 35 for three specific reasons:

1. **Volume assumptions.** Five days a week, two-hour sessions, daily cardio. You have a job, kids, and a spouse. The math doesn't work.
2. **Recovery assumptions.** Young lifters recover from a heavy squat day in 48 hours. You need 72-96. The program doesn't account for this and you spiral into overtraining.
3. **Injury assumptions.** Hip mobility, shoulder impingement, cranky lower back — the 22-year-old program assumes you don't have any of these. You do. Within 4 weeks, you're hurt.

The result: you start strong, plateau by week 6, get injured by week 10, and quit by week 12 — convinced it's your age.

It isn't. It's the program.

---

## The Solution

The Blueprint is a 12-week, three-phase system built specifically for the 35-55 year old man with 3-5 hours per week:

- **Phase 1 — Rebuild (weeks 1-4):** Re-establish movement patterns, fix the mobility holes, build a base of volume without breaking you.
- **Phase 2 — Grow (weeks 5-8):** Progressive overload on four lifts, supported by accessory work chosen to protect your joints — not copy an IFBB pro split.
- **Phase 3 — Cut (weeks 9-12):** Deliberate caloric deficit with protein held high, lifts maintained, and a refeed protocol so you don't crater your sleep or sex drive.

Every protocol is explicit. No "listen to your body" hand-waving. No mindset fluff. Numbers, sets, reps, grams, hours.

# Dinner Templates

Six templates. Pick four for your rotation. Dinner is where most of the cooking happens, where leftovers for lunch get generated, and where weekend meals diverge from weekday meals. Build for both.

Each template hits 40-60g protein at the standard portion. All numbers are at standard portion and rounded to the nearest 5 calories.

## Template D1: Beef and Starch and Vegetable

**Structure:** lean ground beef or sirloin + potato or rice + roasted vegetable.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz cooked lean ground beef (93/7) or sirloin steak
- 1 medium baked potato or 3/4 cup rice
- 2 cups roasted vegetables (broccoli, brussels sprouts, carrots, peppers)
- 1 teaspoon olive oil for roasting

**Macros (ground beef):** 48g protein, 42g carbs, 18g fat. About 530 calories.
**Macros (sirloin):** 50g protein, 42g carbs, 14g fat. About 500 calories.

**Notes:** 93/7 ground beef is the macro sweet spot. 80/20 doubles the fat at the same protein. Sirloin runs leaner than ribeye at half the price per pound. Salt and pepper is enough seasoning; complex sauces add calories you did not budget for.

## Template D2: Chicken Sheet Pan

**Structure:** chicken thighs or breasts + chopped vegetables + olive oil + spice blend, all on one sheet pan.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz cooked chicken (boneless thigh or breast)
- 2 cups chopped vegetables (any combination)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 cup cooked rice or 1 medium sweet potato on the side

**Macros (breast + sweet potato):** 52g protein, 35g carbs, 14g fat. About 480 calories.
**Macros (thigh + rice):** 45g protein, 45g carbs, 18g fat. About 510 calories.

**Notes:** Sheet pan dinners are the lowest-effort hot meal in the planner. 425 degrees, 25 minutes for breast, 30 minutes for thigh. Cook a double batch and Lunch Template L5 is handled.

## Template D3: Fish and Greens

**Structure:** salmon, white fish, or shrimp + leafy or cruciferous vegetable + carb on the side.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz salmon, cod, or shrimp
- 2 cups sauteed spinach, kale, or broccoli
- 3/4 cup rice, quinoa, or 1 medium potato
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- Lemon and herbs

**Macros (salmon):** 40g protein, 38g carbs, 20g fat. About 510 calories.
**Macros (cod):** 40g protein, 38g carbs, 7g fat. About 380 calories.
**Macros (shrimp):** 36g protein, 38g carbs, 6g fat. About 360 calories.

**Notes:** Frozen wild salmon is half the price of fresh and the macros are identical. Cod and shrimp are the leanest proteins in the planner; pair them with a higher-fat sauce or extra olive oil if your day is short on fat.

## Template D4: Vegetarian Protein Bowl

**Structure:** legume or tofu base + grain + roasted vegetables + tahini or yogurt sauce.

**Standard portion:**

- 1 cup cooked lentils or 6 oz firm tofu or 1 cup chickpeas
- 3/4 cup cooked quinoa or rice
- 2 cups roasted vegetables
- 2 tablespoons tahini or Greek yogurt sauce

**Macros (lentils + quinoa):** 28g protein, 65g carbs, 10g fat. About 470 calories.
**Macros (tofu + rice):** 28g protein, 50g carbs, 14g fat. About 440 calories.

**Notes:** This is the lowest-protein-per-calorie template in the planner. If you eat vegetarian dinners, plan to push protein at breakfast (B1 scaled to 60g) and lunch (L1 or L3) to land your daily total. Plant proteins are real protein, but they are dilute compared to chicken or whey.

## Template D5: One-Pan Pasta or Stir-Fry

**Structure:** lean protein + carb base (pasta or noodles or rice) + vegetables + sauce, all in one pan.

**Standard portion:**

- 5 oz cooked chicken, shrimp, or lean ground turkey
- 2 oz dry pasta, 3 oz dry rice noodles, or 3/4 cup cooked rice
- 2 cups vegetables
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce or marinara
- 1 teaspoon oil

**Macros:** 42g protein, 50g carbs, 10g fat. About 450 calories.

**Notes:** This template moves fast on a busy weeknight. Total cook time is around 20 minutes from cold pan to plated. Watch the sauce. Bottled stir-fry sauces and creamy pasta sauces can add 100-200 calories per serving. Soy sauce, marinara, and lemon-garlic-oil are the cleanest options.

## Template D6: Slow Cooker or Instant Pot Protein

**Structure:** large cut of protein cooked low and slow + side starch + side vegetable, prepared in two stages.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz pulled chicken, pulled pork (loin, not shoulder), or shredded beef (lean cut)
- 1 cup rice, 1 medium baked potato, or 2 small corn tortillas
- 2 cups vegetables (slaw, salad, or roasted)

**Macros (pulled chicken + rice):** 48g protein, 50g carbs, 8g fat. About 470 calories.
**Macros (pulled pork loin + tortillas):** 45g protein, 35g carbs, 12g fat. About 440 calories.

**Notes:** This is the prep-once-eat-four-times template. A 3-pound pork loin or 4 pounds of chicken thighs cooks in 4-6 hours and produces 6-8 servings of protein. Refrigerate, reheat, eat across the week. Bottled barbecue sauce can add 70-150 calories per serving; use a tablespoon, not a quarter cup.

## How to pick your four

Pick one no-think weeknight template (D2 or D5). Pick one prep-batch template (D6). Pick one fish or lean option (D3) to keep your weekly fat intake balanced. The fourth is preference. If you cook for a family, D6 is the highest-leverage choice in the planner; one batch feeds four people for two nights.

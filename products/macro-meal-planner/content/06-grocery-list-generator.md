# Grocery List Generator

Building a weekly grocery list should take 10 minutes, not an hour of recipe browsing. This chapter gives you the system: pick your templates for the week, check the master ingredient list, buy what you are missing.

## The Template Rotation Method

Look at your week ahead. Count your meals: 7 breakfasts, 7 lunches, 7 dinners, 7 snacks (if applicable). Pick from the templates in Chapters 2 through 5.

A sample week:

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner | Snack |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|-------|
| Mon | Protein Oats | Bowl | Chicken + Rice + Veg | Greek Yogurt |
| Tue | Egg Scramble | Wrap | One-Pan Fish | Protein Shake |
| Wed | Protein Oats | Salad | Slow-Cooker Beef | Cottage Cheese |
| Thu | Breakfast Sandwich | Bowl | Chicken + Potato + Veg | Greek Yogurt |
| Fri | Greek Yogurt Bowl | Leftover Container | Vegetarian Stir-Fry | Protein Bar |
| Sat | Egg Scramble | Wrap | Steak + Sweet Potato | None |
| Sun | Protein Oats | Salad | Meal Prep for Monday | Protein Shake |

## Building the List

From the rotation above, extract the protein sources:

- Chicken breast or thighs: 2 to 3 pounds (covers Mon, Thu dinners + leftover lunch)
- Ground beef: 1.5 pounds (Wed slow cooker)
- White fish fillets: 1 pound (Tue dinner)
- Steak: 2 portions (Sat dinner)
- Eggs: 18 (Tue/Sat scrambles + Thu sandwich)
- Greek yogurt: 32 oz tub (Mon/Thu snacks + Fri breakfast)
- Whey protein: already in pantry (Tue/Sun shakes)

Then carb sources:

- Oats: 2 cups (Mon/Wed/Sun breakfasts)
- Rice: 2 cups dry (Mon/Thu dinners + bowls)
- Wraps: 4 large (Tue/Fri lunches)
- Sweet potato: 2 (Sat dinner)
- Bread: half loaf (Thu sandwich)
- Pasta or noodles: 1 box (Fri stir-fry)

Then produce:

- Mixed greens: 2 bags (salads)
- Bell peppers: 4 (scrambles + stir-fry)
- Onions: 3 (universal)
- Broccoli: 2 heads (dinner sides)
- Spinach: 1 bag (bowls + scrambles)
- Bananas: 6 (oats + snacks)
- Berries: 2 pints (yogurt bowl + oats)
- Avocados: 3 (bowls + wraps)

Then fats and pantry:

- Olive oil: check supply
- Nut butter: check supply
- Cooking spray: check supply
- Seasoning (salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika): check supply

## The "Check Supply" Rule

Pantry staples (oils, spices, nut butters, protein powder, oats) last multiple weeks. Do not re-buy every trip. Before you leave for the store, open the pantry and check levels. Restock only what is actually low. This prevents the accumulation of 4 half-used jars of peanut butter.

## Scaling for Calories

If your target is 2,000 calories per day, the portions above work roughly as written. For higher targets:

- 2,500 calories: add 25 percent more carb sources (extra cup of rice, extra potato, larger oat portions)
- 3,000 calories: add 25 percent more carbs AND increase fat sources (extra tablespoon of oil, extra avocado, full-fat dairy instead of low-fat)

For lower targets (1,600 to 1,800): reduce carbs by 25 percent and keep protein and fat portions unchanged.

# Lunch Templates

Six templates. Pick four for your rotation. Lunch is where most plans collapse because lunch is portable, often eaten at a desk, and frequently bought rather than made. These templates account for that.

Each template hits 40-60g protein at the standard portion. All numbers are calculated at the standard portion and rounded to the nearest 5 calories.

## Template L1: The Bowl

**Structure:** lean protein + grain or starch + roasted or raw vegetables + sauce or dressing + optional fat.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz cooked chicken breast, ground turkey, or lean ground beef (93/7)
- 3/4 cup cooked rice, quinoa, or farro
- 2 cups vegetables (any combination)
- 2 tablespoons sauce (salsa, light vinaigrette, tahini thinned with water)
- 1/4 avocado or 1 tablespoon olive oil

**Macros:** 48g protein, 50g carbs, 16g fat. About 510 calories.

**Notes:** The most flexible template in the planner. Almost any combination of protein, grain, and vegetable works as long as the sauce is not a hidden calorie bomb. Restaurant bowls (Chipotle, Cava, Sweetgreen) are this template; build them with the same logic.

## Template L2: The Wrap

**Structure:** large tortilla + protein + vegetables + light sauce + optional cheese.

**Standard portion:**

- 1 large flour or low-carb tortilla
- 5 oz deli turkey, shredded chicken, or canned tuna mixed with Greek yogurt
- 1.5 cups vegetables (lettuce, tomato, cucumber, bell pepper)
- 1 tablespoon mustard or hot sauce
- 1 oz cheese, optional

**Macros (no cheese):** 42g protein, 35g carbs, 9g fat. About 380 calories.
**Macros (with cheese):** 49g protein, 35g carbs, 18g fat. About 470 calories.

**Notes:** Watch the tortilla. A "burrito-size" tortilla can run 220 calories on its own. Mission Carb Balance and similar low-carb wraps cut that in half. Read the label.

## Template L3: The Salad

**Structure:** greens + protein + complex carb (croutons, beans, grain) + vegetables + dressing.

**Standard portion:**

- 3 cups mixed greens
- 6 oz grilled chicken or 1 can tuna or 4 hard-boiled eggs
- 1/2 cup cooked quinoa or chickpeas
- 1.5 cups raw vegetables
- 2 tablespoons dressing (oil-based, not creamy)

**Macros (chicken version):** 50g protein, 32g carbs, 17g fat. About 480 calories.

**Notes:** Salad is the easiest template to ruin. A "Cobb salad" at most chains lands at 900-1200 calories because the dressing is half the dish. Use 2 tablespoons measured, not poured. Creamy dressings double the fat at the same flavor; oil-based vinaigrettes are the safer default.

## Template L4: The Sandwich

**Structure:** bread + lean protein + cheese + vegetables + condiment.

**Standard portion:**

- 2 slices whole grain bread (about 80 calories per slice)
- 5 oz deli turkey, roast beef, or chicken
- 1 slice cheese (1 oz)
- Lettuce, tomato, onion, pickle
- Mustard or 1 tablespoon light mayo

**Macros:** 45g protein, 38g carbs, 14g fat. About 460 calories.

**Notes:** Quality of the deli meat matters more than people think. Boar's Head and grocery deli ovengold-style turkey hit 5g protein per ounce. Bottom-shelf processed turkey can be 3g protein per ounce at half the cost; you eat more for the same protein and your sodium climbs. Pay for the better meat.

## Template L5: Leftover Protein + Carb + Vegetable

**Structure:** whatever protein you cooked the night before + a starch + a microwaved vegetable.

**Standard portion:**

- 6 oz of last night's protein (chicken, beef, fish, pork)
- 1 cup cooked rice, sweet potato, or pasta
- 1.5 cups vegetables (frozen steamable bag works)
- 1 tablespoon sauce or olive oil

**Macros:** 45-55g protein, 45g carbs, 12-18g fat. 470-560 calories depending on protein source.

**Notes:** This is the highest-leverage template in the planner. Cook a double portion at dinner and you have lunch the next day at zero additional time. Two batches of dinner can become four lunches. Frozen vegetables in steam bags are not a compromise; they are nutritionally close to fresh and they cost half as much.

## Template L6: The Prep Container

**Structure:** pre-portioned protein + grain + vegetable, built five at a time on Sunday.

**Standard portion (per container):**

- 5 oz cooked protein
- 3/4 cup cooked grain
- 2 cups roasted vegetables
- 2 tablespoons sauce (in a separate small container; add at meal time)

**Macros:** 42g protein, 45g carbs, 12g fat. About 450 calories.

**Notes:** This template is the Sunday prep workflow's primary output (see Section 7). Build five containers, eat five lunches, do not think about lunch from Monday through Friday. Sauce goes in a separate container so the food does not get soggy. Glass containers reheat better than plastic and last longer.

## How to pick your four

Pick at least one no-cook template (L2, L3, or L4) for days when prep falls apart. Pick at least one prep-based template (L5 or L6) for the weeks when prep happens. The remaining two are personal preference. If you eat the same lunch four days in a row and it does not bother you, that is a feature.

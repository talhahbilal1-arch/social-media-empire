# Snacks and Liquid Calories

Snacks fill protein gaps. Drinks blow holes in your calorie budget. Both deserve a system.

## The four snack templates

Pick two for your rotation. Each one is built to hit 15-25g protein and stay under 250 calories. Snacks are not meals; they are small, targeted, and they have a job.

### Snack S1: Cottage Cheese and Fruit

**Structure:** 3/4 cup low-fat cottage cheese + 1/2 cup berries or 1 small apple, chopped.

**Macros:** 21g protein, 18g carbs, 3g fat. About 180 calories.

**Notes:** Good Culture and Daisy are the cleanest brands. Some lower-end cottage cheeses are watery and salty; pay an extra dollar for a brand you actually want to eat.

### Snack S2: Greek Yogurt with Whey Stir-In

**Structure:** 1 cup plain 0% Greek yogurt + 1 scoop whey + 1 tablespoon nut butter or 1/4 cup berries.

**Macros (with nut butter):** 47g protein, 12g carbs, 8g fat. About 290 calories.
**Macros (with berries):** 47g protein, 18g carbs, 1g fat. About 245 calories.

**Notes:** This is the highest-protein snack in the planner. Use it on training days or any day you are 30g short by 4 PM.

### Snack S3: Hard-Boiled Eggs and Jerky or Nuts

**Structure:** 2 hard-boiled eggs + 1 oz beef jerky or 1 oz almonds.

**Macros (with jerky):** 22g protein, 6g carbs, 11g fat. About 220 calories.
**Macros (with almonds):** 18g protein, 4g carbs, 16g fat. About 240 calories.

**Notes:** Jerky drives the protein hit; almonds drive the fat. Choose based on what your day needs. Both are shelf-stable for travel and desk drawers.

### Snack S4: Protein Shake

**Structure:** 1.5 scoops whey + 1 cup unsweetened almond milk or water + optional 1/2 banana.

**Macros (water):** 36g protein, 4g carbs, 2g fat. About 180 calories.
**Macros (with banana):** 37g protein, 18g carbs, 2g fat. About 240 calories.

**Notes:** The simplest protein in the planner. A shaker bottle and a scoop bag in your gym bag covers post-workout. Whey isolate is more expensive than concentrate; both work. Plant-based protein blends from Orgain and Owyn run within a few grams of whey at the same scoop size.

## Liquid calories: the silent budget killer

Most people who track macros forget what they drink. A medium oat milk latte is 230 calories. Two of them is a whole meal. Three glasses of wine on Friday is 360 calories before you have eaten anything. Add it up across a week and you are 1,500-2,500 calories over your target without a single "bad" food.

### What to count, what to ignore

**Always count:**

- Coffee drinks with milk, syrup, or whipped cream (latte, cappuccino, macchiato, mocha)
- Smoothies, juices, and any blended drink
- Beer, wine, cocktails, hard seltzers
- Sweetened tea, lemonade, sports drinks
- Plant milks added to anything (oat milk has more carbs than dairy milk)

**Do not count:**

- Black coffee
- Plain or sparkling water
- Unsweetened tea
- Diet sodas (debatable on long-term health; calorically irrelevant)
- A splash of milk or cream under 2 tablespoons per day

### Reference numbers (memorize these)

| Drink | Approximate calories |
|-------|---------------------|
| 12 oz light beer | 100-110 |
| 12 oz regular beer | 150-180 |
| 12 oz IPA or stout | 200-250 |
| 5 oz red or white wine | 120-130 |
| 1.5 oz spirit, neat or with soda water | 95-110 |
| Cocktail with juice or syrup | 200-400 |
| Hard seltzer | 95-100 |
| Medium latte (whole milk) | 180-220 |
| Medium latte (oat milk) | 230-280 |
| Frappuccino or sweetened blended drink | 350-500 |
| 16 oz fruit smoothie | 300-450 |
| 12 oz orange or apple juice | 165-180 |
| Sports drink, 20 oz | 130-150 |

### The weekly drink budget

Pick a number. Most buyers do well at 500-700 liquid calories per week as a hard cap. That is roughly four light beers, or three glasses of wine plus a Saturday latte, or two cocktails. Track it on the spreadsheet (see spreadsheet spec). Once you can see the number, you stop guessing.

If you do not drink alcohol, your liquid calorie budget can go to better coffee or a daily smoothie without breaking the math.

## How snacks and drinks fit the day

Snacks fit between meals when there are more than five hours between them, or when training raises protein need. Drinks fit anywhere your weekly cap allows. Neither is obligatory. Some days you eat three meals and drink water and the math still works.

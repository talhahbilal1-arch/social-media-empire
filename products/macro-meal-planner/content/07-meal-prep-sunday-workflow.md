# Meal Prep: The Sunday Workflow

Meal prep is not about spending your entire Sunday in the kitchen. It is about investing 2 hours on Sunday so that Monday through Friday, hitting your macros requires reheating, not cooking.

## The Saturday Setup (15 Minutes)

Do this the day before prep day:

1. Finalize your template rotation for the week (Chapter 6)
2. Build your grocery list
3. Shop (30 to 45 minutes in-store, or place an online pickup order)

When you get home, put proteins in the fridge to thaw if frozen. That is it for Saturday.

## The Sunday Workflow (2 Hours)

### Hour 1: Proteins and Grains (Simultaneous)

**Start the oven at 400F.** While it preheats:

- Season 2 to 3 pounds of chicken breast or thighs with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika
- Place on a sheet pan lined with parchment

**Start the rice cooker or stovetop pot.** 3 to 4 cups of dry rice covers most of the week.

**Put the chicken in the oven.** Set a timer for 25 minutes (breasts) or 35 minutes (thighs).

While both are cooking:

- Wash and chop vegetables: bell peppers, broccoli, onions, spinach
- Store chopped vegetables in separate containers (do not mix, as they have different shelf lives)
- Portion out Greek yogurt into individual containers if buying a large tub
- Hard-boil 6 to 8 eggs (12 minutes in boiling water, then ice bath)

**When chicken is done:** let it rest 5 minutes, then slice or shred. Divide into 4 to 5 portions in containers.

**When rice is done:** fluff and divide into 4 to 5 portions.

### Hour 2: Secondary Proteins and Assembly

- Brown 1.5 pounds of ground beef or turkey with onions and seasoning (15 minutes)
- While that cooks, assemble 3 to 4 "grab and go" lunch containers:
  - Base: rice or greens
  - Protein: chicken or ground meat
  - Vegetables: whatever you chopped
  - Fat: avocado (add day-of, not during prep, to prevent browning) or a drizzle of olive oil
- Portion any remaining protein into freezer-safe bags for the second half of the week

### Final 10 Minutes

- Label containers with the day and meal (masking tape and a marker)
- Stack in the fridge: meals for Monday through Wednesday in front, Thursday and Friday in back
- Put any extra portions in the freezer
- Clean the kitchen

## Container Strategy

You need 10 to 12 meal prep containers. Glass with snap lids holds up better than plastic and does not stain or retain smells. Budget option: buy one 10-pack of glass containers (roughly $25 on Amazon). They last years.

**Container sizing guide:**

| Meal | Container Size |
|------|---------------|
| Lunch / Dinner | 28 to 36 oz (3 compartments preferred) |
| Breakfast (oats, scramble) | 16 to 20 oz |
| Snack (yogurt, nuts) | 8 to 12 oz |

## Shelf Life Rules

| Food | Refrigerator Life |
|------|------------------|
| Cooked chicken | 4 days |
| Cooked ground beef | 4 days |
| Cooked rice | 5 days |
| Hard-boiled eggs (peeled) | 5 days |
| Chopped raw vegetables | 4 to 5 days |
| Assembled containers | 3 to 4 days (then reheat from frozen) |

If you prep on Sunday, eat refrigerated meals through Wednesday. Thursday and Friday meals should come from the freezer or be assembled fresh from leftover ingredients.

## The "I Hate Meal Prep" Shortcut

If you genuinely will not do a 2-hour Sunday session, do this instead:

- Cook one protein in bulk (the sheet-pan chicken takes 5 minutes of active work)
- Make a pot of rice
- Buy pre-cut vegetable trays and bagged salad
- Assemble meals in 3 minutes each morning by combining protein + carb + veg in a container

This takes 30 minutes total across the week and still beats the alternative of ordering takeout at noon because you have nothing packed.

# Eating Out: The Survival Guide

Eating out does not have to derail your macros. It does require a strategy. The goal is not perfection. The goal is staying within 200 to 300 calories of your target and hitting your protein number, even when someone else is cooking.

## The Universal Rules

These apply regardless of the restaurant type:

1. **Check the menu before you go.** Most restaurants post menus online. Spend 2 minutes finding the highest-protein option and commit to it before you arrive. Decision-making at the table under social pressure is where plans die.
2. **Eat protein first.** If the plate has chicken, rice, and vegetables, eat the chicken first. If you get full, you have at least hit your protein.
3. **Ask for sauce on the side.** Sauces and dressings are where hidden calories live. A grilled chicken salad is 400 calories. That same salad with ranch dressing becomes 700.
4. **Skip the bread basket.** Not because bread is evil, but because 3 pieces of bread with butter is 400 calories of carbs and fat that displaces the protein you actually need.
5. **Drink water.** A soda is 150 calories. A margarita is 300. Two beers are 300 to 400. These add up faster than entrees.

## Restaurant-Specific Strategies

### Italian

- Order: grilled chicken or fish with a side of vegetables and a small portion of pasta
- Avoid: cream-based sauces (Alfredo, vodka), breaded and fried options (chicken parm), unlimited breadsticks
- Macro hack: ask for the protein double-portioned and the pasta half-portioned (most restaurants will accommodate)
- Estimate: grilled chicken over marinara with vegetables, roughly 500 to 650 calories

### Mexican

- Order: fajitas (chicken, steak, or shrimp) with corn tortillas, salsa, and guacamole (measured)
- Avoid: chimichangas (deep-fried), cheese-heavy quesadillas, sour cream and cheese loaded on top
- Macro hack: fajitas come deconstructed, so you control the assembly. Load protein, skip the rice if you need to cut carbs, use 2 tortillas instead of 4
- Estimate: chicken fajitas with 2 corn tortillas, roughly 450 to 600 calories

### American Grill / Steakhouse

- Order: a lean steak (sirloin, filet) or grilled chicken breast with a baked potato or steamed vegetables
- Avoid: loaded baked potatoes (butter, sour cream, bacon, cheese add 400+ calories), onion rings, loaded nachos as appetizers
- Macro hack: ask for the steak cooked without extra butter (many restaurants finish steaks with a tablespoon of butter on top, adding 100 calories)
- Estimate: 8 oz sirloin with baked potato (plain) and steamed broccoli, roughly 550 to 700 calories

### Asian (Chinese, Thai, Japanese)

- Order: stir-fried chicken or shrimp with vegetables, steamed rice on the side. Sushi with sashimi-heavy rolls.
- Avoid: deep-fried options (General Tso, tempura, sweet and sour), coconut-milk curries (high-fat), fried rice
- Macro hack: ask for "light oil" or "steamed with sauce on the side." Most stir-fry calories come from the cooking oil, not the food.
- Estimate: chicken stir-fry with steamed rice, roughly 500 to 700 calories

### Burger Restaurants

- Order: a single-patty burger with lettuce, tomato, onion, mustard. Sweet potato fries over regular if available.
- Avoid: double or triple patties with bacon and cheese, milkshakes (800 to 1,200 calories each)
- Macro hack: skip the bun if you need to cut carbs (lettuce wrap), or eat it open-faced (one bun half)
- Estimate: single burger with bun and side salad, roughly 500 to 650 calories

### Pizza

- Order: 2 slices of thin-crust pizza with a protein topping (chicken, sausage) and a side salad
- Avoid: deep-dish (double the calories per slice), extra cheese, stuffed crust, garlic knots
- Macro hack: 2 slices is the limit. Eat the salad first to take the edge off hunger. If you want more, order a grilled chicken appetizer.
- Estimate: 2 slices thin-crust with chicken, roughly 500 to 600 calories

### Fast-Casual (Chipotle, Sweetgreen, etc.)

- Order: a bowl (skip the tortilla) with double protein, fajita vegetables, salsa, and half-portion of rice
- Avoid: sour cream, cheese, queso, chips on the side, large tortilla wraps
- Macro hack: double protein at Chipotle costs $3 to $4 extra and adds 30 to 40 grams of protein. This is the best macro-per-dollar deal in fast food.
- Estimate: chicken bowl with rice, beans, salsa, and lettuce, roughly 550 to 700 calories

### Breakfast / Brunch

- Order: an omelet with vegetables and a side of fruit, or eggs with turkey sausage and toast
- Avoid: pancake stacks (600 to 900 calories with syrup), Belgian waffles, French toast, biscuits and gravy
- Macro hack: ask for egg whites if you need to cut fat, or keep the yolks and skip the toast
- Estimate: 3-egg omelet with vegetables and toast, roughly 400 to 550 calories

## The "Social Pressure" Protocol

When someone says "just live a little" or "one night won't hurt," they are right in isolation. One meal does not ruin a program. But if "one meal" happens 3 times a week, it is no longer one meal. It is a pattern.

The response is simple: order what fits your targets, eat it, enjoy the company, and do not explain or justify your food choices to the table. Nobody needs a lecture on macros, and you do not need permission to eat a salad.

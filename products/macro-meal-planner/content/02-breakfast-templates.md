# Breakfast Templates

Four templates. Pick three for your rotation. Each one hits 30-50g of protein at the standard portion and scales cleanly to 40g, 60g, or 80g hits if you front-load protein at breakfast.

All macros below are calculated at the standard portion. Calorie totals are rounded to the nearest 5.

## Template B1: Protein Oats

**Structure:** rolled oats + whey or casein + fruit + nut butter or seeds.

**Standard portion (one serving):**

- 1/2 cup dry rolled oats
- 1 scoop whey (about 30g powder)
- 1 medium banana or 1 cup berries
- 1 tablespoon almond or peanut butter

**Macros at standard portion:** 38g protein, 60g carbs, 12g fat. About 510 calories.

**Scale to 40g protein:** standard already lands here. Done.

**Scale to 60g protein:** add 1 scoop whey to oats post-cook, plus 1/4 cup egg whites stirred in while warm. Adds 30g protein, 0g carbs, 0g fat. New total: 68g protein.

**Scale to 80g protein:** standard plus 1 scoop whey, 1/2 cup egg whites, and switch banana to 1 cup berries to free calories for the protein. New total: 78g protein.

**Notes:** Cook oats in water, not milk, unless you want the extra carbs and fat. Stir whey in off the heat or it goes grainy.

## Template B2: Egg Scramble

**Structure:** whole eggs + extra whites + vegetables + starchy carb on the side.

**Standard portion (one serving):**

- 3 whole eggs
- 1/2 cup egg whites
- 1 cup mixed vegetables (peppers, onion, spinach, mushroom)
- 1 slice sourdough or 1 small potato

**Macros at standard portion:** 36g protein, 25g carbs, 18g fat. About 420 calories.

**Scale to 40g protein:** standard plus one extra egg white. Lands at 40g.

**Scale to 60g protein:** add 3 oz cooked turkey sausage or shredded chicken to the scramble. Adds 21g protein. New total: 57g protein, round to 60.

**Scale to 80g protein:** standard plus 4 oz turkey sausage and 1/2 cup additional egg whites. New total: 78g protein.

**Notes:** Cook vegetables first, then add eggs. Skip the cheese unless you account for it (1 oz adds 7g protein, 9g fat).

## Template B3: Greek Yogurt Bowl

**Structure:** plain Greek yogurt (0% or 2%) + berries + granola + nuts or seeds.

**Standard portion (one serving):**

- 1 cup plain 0% Greek yogurt
- 1 cup mixed berries
- 1/4 cup low-sugar granola
- 1 tablespoon chopped almonds or chia seeds

**Macros at standard portion:** 28g protein, 38g carbs, 9g fat. About 340 calories.

**Scale to 40g protein:** add 1 scoop whey or collagen stirred into the yogurt. Adds 25g protein. Or use 1.5 cups yogurt instead of 1.

**Scale to 60g protein:** 1.5 cups yogurt plus 1 scoop whey stirred in. New total: 62g protein.

**Scale to 80g protein:** 2 cups yogurt, 1 scoop whey, drop granola to 2 tablespoons to free calories. New total: 80g protein.

**Notes:** Read the label. Fage 0%, Chobani Plain, and Two Good are the cleanest options for protein per gram. Avoid flavored yogurts unless you log the added sugar.

## Template B4: Breakfast Sandwich

**Structure:** eggs + lean breakfast meat + cheese + bread, wrap, or English muffin.

**Standard portion (one serving):**

- 2 whole eggs
- 2 oz Canadian bacon or 3 slices turkey bacon
- 1 slice cheese (1 oz)
- 1 English muffin or 1 medium tortilla

**Macros at standard portion:** 32g protein, 30g carbs, 18g fat. About 410 calories.

**Scale to 40g protein:** add 1/2 cup egg whites to the sandwich. Adds 13g protein. New total: 45g.

**Scale to 60g protein:** standard plus 1/2 cup egg whites and 2 extra oz of Canadian bacon. New total: 60g protein.

**Scale to 80g protein:** double the eggs and meat (4 whole eggs, 4 oz Canadian bacon), keep cheese and bread the same. New total: 78g protein.

**Notes:** Canadian bacon is the lowest-fat breakfast meat per gram of protein. Streaky bacon doubles the fat at the same protein hit. Wraps are usually higher in calories than English muffins despite looking lighter; check the label.

## How to pick your three

Pick the templates that use foods you already buy. If you do not have whey in the house, B1 and B3 are off the table for week one. If you do not eat eggs daily, B2 and B4 are off. The right rotation is the one you can run without a single extra trip to the store.

# The 90-Day Macro Meal Planner

## Positioning

- **Name:** The 90-Day Macro Meal Planner
- **Price:** $17 (order bump on the $97 Blueprint), $47 standalone
- **Tagline:** Stop guessing what to cook. Hit your macros for 90 days without rewriting your life.
- **Hero promise:** A template-based eating system that hits your macros without rigid recipes, calorie math at every meal, or eating the same thing every day.
- **Format:** PDF planner (40-60 pages) + fillable spreadsheet (Google Sheets and Excel)
- **Delivery:** Gumroad digital download
- **Brand:** fitover35.com

## Target Buyer

The Blueprint buyer who already knows his macro target but freezes the moment he stares at the fridge. He has the calculator. He does not have the structure to act on it three meals a day, seven days a week, for ninety days.

Secondary: anyone who has bounced off three rigid meal plans because life does not run on a 1.5 oz portion of brown rice at 12:30 PM Tuesday.

## Pain Points Solved

- Decision fatigue at meal time, especially on weekdays
- Rigid meal plans that collapse the first time a meeting moves
- Macro math at every single meal
- Endless cooking variety that never settles into a workflow
- Grocery overspend from buying ingredients that do not get used
- Restaurant meals torpedoing a clean week because there was no plan

## What Is Inside

1. The template system explained: 4 breakfast, 6 lunch, 6 dinner, 4 snack templates
2. Macro profile per template at a standard portion plus scale-up rules for 40g, 60g, and 80g protein hits
3. Weekly grocery list generator keyed to template rotation and calorie tier
4. Sunday meal prep workflow (2 hours, end to end)
5. Restaurant survival guide for 8 common cuisines
6. Liquid calorie tracker and snack templates
7. Higher-calorie day rules (the so-called cheat meal, reframed)
8. Fillable spreadsheet: weekly planner, grocery generator, macro tracker

## Why $17 (Order Bump)

- $17 sits inside the impulse-buy zone where most buyers do not stop to think
- The Blueprint already cost $97; the planner is 17% of that, framed as a no-brainer add-on
- Take-rate target: 30-40% of Blueprint buyers. At 100 Blueprint sales per month, that is $510-680 per month of pure margin
- Standalone $47 anchors the perceived value. The order-bump price reads as a discount, not the floor
- No additional fulfillment cost. The PDF and spreadsheet are built once and shipped forever
- Gumroad fee on $17 is roughly $2; net per unit is ~$15

## Why It Belongs Next To The Blueprint

The Blueprint tells the buyer his macro target. The planner tells him exactly how to hit that target every day for 90 days without thinking about it. They are complementary, not redundant: the Blueprint is the program, the planner is the execution layer for the food side. Selling them together raises the average order value without raising friction.

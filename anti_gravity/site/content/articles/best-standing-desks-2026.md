## Standing Desk Buying Guide

### 1. Determine Your Weight Capacity Needs

Add up everything that will sit on your desk:
- Monitor: 5-15 lbs each
- Laptop: 3-5 lbs
- Keyboard + mouse: 2-3 lbs
- Desk lamp: 1-3 lbs
- Speakers, docking stations, etc.: 10-20 lbs

**Total:** Most setups fall between 30-80 lbs. Even 200 lbs capacity is plenty. Only go above that if you have multiple large monitors or other heavy equipment.

### 2. Height Range Matters

The average adult woman needs 22"-28" when sitting and 40"-46" when standing. Men typically need 24"-30" sitting and 42"-48" standing.

**Pro Tip:** If you're under 5'6" or over 6'2", prioritize desks with extended height ranges. The Flexispot E7 (22.4"-48.8") is best for shorter and taller users.

### 3. Motor Type: Dual vs. Single

- **Dual motor:** More stability, especially at full height. Less wobble. Better for heavier loads.
- **Single motor:** Adequate for lightweight setups. Can wobble if loaded unevenly.

**Our recommendation:** Go dual motor if you can afford it ($350+). If you're on a strict budget and have a light setup, a quality single motor works fine.

### 4. Stability Matters

Look for:
- Anti-collision detection (prevents damage if something's in the way)
- Smooth, quiet operation (sign of good engineering)
- Sturdy base frame (usually X-shaped or C-shaped)

### 5. Cable Management

Motorized desks need cable management systems to prevent wires from getting pinched during height adjustments. All the desks on this list include this feature.

### 6. Warranty and Support

- Budget desks ($250-300): 1-3 year warranty
- Mid-range ($350-400): 3-5 year warranty
- Premium ($400+): 5-10 year warranty

UPLIFT's 10-year warranty is exceptional and reflects their confidence in the product.

## How to Set Up Your Standing Desk for Ergonomics

Just having a standing desk isn't enough. Proper setup is critical:

### Monitor Height
- Top of monitor should be at or slightly below eye level when standing
- Arm's length away (20-26 inches)
- This prevents neck strain

### Keyboard and Mouse
- Elbows at 90° angle when typing
- Wrists neutral, not bent up or down
- Use a keyboard tray if needed (adds $30-50)

### Standing Duration
- Start with 20-30 minutes of standing per 2 hours of sitting
- Gradually increase as your body adapts
- Alternate between sitting and standing throughout the day

### Footwear
- Wear supportive shoes (not flip-flops)
- Consider an anti-fatigue mat ($30-80) for additional comfort

## FAQ

**Q: Will a standing desk really improve my health?**
A: Studies show standing desks reduce sedentary behavior and can improve circulation and posture. However, standing all day is just as bad as sitting all day. The key is alternating between sitting and standing.

**Q: How long should I stand each day?**
A: Start with 20-30 minutes per 2-hour block. Most experts recommend a 50/50 sit-stand ratio, but start gradual and let your body adapt. Aim for 2-4 hours of standing per 8-hour workday.

**Q: Are electric desks quieter than manual hand-crank desks?**
A: Generally yes, though quality varies. All the desks listed here operate at under 50-60dB (conversational volume). You might notice the motor, but it's not disruptive.

**Q: Do I need a special monitor arm for a standing desk?**
A: No, standard monitor arms work fine. However, a monitor arm ($40-150) is highly recommended for proper ergonomics as your desk height changes.

**Q: What about anti-fatigue mats?**
A: They're not essential but help. A quality mat ($40-80) cushions your feet and reduces fatigue when standing for extended periods. Helpful, not mandatory.

**Q: Can I use a standing desk with my current desktop?**
A: Yes, the desks listed here work with any standard rectangular desktop. Budget $50-150 for a quality desktop (72"x30" is standard). Some packages include the desktop; others don't.

**Q: How difficult is assembly?**
A: Most motorized desks require 1-2 hours with a second person, or 2-3 hours solo. The UPLIFT is more complex; the Fezibo is simpler. None require special tools beyond a screwdriver.

**Q: What's the lifespan of a standing desk motor?**
A: Quality motors (like UPLIFT and Branch) last 5-10+ years with normal use. Budget motors may last 3-5 years. All can be repaired or replaced under warranty.

# Pinterest Hook Frameworks — 45 Proven Formulas

These are the exact hook frameworks used in the live system. Each one is a template —
fill in the brackets with your niche-specific content. The system rotates through these
to prevent feed fatigue and test which formats perform best.

**How to use:** Pick a framework, fill in the brackets, aim for 40-60 characters total.
The curiosity gap is the key — the reader MUST click to get the answer.

---

## CATEGORY A: CURIOSITY GAP (Stop-the-Scroll)

The reader sees the title and HAS to know the answer. Never give it away.

```
1. "Stop [Common Exercise/Habit] After [Age] — Do This Instead"
   Example: "Stop Crunches After 35 — Do This Instead"

2. "I Tried [Specific Thing] for 30 Days — Here's What Happened"
   Example: "I Tried Zone 2 Cardio for 30 Days — Here's What Happened"

3. "The [Adjective] [Food/Exercise/Habit] That [Surprising Result]"
   Example: "The Cheap Supplement That Doubled My Recovery"

4. "Why [Popular Thing] Is Secretly [Bad Outcome] After [Age]"
   Example: "Why HIIT Is Secretly Aging You After 40"

5. "The [Thing] I Refused to Try (Until Now)"
   Example: "The Supplement I Refused to Try (Until Now)"

6. "[Thing] vs [Other Thing] — The Winner Isn't What You Think"
   Example: "Cardio vs Weights — The Winner Isn't What You Think"

7. "I Quit [Common Habit] for [Timeframe] and My [Metric] [Result]"
   Example: "I Quit Alcohol for 60 Days and My Testosterone Did This"

8. "What Nobody Tells You About [Topic] After [Age]"
   Example: "What Nobody Tells You About Cutting After 35"

9. "The [Time of Day] Mistake Killing Your [Goal]"
   Example: "The Morning Mistake Killing Your Fat Loss"

10. "[Thing] Every [Audience] Gets Wrong (Are You Doing This?)"
    Example: "The Thing Every Man Over 35 Gets Wrong About Protein"
```

## CATEGORY B: LISTICLE (Drives Maximum Saves)

Lists are saved more than any other format on Pinterest. Always tease the full list.

```
11. "5 [Things] That [Result] (Most People Miss #[Number])"
    Example: "5 Supplements That Actually Work (Most People Miss #3)"

12. "5 Signs Your [Thing] Needs [Solution]"
    Example: "5 Signs Your Testosterone Needs Attention"

13. "[Number] [Topic] Mistakes You're Making After [Age]"
    Example: "7 Workout Mistakes You're Making After 35"

14. "[Number] Things That Changed After I [Did Something]"
    Example: "5 Things That Changed After I Fixed My Sleep"

15. "The [Number] [Topic] Habits of Men Who [Result]"
    Example: "The 4 Training Habits of Men Who Stay Lean After 40"

16. "[Number] Ways to [Goal] Without [Common Sacrifice]"
    Example: "5 Ways to Lose Belly Fat Without Counting Calories"

17. "[Number] [Topic] Tricks That Take Under [Time]"
    Example: "5 Recovery Tricks That Take Under 5 Minutes"

18. "[Number] [Items] Worth Every Penny for [Audience]"
    Example: "5 Supplements Worth Every Penny for Men Over 35"

19. "[Number] Reasons [Common Belief] Is Actually Wrong"
    Example: "3 Reasons More Cardio Is Actually Making You Fatter"

20. "Do These [Number] Things Every Day for [Result]"
    Example: "Do These 4 Things Every Day for Better Testosterone"
```

## CATEGORY C: AUTHORITY / PROOF (Builds Trust, Gets Saves)

Claims backed by data or experience. Specific numbers outperform vague claims.

```
21. "The Proven [Timeframe] Protocol That [Specific Result]"
    Example: "The Proven 8-Week Protocol That Resets Your Metabolism"

22. "I Tracked [Metric] for [Timeframe] — The Results Were Shocking"
    Example: "I Tracked My HRV for 90 Days — The Results Were Shocking"

23. "My Doctor Said This About [Topic] — I Wasn't Ready"
    Example: "My Doctor Said This About My Testosterone — I Wasn't Ready"

24. "What [Number]% of [Audience] Get Wrong About [Topic]"
    Example: "What 73% of Men Over 35 Get Wrong About Protein"

25. "Scientists Say [Surprising Claim] (Here's What It Means)"
    Example: "Scientists Say THIS Matters More Than Your Workout"

26. "[Big Name/Source] Recommends [Surprising Thing] — Here's Why"
    Example: "Research Recommends THIS Recovery Tool Over Ice Baths"

27. "I Lost [Number][Unit] in [Timeframe] Without [Sacrifice]"
    Example: "Lost 22lbs in 90 Days Without Giving Up Alcohol"

28. "The [Specific Number]-Minute Routine That [Significant Result]"
    Example: "The 10-Minute Morning Routine That Fixed My Back Pain"
```

## CATEGORY D: CONTRAST / REFRAME (Pattern Interrupts)

Challenges what the reader believes. Works best with confident, specific claims.

```
29. "Stop [Common Advice] — Do This Instead (It Works)"
    Example: "Stop Eating Less — Do This Instead (It Works)"

30. "The [Topic] Myth That's Been Costing You [Loss]"
    Example: "The Protein Myth That's Been Costing You Muscle"

31. "You've Been [Doing Something] Wrong — Here's the Fix"
    Example: "You've Been Deadlifting Wrong — Here's the Fix"

32. "Most [Audience] Do [Common Thing]. High Performers Do [Different Thing]."
    Example: "Most Men Count Calories. The Lean Ones Do This Instead."

33. "The [Topic] Advice That Sounds Right But Destroys [Goal]"
    Example: "The Diet Advice That Sounds Right But Destroys Muscle"

34. "Forget [Popular Approach] — This Is What Actually Works"
    Example: "Forget HIIT — This Is What Actually Burns Belly Fat"

35. "The [Opposite of Expected] Thing About [Topic]"
    Example: "The Counterintuitive Thing About Building Muscle After 40"
```

## CATEGORY E: PERSONAL STORY (Highest Relatability)

First-person makes the pin feel like a recommendation from a friend, not an ad.

```
36. "This One Change Fixed My [Problem] in [Timeframe]"
    Example: "This One Change Fixed My Back Pain in 2 Weeks"

37. "I Used to [Bad Habit/Approach] Until I Learned This"
    Example: "I Used to Crash Diet Until I Learned This"

38. "After [Timeframe] of [Struggle], I Finally Found What Works"
    Example: "After 5 Years of Bad Workouts, I Finally Found What Works"

39. "The Cheap [Product] That Replaced My Expensive [Thing]"
    Example: "The $8 Supplement That Replaced My $80 Pre-Workout"

40. "I Was Skeptical About [Topic] Until [Event That Changed Mind]"
    Example: "I Was Skeptical About Cold Showers Until My Blood Test"
```

## Hook Performance Notes (From Live Data)

**Highest performing patterns** in the fitness/men over 35 niche:
- Listicles with "After 35" or age-specific framing outperform generic
- "Most people" + contrast framing drives higher saves
- Personal story hooks get more comments and shares
- Curiosity gap titles with numbered items (5 Things...) get the most clicks

**Common mistakes that kill performance:**
- Giving away the answer in the title ("5 Reasons Why Deadlifts Are Great")
- Vague claims ("Improve Your Fitness")
- Starting with "How to" (performs poorly on Pinterest vs. "The secret to...")
- Using the brand name in the title (wastes character space)

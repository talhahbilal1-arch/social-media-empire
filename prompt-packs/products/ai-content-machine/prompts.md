# 📝 AI Content Machine
## 30 Prompts to Create a Month of Content in One Sitting

> **How to use this pack:** Each prompt is designed to produce publish-ready content. Replace [bracketed text] with your details. Chain prompts together for maximum output — e.g., use Prompt 1 to plan, then Prompt 5 to write, then Prompt 12 to repurpose.

## SECTION 1: CONTENT STRATEGY & PLANNING (6 Prompts)

### Prompt 1: 30-Day Content Calendar Generator
```
You are a content strategist who has grown accounts from 0 to 100K followers. Create a 30-day content calendar for me.

**My niche:** [e.g., "personal finance for millennials"]
**Platforms:** [e.g., "Instagram, LinkedIn, blog"]
**Content pillars (3-5 topics I want to be known for):** [list them]
**Posting frequency per platform:** [e.g., "Instagram 5x/week, LinkedIn 3x/week, blog 1x/week"]
**My goal:** [followers / email subscribers / sales / authority]

For each day, provide:
1. Platform
2. Content type (carousel, text post, reel script, blog outline)
3. Topic/headline
4. Content pillar it belongs to
5. CTA (what action do I want after they read/watch?)

Include a mix: 40% educational, 25% personal/story, 20% promotional, 15% engagement/questions.

Format as a table with columns: Day | Platform | Type | Topic | Pillar | CTA
```

### Prompt 2: Audience Research & Persona Builder
```
You are a market research expert. Help me deeply understand my target audience.

**My niche:** [topic/industry]
**My product or service:** [what I sell or will sell]
**Who I think my audience is:** [basic demographics]

Build a detailed audience persona including:
1. Demographics (age, income, location, job title)
2. Psychographics (values, fears, aspirations, frustrations)
3. The 10 questions they're Googling right now about [my niche]
4. Where they hang out online (specific subreddits, Facebook groups, hashtags, podcasts)
5. What they've tried before that didn't work
6. The language they use to describe their problems (exact phrases)
7. Their buying triggers (what makes them pull out their credit card)
8. Their objections to buying (what stops them)

Use this to give me 20 content ideas that directly address their pain points.
```

### Prompt 3: Competitor Content Analysis
```
Analyze my top 3 competitors' content strategy and find my unfair advantage.

**My niche:** [topic]
**Competitor 1:** [name/handle]  
**Competitor 2:** [name/handle]  
**Competitor 3:** [name/handle]  
**What I know about their content:** [brief observations]
**My unique angle/experience:** [what makes me different]

Provide:
1. What content types are working best for each competitor
2. Topics they cover well (that I should also cover, but better)
3. GAPS — topics they miss that I could own
4. Their posting frequency and engagement patterns
5. Their content weaknesses I can exploit
6. My "blue ocean" — 5 content angles that none of them are doing
7. A strategy to differentiate within 30 days
```

### Prompt 4: Content Pillar Architect
```
Help me build 5 content pillars that position me as THE authority in my niche.

**My niche:** [topic]
**My expertise:** [what I know deeply]
**My experience:** [years, credentials, unique background]
**My hot take (controversial opinion in my space):** [share it]

For each of the 5 pillars:
1. Pillar name (catchy, memorable)
2. Why this pillar matters to my audience
3. 10 subtopics within this pillar
4. The "signature framework" I can create around this pillar
5. Content format that works best for this pillar
6. How this pillar drives revenue (not just engagement)
```

### Prompt 5: One Idea → 10 Pieces of Content
```
Take this single content idea and turn it into 10 pieces of content across different formats.

**The idea:** [your content idea or insight]
**My platforms:** [list where you post]
**My voice:** [casual / professional / edgy / educational]

Create:
1. A Twitter/X thread (8-10 tweets)
2. A LinkedIn post (text, 1300 characters max)
3. An Instagram carousel outline (8-10 slides with header + body text)
4. A blog post outline (H2 headers, key points)
5. An email newsletter version
6. A short-form video script (60 seconds for Reels/TikTok/Shorts)
7. A quote graphic (one punchy line from the idea)
8. A poll or question post for engagement
9. A "myth vs reality" post
10. A personal story version of the idea

Each piece should feel NATIVE to its platform, not copy-pasted.
```

### Prompt 6: Trending Topic Hijacker
```
You are a trend-savvy content creator. Help me ride trending topics without being cringy.

**My niche:** [topic]
**Current trending topic/event:** [what's trending right now]
**My expertise angle:** [how this relates to what I know]
**My brand voice:** [tone]

```

## SECTION 2: WRITING PROMPTS BY FORMAT (8 Prompts)

### Prompt 7: Long-Form Blog Post Writer
```
Write a comprehensive, SEO-optimized blog post.

**Topic:** [your topic]
**Target keyword:** [primary keyword]
**Secondary keywords:** [2-3 related keywords]
**Word count target:** [1500-3000]
**Audience knowledge level:** [beginner / intermediate / advanced]
**Goal of this post:** [rank on Google / drive email signups / sell a product]

Requirements:
1. Compelling headline (write 5 options, mark the best)
2. Hook opening (first 2 sentences must make them keep reading)
3. Clear H2/H3 structure with keyword-optimized headers
4. Actionable advice (not just theory)
5. Real examples or case studies
6. Internal linking opportunities (list 3 related topics I should write about)
7. Meta description (155 characters)
8. CTA that matches the post's goal
9. FAQ section (3-5 questions for featured snippet opportunities)

Write in a conversational, authoritative tone. No fluff. Every paragraph must earn its place.
```

### Prompt 8: Email Newsletter Writer
```
Write an engaging email newsletter issue.

**Newsletter name:** [name]
**This issue's topic:** [main topic]
**My audience:** [who reads this]
**Promotion (if any):** [product/service to mention, or "none"]
**Personal update to weave in:** [something happening in your life/business]

Structure:
1. Subject line (write 5 options — optimize for open rate, not cleverness)
2. Preview text (the line that shows after the subject)
3. Opening hook (personal story or bold statement — NOT "Happy Tuesday!")
4. Main content (the valuable insight, tip, or framework)
5. Segue to promotion (if applicable — must feel natural, not forced)
6. "One thing to try this week" action item
7. PS line (the most-read section of any email — use it wisely)

Tone: like a smart friend writing you an email, not a corporate newsletter.
```

### Prompt 9: Instagram Carousel Creator
```
Design an Instagram carousel that gets saved and shared.

**Topic:** [the lesson/insight to teach]
**Target audience:** [who is this for]
**CTA:** [what do I want them to do after reading]
**My brand colors (optional):** [colors for design reference]

Create a 10-slide carousel:
- Slide 1: Hook headline (pattern interrupt — make them stop scrolling)
- Slides 2-8: Content slides (one key point per slide, scannable text)
- Slide 9: Summary/recap slide
- Slide 10: CTA slide

For each slide provide:
1. Headline text (bold, large)
2. Body text (2-3 short sentences max)
3. Design note (icon/visual suggestion)

Also write the caption (include relevant hashtags and a save-worthy CTA).
```

### Prompt 10: Twitter/X Thread Writer
```
Write a viral-worthy Twitter/X thread.

**Topic:** [what the thread is about]
**My unique take:** [the angle that makes this different from what others have said]
**Length:** [8-15 tweets]
**Goal:** [followers / clicks / authority / engagement]

Rules:
1. Tweet 1 (the hook): must be a standalone banger. If this tweet isn't great, the thread dies.
2. Every tweet must make them want to read the next one
3. Use short sentences. One idea per tweet.
4. Include at least one "I can't believe this" moment
5. Tweet before last: the key insight or "so what?"
6. Last tweet: CTA + retweet request

Include a "pin tweet" version — the best single insight from the thread, standalone.
```

### Prompt 11: Short-Form Video Script (Reels/TikTok/Shorts)
```
Write a short-form video script that keeps viewers watching to the end.

**Topic:** [what the video teaches or discusses]
**Length:** [30 / 60 / 90 seconds]
**Style:** [talking head / voiceover with b-roll / text on screen]
**Hook type:** [controversial statement / "did you know?" / mistake people make / demonstration]
**CTA:** [follow / comment / click link in bio / save]

Script format:
- HOOK (first 3 seconds — the make-or-break moment)
- CONTEXT (why this matters — 5 seconds)
- CONTENT (the meat — 15-60 seconds depending on length)
- PAYOFF (the satisfying conclusion or revelation)
- CTA (what you want them to do — 5 seconds)

Include:
1. On-screen text suggestions
2. B-roll ideas (if voiceover style)
3. Where to pause for emphasis
4. The line that will make people comment
```

### Prompt 12: Content Repurposing Engine
```
Take this existing piece of content and repurpose it into 7 new formats.

**Original content:** [paste your blog post, video transcript, or long-form content]
**Platforms to repurpose for:** [list your platforms]

Transform it into:
1. 3 standalone social media posts (different angles from the same content)
2. An email newsletter issue
3. A carousel outline (8-10 slides)
4. A short-form video script (60 seconds)
5. 5 quote graphics (pull the most shareable lines)
6. A "thread" version (8 tweets)
7. A lead magnet outline (how to expand this into a downloadable guide)

Each must feel ORIGINAL, not recycled. Different hook, different angle, different format.
```

### Prompt 13: Headline & Hook Formula Generator
```
Generate 25 attention-grabbing headlines for my content.

**Topic:** [the subject]
**Audience:** [who reads this]
**Platform:** [where this will be published]
**Goal:** [clicks / opens / shares / saves]

Create 5 headlines in each style:
1. **Curiosity Gap:** "I did X and Y happened" (make them need to know)
2. **Listicle:** "X Ways to..." (specific, odd numbers perform best)
3. **Contrarian:** "Why X is actually wrong" (challenge conventional wisdom)
4. **How-To:** "How to X without Y" (promise + objection removal)
5. **Data-Driven:** "X% of people do Y wrong" (numbers = credibility)

Mark the top 3 that will perform best and explain why.
```

### Prompt 14: SEO Content Brief Generator
```
Create a comprehensive SEO content brief for my writer (or for me).

**Target keyword:** [primary keyword]
**Search intent:** [informational / commercial / transactional / navigational]
**My website/blog topic area:** [niche]
**Content format:** [blog post / landing page / guide / comparison]

Provide:
1. Title tag (60 characters, keyword-optimized)
2. Meta description (155 characters, includes CTA)
3. H2 headings (8-12, covering all subtopics someone searching this would want)
4. Key points to cover under each H2
5. Related keywords to include naturally (15-20)
6. Questions to answer (from "People Also Ask")
7. Internal linking suggestions (related topics to link to)
8. External linking suggestions (authoritative sources to cite)
9. Word count recommendation based on top-ranking content
10. Content angle: how to beat what's already ranking
```

**Product idea:** [describe it]

**Target price:** $[amount]
**My audience size:** [number across platforms]

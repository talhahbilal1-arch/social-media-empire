# 🚀 Digital Product Launch System
## 20 Prompts to Validate, Create, and Sell Your First Digital Product

> **How to use:** Follow prompts 1-5 in order to validate your idea. Then use 6-14 to build it. Use 15-20 to launch and sell. Replace all [brackets].

---

## SECTION 1: IDEA VALIDATION (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 1: Profitable Product Idea Generator
```
You are a digital product strategist who has helped creators generate over $1M in digital product revenue. I want to create a digital product that makes money.

**My skills/expertise:** [what you're good at]
**My audience (if any):** [who follows you, or "no audience yet"]
**Time available to create:** [hours per week]
**Preferred format:** [ebook / course / templates / prompts / toolkit / "not sure"]

Give me:
1. 10 digital product ideas ranked by: ease of creation × market demand × profit potential
2. For each: product name, format, price point, target buyer, one-line pitch
3. The #1 pick and why (with detailed reasoning)
4. The "weekend project" version I could ship in 48 hours to test demand
5. Red flags: which of these 10 I should NOT pursue and why
```

### Prompt 2: Market Demand Validator
```
Validate whether people will actually PAY for this product idea.

**Product idea:** [describe it]
**Target buyer:** [who would buy this]
**Price I'm considering:** $[amount]

Research and tell me:
1. Is this a "vitamin" (nice-to-have) or "painkiller" (must-have)? Why?
2. What similar products exist? (list 5 competitors with prices)
3. How is my product different or better?
4. Where are potential buyers currently discussing this problem? (specific forums, groups, hashtags)
5. 5 exact search terms people type when looking for this solution
6. Based on the evidence: should I build this? (YES with confidence / YES with caveats / PIVOT to X / NO)
7. If YES: the minimum viable version I should build first
```

### Prompt 3: Pre-Launch Interest Tester
```
Create a pre-launch campaign to test demand BEFORE I build the full product.

**Product:** [name and description]
**My audience/reach:** [email list size, social following, or "starting from zero"]
**Platforms I can use:** [social media, email, forums, etc.]

Build:
1. A "coming soon" landing page copy (headline, bullets, CTA for waitlist)
2. 5 social media posts to gauge interest (each tests a different angle)
3. A "beta testers wanted" post to find early adopters
4. A simple survey (5 questions) to validate the idea with real people
5. Success criteria: how many signups/responses = green light to build
6. A pre-sale strategy: how to get paid BEFORE the product exists
```

### Prompt 4: Pricing Strategy Designer
```
Help me price my digital product for maximum revenue (not just maximum sales).

**Product:** [what it is]
**What it helps buyers achieve:** [the outcome/transformation]
**Closest competitors and their prices:** [list what you know]
**My audience's willingness to pay:** [any data, or "unknown"]
**My brand positioning:** [budget-friendly / mid-range / premium]

Analyze and provide:
1. The psychology: why my planned price of $[X] might be wrong
2. The optimal price based on value delivered (not cost to create)
3. A 3-tier pricing structure (if applicable)
4. Launch pricing strategy: introductory price → regular price
5. When to offer discounts (and when discounts HURT sales)
6. How to increase perceived value without changing the product
7. The pricing page copy that justifies the price
```

### Prompt 5: Target Customer Deep Dive
```
Create a detailed buyer persona for my digital product.

**Product:** [what it is]
**What problem it solves:** [the pain point]
**Price:** $[amount]

Build a complete persona:
1. Demographics (age, role, income, location)
2. Their current situation (what's frustrating them)
3. What they've already tried (and why it didn't work)
4. Their dream outcome (what does success look like?)
5. Buying triggers (the moment they say "I need to buy this")
6. Objections (why they might NOT buy — and how to counter each)
7. Where they spend time online (specific places to reach them)
8. The exact language they use to describe their problem (for my copy)
```

## SECTION 2: PRODUCT CREATION (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 6: Product Outline & Structure
```
Create a detailed outline for my digital product.

**Product type:** [ebook / course / template pack / toolkit]
**Topic:** [subject]
**Target length/size:** [pages, modules, number of templates]
**Buyer's starting point:** [what they know before using this]
**Buyer's end state:** [what they can do after using this]

Build:
1. A product structure (modules/chapters/sections with titles)
2. For each section: key points to cover, learning outcomes, and exercises
3. The "quick win" — what section delivers value fastest (put this first)
4. Bonus materials to include (checklists, templates, resources)
5. The exact deliverable list (what files/formats they receive)
6. Quality checklist: what separates a $10 product from a $100 product
```

### Prompt 7: Product Content Writer (Section by Section)
```
Write Section [X] of my digital product.

**Product:** [name]
**This section's topic:** [what this chapter/module covers]
**Key points to cover:** [list 3-5 main ideas]
**Tone:** [conversational / professional / motivational]
**Format:** [written guide / slide deck / worksheet]

Write the complete content for this section:
1. Section introduction (why this matters — 2-3 sentences)
2. Main content with actionable steps (not just theory)
3. Real-world examples or case studies
4. Common mistakes to avoid
5. Exercise or action item for the reader
6. Transition to the next section

Make it genuinely useful. If someone only read THIS section, they should still get value.
```

### Prompt 8: Worksheet & Template Creator
```
Create bonus worksheets and templates for my digital product.

**Product topic:** [subject]
**Target audience:** [who uses this]
**Number of templates needed:** [X]

For each, create:
1. Template title and purpose
2. The actual template content (fill-in-the-blank, checklists, frameworks)
3. Instructions for how to use it
4. An example (one filled-in version so they see what "good" looks like)

Template ideas to include:
- Action plan / roadmap template
- Decision-making framework
- Tracking spreadsheet outline
- Scripts/copy templates
- Checklist for key processes
```

### Prompt 9: Product Polish & Quality Review
```
Review my digital product draft and find every weakness.

**Product type:** [format]
**Target price:** $[amount]
**Draft content (paste or summarize):** [paste key sections]

Evaluate:
1. Does the product deliver on its promise? (be brutally honest)
2. Would someone pay $[price] for this? Why or why not?
3. What's missing that a buyer would expect?
4. What sections are too long/short/unclear?
5. Is the formatting professional enough?
6. Grammar, consistency, and tone check
7. What would turn a 3-star review into a 5-star review?
8. The exact improvements needed, ranked by impact
```

### Prompt 10: Product Cover & Marketing Asset Brief
```
Create briefs for the visual assets I need for my product launch.

**Product name:** [name]
**Tagline:** [one-line description]
**Brand colors:** [if any]
**Competitor visual styles:** [what others in my niche look like]
**Platforms where this will be displayed:** [Gumroad, Etsy, my website, social media]

Create briefs for:
1. Product cover image (what it should show, text placement, mood)
2. Mockup description (how to display the product attractively)
3. Social media announcement graphic
4. Banner/header for the sales page
5. Thumbnail for marketplaces
6. Color palette and typography recommendations

Include image dimension requirements for each platform.
```

## SECTION 3: SALES & MARKETING (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 11: Sales Page Copy That Converts
```
Write a complete sales page for my digital product.

**Product:** [name]
**Price:** $[amount]
**Target buyer:** [who is this for]
**Main benefit:** [the #1 thing they get]
**Supporting benefits:** [3-5 additional benefits]
**What's included:** [list all deliverables]
**Guarantee:** [money-back guarantee period, if any]

Write the full page:
1. Headline (3 options — pick the strongest)
2. Subheadline
3. Opening: the pain point (make them feel understood)
4. The solution: introduce the product as the bridge
5. What's inside (detailed breakdown with bullet points)
6. Who this is for / who this is NOT for
7. Social proof section (placeholder for testimonials)
8. Pricing presentation (how to display the price persuasively)
9. FAQ section (5 common objections handled)
10. Final CTA with urgency element
11. PS line (address the last remaining objection)

Every line must move them closer to clicking "Buy." No filler.
```

### Prompt 12: Email Launch Sequence (7 Days)
```
Write a 7-day email launch sequence for my digital product.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Launch date:** [date]
**List size:** [number]
**Early-bird discount:** [amount or "none"]

Write all 7 emails:
Day 1: The story — why I created this
Day 2: The problem — paint the pain
Day 3: The solution — introduce the product
Day 4: Social proof — results and testimonials
Day 5: The deep dive — what's inside (detailed breakdown)
Day 6: Objection handling — FAQ + "who this is NOT for"
Day 7: Last chance — urgency + final CTA

For each email:
- Subject line (2 options per email)
- Preview text
- Full body copy
- CTA button text
- PS line
```

### Prompt 13: Gumroad Listing Optimizer
```
Write an optimized Gumroad product listing.

**Product:** [name]
**Price:** $[price]
**What it is:** [1-2 sentence description]
**What's included:** [list all files/deliverables]
**Who it's for:** [target buyer]

Create:
1. Product title (SEO-optimized, under 80 characters)
2. Short description (the first thing buyers see — must hook them)
3. Long description with:
   - Problem statement
   - What they'll learn or get
   - Full contents list
   - Who this is for
   - Guarantee/refund policy
4. Tags (10 relevant tags for Gumroad discovery)
5. Pricing strategy (base price + suggested prices for "pay what you want")
6. Thumbnail brief (what should be on the cover)
```

### Prompt 14: Social Media Launch Campaign
```
Create a complete social media campaign to launch my digital product.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Launch timeline:** [days until launch / launch day / post-launch]
**My platforms:** [where I'll post]
**My Following:** [size per platform]

Create 15 posts across the launch timeline:

PRE-LAUNCH (5 posts):
- Teaser post
- Behind the scenes
- Problem awareness post
- "Who wants early access?" post
- Countdown post

LAUNCH DAY (5 posts):
- Announcement post
- "What's inside" breakdown post
- Launch special / early-bird deal post
- Testimonial or result post
- "Last X hours at this price" post

POST-LAUNCH (5 posts):
- Late-bird offer
- Customer result highlight
- FAQ post (handling objections)
- "Why I created this" story post
- Evergreen "new here?" post

Include captions, hashtags, and optimal posting times for each.
```

## SECTION 4: POST-LAUNCH GROWTH (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 15: Review & Testimonial Collection System
```
Build a system to collect reviews and testimonials after purchase.

**Product:** [name]
**Where I sell it:** [Gumroad / Etsy / my website]
**Current number of buyers:** [if any]

Create:
1. A post-purchase email (sent 3 days after purchase) that gently asks for a review
2. A follow-up email (7 days later) for those who didn't review
3. Specific questions that guide buyers to write USEFUL reviews (not "it was good")
4. A template buyers can fill in if they're busy
5. How to turn the best testimonials into sales page assets
6. An incentive strategy (discounts on future products for reviews)
```

### Prompt 16: Upsell & Cross-Sell Strategy
```
Design upsells and cross-sells for my product ecosystem.

**Current product:** [name] at $[price]
**Buyer profile:** [who buys this]
**Other products I have (or plan to create):** [list]

Create:
1. An order bump idea (small add-on at checkout)
2. An upsell product idea (higher-priced complementary product)
3. A cross-sell recommendation post-purchase
4. A "bundle" offer combining multiple products
5. Email sequence to guide customers from first purchase to premium offer
6. A product roadmap: what to create next based on customer journey
```

### Prompt 17: Affiliate Program Designer
```
Design an affiliate program for my digital product.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Profit margin:** [percentage]
**Current marketing channels:** [how I currently sell]

Create:
1. Commission structure (percentage per sale)
2. Affiliate recruitment message (to send to potential affiliates)
3. Swipe copy for affiliates (3 email templates, 5 social posts)
4. A simple landing page for affiliate signups
5. Rules and guidelines (what affiliates can/can't do)
6. How to track and pay affiliates (tools and process)
```

### Prompt 18: Product V2 Planning
```
My product V1 is selling. Plan the V2 upgrade.

**Current product:** [name]
**Sales so far:** [number]
**Average review:** [rating]
**Common feedback:** [what buyers say — good and bad]
**Feature requests:** [what buyers are asking for]

Plan V2:
1. What to keep (what's working)
2. What to fix (common complaints)
3. What to add (most-requested features)
4. How to price V2 (charge more? free upgrade for V1 buyers?)
5. Launch strategy for V2 (re-launch as an event)
6. Communication to V1 buyers about the upgrade
```

### Prompt 19: Evergreen Sales Funnel
```
Build an evergreen sales funnel so my product sells daily without active launches.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Current traffic sources:** [where people find me]
**Email list size:** [number]
**Lead magnet (if any):** [what I offer for free]

Design:
1. The traffic → lead magnet → email sequence → sale funnel
2. A lead magnet that naturally leads to my paid product
3. A 5-email nurture sequence that educates then sells
4. Where to embed purchase links in my existing content
5. Retargeting: how to re-engage people who visited but didn't buy
6. Monthly revenue projection based on current traffic
```

### Prompt 20: Multi-Platform Distribution Strategy
```
Help me sell my digital product across multiple platforms to maximize reach.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Currently listed on:** [platforms]
**Product format:** [PDF / video / template / code]

For each platform, create an optimized listing:
1. **Gumroad** — listing copy, tags, pricing strategy
2. **Etsy** — SEO-optimized title, description, tags (13 tags max)
3. **PromptBase** (if prompts) — category, description, sample outputs
4. **My own website** — landing page copy
5. **CreativeMarket** (if applicable) — listing optimization
6. Platform-specific tips for maximizing discovery on each
7. Pricing strategy per platform (same price everywhere? or vary?)
```

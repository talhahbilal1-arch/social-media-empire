# Ready-to-Use Scripts — Online Coach Client Machine

**Copy these word-for-word, then edit with your voice.**
These handle the most common conversations coaches dread or avoid.

---

## SCRIPT 1: The DM → Discovery Call Sequence

**A prospect comments on your post or sends a cold DM. Here's the full flow.**

---

**Step 1: Reply to their comment or first DM**

If they said something like "this is so helpful!" or asked a question:

> "Thanks for the kind words! Can I ask — are you dealing with [THEIR PROBLEM] right now?"

Or if they said they're struggling with something specific:

> "Oh that resonates. [SHORT VALIDATION]. How long has that been going on for you?"

**Goal:** Get them into a back-and-forth. One question at a time.

---

**Step 2: After 2-3 exchanges, invite the call**

> "You know what, your situation sounds a lot like people I work with. I'd love to properly understand where you're at — would you be open to a quick 20-min chat? No pitch, just a conversation to see if there's anything I can help with."

If they say yes:
> "Great! Here's my calendar link: [LINK]. Pick whatever time works for you."

If they hesitate:
> "No pressure at all — I know that kind of thing isn't for everyone. What would be more useful for you right now?"

---

**Step 3: Confirmation text 24h before the call**

> "Hey [NAME], looking forward to chatting tomorrow at [TIME]. Quick heads up — I'll ask you a few honest questions about where you're at and what you're trying to achieve. The more real you can be, the more I can help. See you then!"

---

## SCRIPT 2: Discovery Call Opening (First 3 Minutes)

**The first 3 minutes set the tone for the whole call. Use this exactly.**

---

> "Hey [NAME], good to meet you. Thanks for making time — I know the calendar link thing can feel a bit odd when you've only talked to someone online.

> So here's how I usually run these: I'm going to ask you a few honest questions about where you're at right now and where you're trying to get to. I'll be straight with you about what I think, and at the end you can decide if it makes sense to talk about working together — or not. No pressure either way.

> Sound good? Cool. So tell me — what's going on with [YOUR NICHE TOPIC] right now? Like, what's the thing that made you want to have this conversation?"

**Then be quiet. Let them talk.**

---

## SCRIPT 3: Presenting the Price

**The moment most coaches fumble. Here's the exact language.**

---

**Transition into the price:**
> "Okay, so based on everything you've told me — [SUMMARIZE THEIR SITUATION IN THEIR OWN WORDS] — I think the right fit for you is [PROGRAM NAME].

> Here's what that looks like: [DESCRIBE PROGRAM IN 2-3 SENTENCES — what they get, how it works].

> The investment for that is [$X] per month for [X months / ongoing].

> [STOP TALKING. WAIT.]"

**The silence after the price is intentional. They need a moment to process.**
**Do NOT fill the silence with justifications or add-ons unprompted.**

---

**If they say "okay, let's do it":**
> "Great. I'll send you the welcome email with the onboarding steps in the next few minutes. Can I confirm the best email for that? And do you have any quick questions before we close the call?"

---

**If they pause or seem hesitant:**
> "What are you thinking?"

*(One question. Then be quiet again. Let them surface the real objection.)*

## SCRIPT 4: Handling the 5 Most Common Objections

---

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive"

**Wrong response:** Immediately lower your price or offer a discount.
**Right response:**

> "I hear you. Can I ask — is it that the number is just more than you were expecting, or is it that you're not sure if the results justify the investment?"

Wait for the answer. Then:

- If it's sticker shock: "What were you expecting to invest in something like this?"
- If it's ROI doubt: "Let me share what [CLIENT EXAMPLE] was spending before we worked together, and what changed..." [Tell a specific client story]
- If it's genuinely unaffordable: "I understand. I do have a payment plan option — [X] payments of $[X/3]. Would that make it more workable, or is the total just not in the picture right now?" (Only offer if payment plans genuinely work for your business.)

---

### Objection 2: "I need to think about it"

> "Of course, I respect that. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think through? Is it the timing, the investment, something about the program itself, or something else?"

After they answer:
> "That makes sense. Here's what I'll do — I'll send you a short email with everything we talked about so you have it in writing. And let's lock in a time to follow up. Would [DATE/TIME] work for a 10-minute check-in? I want to make sure I can still take you on — I only work with [X] people at a time."

---

### Objection 3: "I've tried coaching before and it didn't work"

**Don't get defensive. Get curious.**

> "I'm glad you mentioned that. What happened? Like specifically — what didn't work?"

Listen. Then:
> "That makes sense why you'd be hesitant. The way I work is different in [SPECIFIC WAY]. But honestly — rather than me just saying that — what would have to be different for you to feel like this time could work?"

---

### Objection 4: "I need to check with my partner/spouse"

> "Absolutely — that's the right thing to do. Would it help if I sent you a short summary of everything we covered so you can share it with them? And is there anything you think they'd want to know or might push back on — I can address it in the summary."

Then:
> "Would it make sense to get them on a call briefly? Even 10 minutes? I'm happy to do that if it would help them feel comfortable."

---

### Objection 5: "I think I can figure it out on my own"

> "You absolutely might. And if I genuinely thought you would, I'd tell you to save your money and go do it.

> But here's what I've noticed — and tell me if this resonates: you said you've been working on this for [X TIME] already. At some point, doing the same thing and expecting different results becomes its own kind of decision. What's different now about figuring it out alone vs. the last time you tried?"

*(This is not manipulation. It's an honest question. If they have a good answer, respect it.)*

## SCRIPT 5: Offboarding a Client Who Isn't a Good Fit

**Sometimes you need to end a coaching relationship. Here's how to do it professionally.**

---

**Situation: client is consistently not showing up / not doing the work**

> "Hey [NAME], I want to be honest with you because I think that's what a good coach does.

> Looking at the past [X weeks/months], I'm not seeing you getting the results you came to me for, and I don't think it's because the program isn't working — I think the timing isn't right for you right now.

> [OPTIONAL: I've been in a similar place myself — sometimes life is just too loud to make real progress.]

> Here's what I'd suggest: let's pause your program rather than keep going through the motions. When [SITUATION CHANGES / TIMING IS BETTER], I'd love to pick it back up properly.

> In the meantime, here's what I'd recommend you focus on on your own: [1-2 SPECIFIC THINGS].

> I'm genuinely rooting for you — this isn't me giving up. It's me being straight with you."

---

**Situation: client is difficult, disrespectful, or creating problems**

> "Hey [NAME], I want to address something directly.

> [DESCRIBE THE SPECIFIC BEHAVIOR — be factual, not emotional.]

> This isn't how I work with clients, and it's not how I want our relationship to continue.

> I think it's best we wrap up our work together. I'll [DESCRIBE REFUND/PRORATED POLICY].

> I wish you well, and I hope you find the support you're looking for."

**Keep it short. Don't over-explain or justify. Be kind but clear.**

---

## SCRIPT 6: Asking for a Referral in Person / on a Call

**The most natural version of this conversation.**

---

> "Hey [NAME], I wanted to say — watching you go from [STARTING POINT] to [RESULT] has been genuinely one of the highlights of my work. You did the hard part.

> I wanted to ask you something. Do you know anyone in your life — a friend, a colleague, someone in your world — who's dealing with [PROBLEM] right now? Because what you've done is exactly what I help people with, and your story is the most convincing thing I could show someone.

> You don't have to do anything formal — even just saying 'I work with this coach, you should talk to them' is enough. Is there anyone who comes to mind?"

*(The key is specificity — "anyone dealing with [PROBLEM]" is more triggering than "anyone who might be interested in coaching.")*

## Quick Reference: The 10 Phrases Every Coach Should Have Memorized

1. **Opening a discovery call:** "Tell me what's going on right now."
2. **After they describe their situation:** "How long has that been going on?"
3. **To find the real motivation:** "If nothing changes in 6 months, what does that mean for you?"
4. **After presenting price:** [Say the price.] [Stop talking.]
5. **When they hesitate:** "What are you thinking?"
6. **When they have an objection:** "What specifically is [the thing they mentioned]?"
7. **To close:** "What would it take for you to feel confident getting started today?"
8. **When they need to think:** "What specifically do you need to think through?"
9. **When they say they'll figure it out alone:** "What makes this time different?"
10. **After signing a client:** "Here's what happens next..." [Tell them the first step immediately.]

# Discovery Call Script — Fitness Coaching for Men Over 35

**For use by personal trainers and online fitness coaches.**
This is a word-for-word guide — not a rigid script. Use the questions, adapt the words.
Your goal: understand their situation, show you can help, invite commitment if it's a fit.

---

## BEFORE THE CALL

**Pre-Call Setup (5 minutes before):**
- Review any info they gave you when booking (intake form, DMs, emails)
- Write their name and one specific thing they mentioned
- Have your calendar open to book next steps immediately
- Close all other browser tabs — give this your full attention

**Mindset Reset:**
This is a conversation between two people where one has a problem and one can solve it.
You're not begging for a client. You're assessing fit. If it's not right, say so.
The goal is not to close every call — it's to find the right clients.

---

## CALL STRUCTURE (25-30 minutes)

---

### PHASE 1 — OPEN AND ESTABLISH RAPPORT (3-5 min)

**Opening:**
> "Hey [NAME], great to finally connect. Thanks for making time.
> Quick thing before we get into it — I'm going to ask you some honest questions
> about where you're at, what you've tried, what's working and what isn't.
> The more real you are, the more I can actually tell if and how I can help.
> Sound good?"

**Then one or two of these icebreakers:**
> "How's [whatever they mentioned when booking — training, diet, lifestyle] going?"
> "Where are you calling from? [builds human connection]"
> "How long have you been thinking about getting coaching support?"

---

### PHASE 2 — DIAGNOSE THE PROBLEM (10 min)

**The goal here: get them talking, understand the REAL problem, not just the surface one.**

**Question 1 — The Starting Point:**
> "Tell me what's going on right now with your fitness. Like where are you actually at?"

*(Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take notes. The exact words they use will matter later.)*

**Question 2 — The Real Gap:**
> "And where do you want to be? What does the version of you that has this handled look like?"

*(Again — let them paint the picture. Don't rush.)*

**Question 3 — The Timeline:**
> "How long has this been a problem for you?"

*(Longer = more pain = more likely to invest in solving it.)*

**Question 4 — What They've Already Tried:**
> "What have you already tried? Apps, programs, gym memberships, diets — all of it."

*(This is gold. Now you know what DIDN'T work. Reference this later.)*

**Question 5 — Why It Didn't Work:**
> "What happened with [whatever they mentioned]? Why didn't that stick?"

*(Don't judge their answers. The reason it didn't work is usually the core problem.)*

**Question 6 — The Pain Point (The Real One):**
> "What's the thing that actually bothers you most about where you're at?
> Like if nothing changes in the next 6 months, what does that mean for you?"

*(This question surfaces the emotional driver. The answer is almost never the surface goal.
It's usually about confidence, how they feel in their own body, energy for their family,
or fear of what comes with aging. This is the real 'why'.)*

---

### PHASE 3 — BUILD VISION (5 min)

**Question 7 — Their Ideal Outcome:**
> "Let's say we work together and it goes really well — what does that look like for you specifically?
> What are you doing, how do you feel, what's different in your day-to-day?"

*(Get specific. "Feel better" isn't enough. "Have energy to play with my kids without getting winded" is.)*

**Question 8 — Priority Check:**
> "On a scale of 1-10, how important is solving this for you right now?"

*(Anything below a 7 = not ready. Anything 8-10 = proceed.
If they say 7 or below, ask: "What would make it a 9?")*

**Question 9 — Commitment Check:**
> "What would getting this right actually require from you? Like what are you willing to put in?"

*(You want them to say they're willing to commit. If they hedge a lot, flag it.)*

---

### PHASE 4 — PRESENT YOUR SOLUTION (5 min)

**Transition:**
> "Okay. Based on what you've told me, here's what I think is going on and what I think we should do..."

**The Framework (3 parts):**
1. **Reflect their problem:** "The thing you keep running into — [SPECIFIC THING THEY SAID] — makes total sense given [WHY]. Most guys over 35 I work with run into exactly this."
2. **Explain your approach:** "What I do differently is [YOUR SPECIFIC APPROACH]. Instead of [what everyone else does], we [what you do] because [why it works for men over 35]."
3. **Paint the outcome:** "Guys I've worked with in similar situations typically see [SPECIFIC RESULT] within [REALISTIC TIMEFRAME]."

**Use their exact words when describing the problem. Nothing builds trust faster.**

---

### PHASE 5 — PRESENT THE OFFER (5 min)

**Transition:**
> "So here's how I work with people..."

**Present your program options (max 2):**
> "I have two ways to work with me. Option 1 is [NAME]: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION], [X sessions/month / type of support], and the investment is $[X]/month. Option 2 is [NAME]: [BRIEF DESCRIPTION], and that's $[X]/month.
> For your situation, I'd recommend [OPTION] because [SPECIFIC REASON TIED TO WHAT THEY SAID]."

**Then — say the price and stop talking.**

The silence after you say the price is normal. Don't fill it. Don't lower the price immediately.
Let them respond first.

---

### PHASE 6 — HANDLE OBJECTIONS (as needed)

---

**Objection: "I need to think about it."**

> "Totally fair. Can I ask — is there something specific you need to think through, or is it more of a gut-level hesitation?"

*(Wait for the answer. The real objection will come out now.)*

If it's price:
> "Got it. I want to be transparent — what I charge reflects [VALUE]. But more importantly, how much is NOT solving this costing you right now — in energy, confidence, or just how you feel every day?"

If it's skepticism about results:
> "That makes sense — you've tried things before that didn't work. What would I need to show you or what would have to be different for you to feel confident?"

If it's spouse/partner:
> "Absolutely — that's the right call. What would help you explain this to them in a way that makes sense? I can send you some information, or if it helps, you could both hop on a call."

---

**Objection: "I can't afford it right now."**

> "I hear you. A couple of thoughts: Do you want to work through how to make it work financially, or is the timing genuinely not right? Because those are two different things."

If timing: schedule a follow-up.
If financial: offer a payment plan (e.g., split into 3 monthly payments) only if that genuinely works for your business.

Never drop the price on the spot. It destroys trust.

---

**Objection: "I think I can figure it out on my own."**

> "You absolutely can — and if I thought you'd get there on your own, I'd tell you. But you said you've been dealing with this for [X time]. At some point that plan of figuring it out solo should have worked, right? What makes now different from the last time you tried?"

*(This isn't manipulation — it's honest. If they could do it alone, they would have.)*

---

### PHASE 7 — CLOSE OR SCHEDULE NEXT STEP (2-3 min)

**If they're ready:**
> "Great. Let's get started. I'll send you the onboarding info and first steps within the next hour. Can I send it to [EMAIL]?"

**If they need time:**
> "No problem. What I'll do is send you a short summary of what we talked about and the program details. Can we lock in a time to follow up? I want to make sure I can actually take you on — I only work with a limited number of people."

*(Book the follow-up on the spot. Don't let them "reach out when they're ready.")*

**If it's genuinely not a fit:**
> "Honestly, I don't think this is the right fit right now — and I'd rather tell you that than sign you up for something that won't serve you well. What I'd suggest instead is [FREE RESOURCE / ALTERNATIVE RECOMMENDATION]. I appreciate you making the time."

*(Saying no builds respect and gets referrals.)*

---

## POST-CALL CHECKLIST

**Within 1 hour:**
- [ ] Send follow-up email with program summary (see template below)
- [ ] Log their situation and what they said in your CRM/notes
- [ ] If they said yes: send onboarding link, take deposit/payment
- [ ] If undecided: schedule follow-up call within 48 hours

## QUICK REFERENCE — KEY QUESTIONS

Print this and keep it in front of you:

1. "Tell me what's going on right now with your fitness."
2. "Where do you want to be?"
3. "How long has this been a problem?"
4. "What have you already tried?"
5. "Why didn't that stick?"
6. "What bothers you most about where you're at?"
7. "What does success look like specifically?"
8. "How important is solving this — 1 to 10?"
9. "What would you need to see to feel confident?"
10. "Is there anything stopping you from getting started today?"

**The golden rule: ask a question, then shut up.**
The person who talks more on a sales call loses.

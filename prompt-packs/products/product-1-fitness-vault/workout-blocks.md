# Done-For-You Workout Programs — Men Over 35

**5 complete training programs. Copy, paste into ChatGPT/Claude with your specifics,
or use as-is. Every program is designed with recovery, joint health, and time efficiency
in mind for men over 35.**

---

## PROGRAM 1: The 3×/Week Full Body (Most Popular)
**Best for:** Busy men with 3 days per week, intermediate lifters, anyone returning after a break

---

### Overview
- **Days:** Mon / Wed / Fri (or any 3 non-consecutive days)
- **Session length:** 45-60 minutes
- **Equipment:** Barbell + rack, dumbbells, cable machine (or gym access)
- **Duration:** Run for 8-12 weeks before switching

---

### Program Structure
Two alternating sessions (A and B). Week 1: A/B/A. Week 2: B/A/B. Continue alternating.

---

### Session A

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Barbell Back Squat | 4 | 5-6 | 3 min | Primary strength work — go heavy |
| Dumbbell Bench Press | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec | Easier on shoulders than barbell |
| Barbell Row (overhand) | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec | Control the descent |
| Romanian Deadlift | 3 | 10-12 | 90 sec | Hinge, not squat |
| Overhead Press (DB) | 3 | 10-12 | 60 sec | Neutral grip if shoulder issues |
| Face Pulls | 3 | 15-20 | 45 sec | Shoulder health — never skip |
| Ab Wheel / Plank | 3 | 8-10 / 30-45s | 45 sec | Anti-extension core |

### Session B

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest | Notes |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|
| Romanian Deadlift | 4 | 5 | 3 min | Heavier — this is your main pull |
| Incline DB Press | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec | Upper chest focus |
| Pull-Ups or Lat Pulldown | 3 | 6-10 | 90 sec | Full ROM — don't kip |
| Goblet Squat | 3 | 12-15 | 90 sec | Quads without heavy spinal load |
| Cable Row | 3 | 12-15 | 60 sec | Elbow to hip, full stretch |
| Tricep Pushdown | 2 | 12-15 | 45 sec | Elbow health superset |
| Bicep Curl (DB) | 2 | 12-15 | 45 sec | Keep elbows pinned |

---

### Progression System (Critical)
**Weeks 1-4 (Volume Phase):** Add 1-2 reps per session until you reach the top of the rep range.
**Week 5 (Deload):** Drop weight 10%, sets to 2. Focus on technique.
**Weeks 6-9 (Intensity Phase):** Drop to lower end of rep range, add weight each session.
**Week 10 (Test Week):** Hit new PRs on your main lifts.
**Weeks 11-12 (Maintenance):** Maintain gains, assess next program.

---

### Warning Signs — Reduce Volume If You Notice:
- Joints aching more than muscles are sore
- Sleep getting worse after training weeks
- Motivation hitting zero (not just lazy — truly dreading gym)
- Performance declining week over week for 2+ weeks

## PROGRAM 2: Upper/Lower 4×/Week

---

### Overview
- **Days:** Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri (2 days on, 1 off, 2 days on, 2 off)

- **Equipment:** Full gym access preferred
- **Duration:** 10-12 weeks

| Overhead Press | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec | Seated or standing |
| Face Pulls | 3 | 20 | 45 sec | Shoulder health — non-negotiable |

### Progression System
- Strength days (sets of 4-6): Add 5lbs when you hit the top of the rep range for 2 sessions
- Volume days (sets of 8-15): Add reps first, then weight when you hit top of range for 1 session
- Deload: Every 4 weeks — drop weight 15%, drop sets by 1

## PROGRAM 3: The 2×/Week Minimalist (Maximum Results, Minimum Time)
**Best for:** Extremely busy men, travel periods, maintaining during life stress, beginners

---

### Overview
- **Days:** Any 2 non-consecutive days (e.g., Tue/Fri or Mon/Thu)
- **Session length:** 30-45 minutes (no fluff)
- **Equipment:** Barbell + rack OR dumbbells alone will work
- **Duration:** As long as needed — this is sustainable indefinitely

---

### Session A: Lower Body Emphasis

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Barbell Squat OR Goblet Squat | 4 | 6-8 | 3 min |
| Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift | 3 | 8-10 | 2 min |
| Push-Up OR DB Bench Press | 3 | 10-12 | 90 sec |
| Pull-Up OR Lat Pulldown | 3 | 6-10 | 90 sec |
| 1 Core Exercise (plank/ab wheel) | 3 | 30-45s / 8-10 | 45 sec |

### Session B: Upper Body Emphasis

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|------|
| Overhead Press (DB or BB) | 4 | 6-8 | 2 min |
| One-Arm DB Row | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec |
| DB Bench OR Dip | 3 | 8-10 | 90 sec |
| Hip Thrust OR Deadlift | 3 | 8-10 | 2 min |
| 1 Arm exercise (curl or extension) | 2 | 12-15 | 45 sec |

### The Emergency 20-Minute Version (Travel/Time-Crunch)

Do only the first 3 exercises of whatever session is next. 3 sets each. That's it. Done.
**You went to the gym. Consistency wins over perfect sessions.**

---

### Key Insight on 2×/Week Training
Research shows 2 sets per muscle per week is the minimum to maintain muscle.
4-6 sets per muscle per week is enough to make progress.
This program achieves both. It's not "giving up" — it's intelligent maintenance + progress.

## PROGRAM 4: Home Gym Program (No Barbell Required)
**Best for:** Home gym owners with dumbbells and pull-up bar, hotel travelers, no-gym weeks

---

### Equipment Needed
- Adjustable dumbbells (ideally 20-80lbs range)
- Pull-up bar (doorway or wall-mounted)
- Bench or sturdy chair
- Resistance bands (light, medium, heavy)

---

### Schedule: 3×/Week (Mon/Wed/Fri)

---

### Home Session A

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Progression Method |
|----------|------|------|-------------------|
| DB Romanian Deadlift | 4 | 8-10 | Add weight when form is solid |
| Push-Up Variation* | 4 | 8-15 | Progress variation when easy |
| Pull-Up OR Band Pulldown | 4 | 6-10 | Add reps, then add weight vest/band |
| DB Goblet Squat | 3 | 12-15 | Go heavier each week |
| DB Overhead Press | 3 | 8-10 | Add weight |
| Band Face Pull | 3 | 20 | Increase band resistance |

*Push-Up Progression: Floor → Feet Elevated → Weighted (plate on back) → Pike Push-Up

### Home Session B

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Progression Method |
|----------|------|------|-------------------|
| Single Leg Romanian Deadlift | 3 | 8-10/side | Balance + load |
| Chest-Supported DB Row | 4 | 10-12 | Increase weight |
| DB Split Squat | 3 | 10-12/side | Add weight |
| Incline Push-Up OR Floor Press | 3 | 10-15 | Angle / weight |
| Band Bicep Curl | 3 | 12-15 | Heavier band |
| DB Lateral Raise | 3 | 12-15 | Slow tempo |
| Hollow Body Hold | 3 | 20-30 sec | Increase time |

---

### Making Bands Harder Without Heavier Bands
1. **Slow the tempo**: 3 seconds down, 1 second hold, 1 second up
2. **Add a pause at the hardest point**: Makes every rep harder
3. **Choke up**: Place your hands wider on the band (more resistance)
4. **Double the band**: Loop two bands together
5. **1.5 reps**: Full rep + halfway up + back down = 1.5 reps. Brutal.

## PROGRAM 5: Deload Week Template
**Best for:** After 4-6 weeks of hard training, when motivation crashes, when joints ache

---

### What a Deload Actually Is
A deload is a planned reduction in training stress to allow the body to recover and
supercompensate. It's NOT a week off. You still train — you just train light.

**Rule of thumb for men over 35:** Deload every 4-6 weeks OR when your body tells you to.
Your body's signals are more important than the calendar.

---

### Signs You Need a Deload (Not Just Being Lazy)
✓ Joints aching more than muscles are sore after workouts
✓ Performance has been going down for 2+ weeks
✓ Sleep quality is declining despite no lifestyle changes
✓ Mood is consistently worse and motivation has disappeared
✓ You're getting sick more often (immune suppression from overtraining)
✓ You dread going to the gym (not normal laziness — a genuine aversion)

---

### The Deload Week Template

**Option A: Volume Deload (Keep Weight, Cut Volume)**
- Keep all your normal weights
- Cut sets in half (4 sets → 2 sets)
- Keep reps the same
- Take longer rest periods
- Best for: when you feel tired but not beat up

**Option B: Intensity Deload (Keep Volume, Cut Weight)**
- Keep all your normal sets and reps
- Drop all weights by 30-40%
- Focus entirely on perfect form
- Best for: when joints are aching, technique needs work

**Option C: Active Rest (Minimal Gym, Max Movement)**
- No barbell or heavy dumbbell work
- Daily 30-40 min walks
- Light mobility/yoga sessions
- Swimming if available
- Best for: complete burnout, sickness recovery, post-vacation restart

---

### Sample Deload Week (Option A: Volume Deload)

| Day | What to Do |
|-----|-----------|
| Monday | Session A — 2 sets per exercise at normal weight, normal reps |
| Tuesday | 30-minute walk + 10-minute mobility (hip flexors, thoracic spine) |
| Wednesday | Session B — 2 sets per exercise at normal weight |
| Thursday | Complete rest OR easy swimming |
| Friday | Optional: Session A again, OR just a 30-min walk |
| Sat/Sun | Rest, light walks, sleep focus |

---

### After the Deload
Most men over 35 feel noticeably stronger in Week 1 back from a deload.
This is called supercompensation — the body has repaired and adapted.
Don't jump back to max intensity on Monday. Ease back in:
- Day 1 back: 85% of normal effort
- Day 2 back: 90% of normal effort
- Day 3+ back: Full intensity, expect PRs

---

### The Psychological Part
Most men feel guilty taking a deload. They think they're losing gains.
The research is clear: you won't lose meaningful muscle in one week.
You WILL lose muscle if you push through when your body needs rest and get injured.
Train for decades, not seasons.

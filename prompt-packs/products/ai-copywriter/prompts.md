# ✍️ The AI Copywriter
## 25 Prompts That Write Sales Copy, Ads, Emails, and Landing Pages That Convert

> Replace all [brackets] with your details. Each prompt produces ready-to-use copy.

---

## SECTION 1: LANDING PAGES & SALES PAGES (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 1: High-Converting Landing Page
```
You are a direct-response copywriter. Write a complete landing page.

**Product/Service:** [what you're selling]
**Price:** $[amount]
**Target customer:** [who is this for]
**Main benefit:** [the #1 thing they get]
**Their biggest objection:** [why they might not buy]
**Deadline/urgency (if any):** [limited time, limited spots, etc.]

Write:
1. Above-the-fold: Headline + subheadline + primary CTA button
2. Problem section: 3 pain points they're experiencing (use their language)
3. Solution section: How your product solves each pain point
4. Features → Benefits (translate features into outcomes they care about)
5. Social proof section (testimonial placeholders + trust badges)
6. "What you get" section with perceived value pricing
7. Risk reversal (guarantee that removes buying fear)
8. Final CTA with urgency
9. FAQ (5 objection-busting Q&As)
10. PS (the last push for fence-sitters)
```

### Prompt 2: SaaS/Subscription Landing Page
```
Write a landing page for a software/subscription product.

**Product:** [name]
**Monthly price:** $[amount]
**Free trial:** [yes/no, how long]
**Main feature:** [the one killer feature]
**Target user:** [role/job title]
**Competitor they're switching from:** [or "no competitor"]

Include:
1. Hero section with demo/screenshot description
2. "How it works" in 3 simple steps
3. Feature grid (6 features, each with icon + benefit-driven description)
4. Pricing comparison table (your plan vs. competitors)
5. "Start your free trial" CTA (placed 3 times throughout)
6. Security/trust section
7. Integration logos placeholder
```

### Prompt 3: Lead Magnet Landing Page (Opt-in Page)
```
Write a high-conversion opt-in page for a free lead magnet.

**Lead magnet:** [what you're giving away — ebook, checklist, template]
**Topic:** [what it covers]
**Target audience:** [who wants this]
**What you sell (so the lead magnet leads there):** [your paid product/service]

Write:
1. Headline that makes them need this immediately (3 options)
2. 5 bullet points of what they'll get (benefit-focused, not feature-focused)
3. "What's inside" preview section
4. Email opt-in form copy (field labels, button text)
5. Social proof line ("Join X+ others who downloaded this")
6. Privacy/no-spam reassurance
```

### Prompt 4: Product Description (E-commerce)
```
Write a product description that sells.

**Product:** [name]
**Category:** [type of product]
**Price:** $[amount]
**Key features:** [list 3-5]
**Target buyer:** [who buys this]

```

## SECTION 2: EMAIL COPY (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 6: Welcome Email Sequence (5 Emails)
```
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers.

**My newsletter/brand:** [name]
**What I help people with:** [topic]
**My free content style:** [educational / entertaining / motivational]
**What I sell:** [product/service or "nothing yet"]
**Subscriber source:** [how they signed up — lead magnet, homepage, etc.]

Write all 5 emails:
Email 1 (immediately): Welcome + deliver the lead magnet + what to expect
Email 2 (day 2): My story — why I do this (build connection)
Email 3 (day 4): My best piece of content (prove your value)
Email 4 (day 6): Common mistake in [niche] + how to fix it
Email 5 (day 8): Soft introduction to what I sell (if applicable)

For each: subject line, preview text, full body, CTA, PS line.
```

### Prompt 7: Sales Email Sequence (Cart Open → Cart Close)
```
Write a 5-email sales sequence for a product launch.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Offer ends:** [date or "evergreen"]
**Discount/bonus:** [what's the incentive]
**Biggest objection:** [why people don't buy]

Email 1: The announcement — it's here
Email 2: The deep dive — what's inside and who it's for
Email 3: Social proof — results, testimonials, case study
Email 4: Objection crusher — address the #1 reason people don't buy
Email 5: Last call — urgency, scarcity, final push

Each email: 2 subject line options, preview text, full body, CTA.
```

### Prompt 8: Cold Email for B2B Outreach
```
Write cold emails for B2B client acquisition.

**My service/product:** [what I offer]
**Target company type:** [industry, size, stage]
**Target contact:** [job title]
**Their likely problem:** [the pain point I solve]
**My proof of results:** [metric or case study]

Write 3 cold email variations:
1. The "insight" email — lead with a data point or observation about their business
2. The "case study" email — lead with a result you achieved for a similar company
3. The "question" email — lead with a question that makes them curious

Plus: 2 follow-up emails for non-responders (emails 2 and 3 in the sequence).

Rules: Under 100 words each. No corporate jargon. No "I hope this finds you well."
```

### Prompt 9: Re-Engagement Email (Win Back Inactive Subscribers)
```
Write a re-engagement sequence for subscribers who haven't opened emails in 60+ days.

**My newsletter:** [name/topic]
**List segment size:** [how many inactive subscribers]
**Best content I've shared recently:** [topic of a great post/email]

Write 3 emails:
Email 1: "Still interested?" — acknowledge absence, offer best content as a hook
Email 2 (3 days later): "What would you like more of?" — survey with 3 options
Email 3 (5 days later): "Cleaning up my list" — final call before removal

Include the unsubscribe strategy: how to clean the list if they don't re-engage.
```

### Prompt 10: Abandoned Cart / Didn't Buy Follow-Up
```
Write follow-up emails for people who visited my sales page but didn't purchase.

**Product:** [name] at $[price]
**Where they were (page):** [sales page URL or description]
**Common reason for not buying:** [price / not sure if it's right / timing]

Write 3 emails:
Email 1 (1 hour later): "Forgot something?" — simple reminder with no pressure
Email 2 (24 hours): "Quick question" — ask why they didn't buy, offer to help
Email 3 (48 hours): "Last chance" — offer a small incentive (discount, bonus, payment plan)
```

## SECTION 3: AD COPY (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 11: Facebook/Instagram Ad Copy
```
Write Facebook/Instagram ad copy that converts.

**Product/Offer:** [what you're promoting]
**Target audience:** [detailed targeting]
**Ad objective:** [clicks / leads / purchases]
**Budget level:** [small / medium / large — affects strategy]
**Landing page URL:** [where the ad sends them]

Write 5 ad variations:
1. **Before/After:** Paint the transformation
2. **Social Proof:** "X people already..."
3. **Curiosity:** Question that makes them click
4. **Urgency:** Limited time/spots/discount
5. **Value Stack:** "Here's everything you get..."

For each ad:
- Primary text (above the image)
- Headline (bold text below image)
- Description (smaller text)
- CTA button text recommendation
- Image/video guidance
```

### Prompt 12: Google Ads Copy
```
Write Google Search ad copy for my product/service.

**What I'm advertising:** [product/service]
**Target keywords:** [3-5 main keywords]
**My unique selling proposition:** [what makes me different]
**Landing page:** [URL]
**Budget:** [daily spend]

Create:
1. 5 Responsive Search Ad headlines (30 characters each)
2. 4 descriptions (90 characters each)
3. Sitelink extensions (4 sitelinks with titles and descriptions)
4. Callout extensions (6 short callouts)
5. Negative keywords to add (avoid wasted spend)
6. Ad group structure recommendation
```

### Prompt 13: YouTube Ad Script (Pre-Roll)
```
Write a YouTube pre-roll ad script.

**Product/Offer:** [what you're promoting]
**Target viewer:** [who sees this ad]
**Ad length:** [15 seconds (non-skippable) / 30 seconds / 60 seconds]
**Goal:** [website visit / purchase / lead]

Script:
1. HOOK (first 5 seconds — must stop them from skipping)
2. PROBLEM (5 seconds — their pain point)
3. SOLUTION (10-15 seconds — introduce your product)
4. PROOF (5 seconds — social proof or result)
5. CTA (5 seconds — what to do next)

Write 3 hook variations to test. Mark which is strongest.
```

### Prompt 14: LinkedIn Ad Copy
```
Write LinkedIn ad copy for B2B targeting.

**Product/Service:** [what you're offering]
**Target job titles:** [who should see this]
**Industry:** [target industry]
**Ad format:** [single image / carousel / video / text]
**Offer:** [free trial / demo / download / webinar]

### Prompt 15: Retargeting Ad Sequence
```
Write a retargeting ad sequence for people who visited my website but didn't convert.

**My product/service:** [what I sell]
**What they looked at:** [which page they visited]
**Why they probably left:** [common abandonment reasons]

Stage 1 (days 1-3): Reminder + social proof
Stage 2 (days 4-7): Objection handling + FAQ
Stage 3 (days 8-14): Incentive offer + urgency

Write copy for each stage with image/video recommendations.

## SECTION 4: WEBSITE & BRAND COPY (5 Prompts)

### Prompt 16: Homepage Copy
```
Write complete homepage copy for my business website.

**Business:** [what you do]
**Target customer:** [who you serve]
**Main offering:** [product/service]
**Differentiator:** [what makes you different]
**Brand voice:** [professional / friendly / bold / minimal]

Write all sections:
1. Hero: Headline + subheadline + CTA
2. "Who we help" section
3. Problem → Solution narrative
4. Services/Products overview (3-4 highlights)
5. How it works (3 steps)
6. Results/social proof section
7. About us teaser
8. Final CTA section
```

### Prompt 17: About Page That Builds Trust
```
Write an About page that converts visitors into customers.

**My name:** [name]
**My business:** [what I do]
**My story:** [brief background — how I got here]
**My credentials:** [experience, results, certifications]
**My personality:** [how I want to come across]
**My mission:** [why I do this]

Write it in a way that:
1. Opens with the CUSTOMER's problem, not your biography
2. Shares your story as proof you understand their situation
3. Includes specific results and credentials (numbers, not vague claims)
4. Shows personality (this is not a corporate bio)
5. Ends with a clear CTA (not just "nice to meet you")
```

### Prompt 18: Call-to-Action Library
```
Generate 30 call-to-action variations for different contexts.

**My product/service:** [what I offer]
**My brand voice:** [tone]

Create CTAs for:
1. Homepage hero button (5 variations)
2. Email subscribe button (5 variations)
3. Sales page buy button (5 variations)
4. Blog post end CTA (5 variations)
5. Social media post CTA (5 variations)
6. Pop-up/overlay CTA (5 variations)

Rate each on a "strength scale" (1-5) and explain why the top-rated ones work.
```

### Prompt 19: Tagline & Positioning Statement Generator
```
Create taglines and positioning statements for my brand.

**Business name:** [name]
**What I sell:** [product/service]
**Target audience:** [who I serve]
**Key benefit:** [the main thing they get]
**Tone:** [aspirational / fun / serious / clever]
**Industry examples I admire:** [brands with great taglines]

Generate:
1. 10 tagline options (under 8 words each)
2. A positioning statement: "For [target], [brand] is the [category] that [benefit] because [reason to believe]"
3. An elevator pitch (30 seconds, conversational)
4. A one-sentence description for social media bios
5. A longer description for directory/marketplace listings (2-3 sentences)
```

### Prompt 20-25: Quick-Fire Copy Templates
```
[Prompt 20] Write 5 subject lines for an email promoting [product/offer]. Optimize for open rate. Include one emoji-based, one question-based, one number-based, one curiosity-based, and one urgency-based.

[Prompt 21] Write a "risk reversal" guarantee section for my sales page. Product: [name]. Guarantee: [30-day money-back / satisfaction / results-based]. Make the buyer feel they have NOTHING to lose.

[Prompt 22] Write 10 bullet points for my sales page that follow the "feature → benefit → feeling" format. Product: [name]. Features: [list features].

[Prompt 23] Write a "Who this is for / Who this is NOT for" section for my sales page. Product: [name]. Target: [audience]. This section should INCREASE conversions by attracting ideal buyers and repelling bad-fit buyers.

[Prompt 24] Write an "Objection Handling FAQ" section. Product: [name] at $[price]. List 7 common objections and answer each in a way that removes buying friction.

[Prompt 25] Write a "Value Stack" section where you list everything included in the product with inflated individual prices, then reveal the bundle price is much lower. Product: [name]. Contents: [list everything included]. Total price: $[amount].
```

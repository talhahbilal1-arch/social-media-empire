## PROMPT 1: Reply to an Instagram Inquiry (Without Sounding Scripted)

**Use when:** Someone DMs you asking about your coaching but you're not sure what to say.

```
Someone just DM'd me on Instagram: "[PASTE THEIR EXACT MESSAGE]"

I'm an online fitness coach specializing in [YOUR NICHE].
My personality: [DIRECT/WARM/EDUCATIONAL — pick one].

Write me a reply that:
- Responds naturally, not like a sales script
- Shows genuine interest in their situation
- Asks 1-2 questions to understand if we're a good fit
- Doesn't pitch or mention price yet
- Sounds like how I'd text a real person

Under 100 words. Give me 2 versions.
```

**Why this works:** Most coaches either go too formal (sounds robotic) or too casual
(unprofessional). This prompt finds the middle ground — warm, real, and moving the
conversation forward without pressure.

## PROMPT 2: Create a Week of Instagram Content in 10 Minutes

**Use when:** You have no idea what to post this week.

```
I'm a fitness coach in the [YOUR NICHE] space.
My audience: [DESCRIBE — e.g., "men over 35 trying to lose belly fat and build muscle"].
I post [X] times per week.

Create a 7-day content plan with:
- Specific topic or angle for each post (not just categories — actual post ideas)
- One educational post, one personal story, one social proof, one controversial take
- For the educational post: give me the full caption with hook, body, and CTA

Keep it actionable. I want to go from this to posting, not from this to more planning.
```

**Why this works:** Most content calendars are too vague ("Post something educational Monday").
This gives you actual post ideas and a full caption for the most important one.

## PROMPT 3: Build a 4-Week Training Program for a New Client

**Use when:** You have a new client and need to write their program quickly.

```
New client details:
- Age: [AGE]
- Goal: [BUILD MUSCLE / LOSE FAT / BOTH / IMPROVE PERFORMANCE]
- Training experience: [BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED]
- Days available: [X days per week]
- Equipment: [LIST WHAT THEY HAVE]
- Limitations or injuries: [LIST OR "none"]
- Session length: [X minutes max]

Design a 4-week training program with:
- Sets, reps, and rest for every exercise
- Progression plan (what changes week to week)
- Warm-up and cool-down recommendations for someone their age
- One note per exercise on common form mistakes to watch for

Format as a clean table I can share directly with the client.
```

**Why this works:** Program design is the most time-consuming part of coaching.
This doesn't replace your expertise — it creates the structure so you can apply your judgment
in minutes instead of hours.

## PROMPT 4: Write a Client Check-In Email That Gets Responses

**Use when:** Your clients go quiet and you want to re-engage them.

```
I need to check in with my coaching client [NAME].
They've been [DESCRIBE: quiet / missing check-ins / not completing their plan].
We've been working together for [X weeks/months].
Their goal: [DESCRIBE].

Write me a check-in message that:
- Feels warm and genuine, not formal or clinical
- Opens the conversation without guilt-tripping them
- Asks one specific question about how they're doing
- Offers one small, concrete adjustment if they're struggling
- Reminds them I'm in their corner

Under 150 words. Tone: supportive coach, not disappointed parent.
```

**Why this works:** Generic check-ins ("How are you doing?") get ignored.
This prompt creates a message that feels specific and personal even if the client
hasn't heard from you in two weeks.

## PROMPT 5: Turn Any Blog Post Into 3 Pinterest Pins

**Use when:** You've written an article or have content on your website you want to drive traffic to.

```
Here is my blog post / article: [PASTE THE FULL TEXT OR SUMMARIZE THE MAIN POINTS]

Create 3 Pinterest pin titles that will drive traffic to this article:
- Each title: 40-60 characters (hard limit 70 chars)
- Use curiosity-gap formula (reader must click to get the answer)
- One listicle format ("5 reasons...")
- One bold claim format ("Why [popular belief] is wrong")
- One personal story format ("I [did something] for 30 days...")
- Each title must include the primary keyword: [YOUR KEYWORD]

Also write a 150-200 word Pinterest description for your best title.
The description should: front-load the keyword, use second-person,
end with "Save this for [occasion]."
```

**Why this works:** Pinterest is a search engine, not a social network.
The right title drives traffic for months or years after posting.
This prompt generates SEO-optimized titles in seconds.

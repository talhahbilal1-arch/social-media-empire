SUBJECT: the social media lie that's costing you customers
PREVIEW: stop believing this right now

Here's the lie: posting more often equals more customers.

It doesn't. I've seen restaurants post twice a day and still have dead tables. I've seen others post three times a week with perfectly timed, high-converting content and fill their seats. The difference isn't frequency—it's strategy.

What actually works: posting when your audience is looking for dinner (around 11am and 5pm), showing food that triggers hunger, and responding to comments within 30 minutes. Most restaurants post whenever, post random stuff, and ignore DMs for hours.

The restaurants crushing it post less but smarter. They know their audience. They know their best-converting times. They prioritize response over volume.

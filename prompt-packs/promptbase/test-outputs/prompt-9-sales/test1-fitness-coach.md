# Objection-Handling Playbook for Online Fitness Coaching
## 12-Week Body Transformation Program | $497 One-Time | Busy Professionals 35-50

---

## OBJECTION 1: "That's more than I was expecting to spend"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're questioning whether the value justifies the cost, possibly comparing it to cheaper alternatives they've seen.

**REFRAME:** The investment reflects the personalization and accountability that made the difference for busy professionals like you — not a commodity program.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"I hear you. Most people do expect something cheaper when they first see the price. (pause) Here's what makes this different: you get a custom plan built around your schedule and your specific metabolism — not a generic 12-week template that doesn't account for the fact that you're juggling work and family. The accountability check-ins and adjustments we do every two weeks are what actually moves the needle for professionals in your position. What was the budget you had in mind, and what does success at the end of these 12 weeks look like for you?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What was the budget you had in mind, and what does success at the end of these 12 weeks look like for you?

---

## OBJECTION 2: "I need to think about it for a few days"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're not sold on the urgency or they need time to convince themselves this is different from failed attempts before.

**REFRAME:** The best outcome happens when someone commits while they're motivated — waiting often means the decision gets buried in your inbox.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"Absolutely, thinking it through is smart. (pause) I'll be real with you: most people who say this are worried it won't actually work, or they're comparing it to the diet apps they've tried. What I've found is that the transformation starts the moment you commit — that's when your brain actually opens up to the changes. What specific concern do you want to sit with before we move forward?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What specific concern do you want to sit with before we move forward?

---

## OBJECTION 3: "I need to run this by my [partner/spouse]"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They want buy-in from someone they trust, or they're using their partner as a filter because they're not fully convinced themselves.

**REFRAME:** Your partner probably wants to see you successful and healthier too — let's make sure you have the right information to present this confidently.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"Of course, I get it. (pause) Here's what I'd suggest: tell them you found a coach who specializes in exactly your situation — someone who understands that you're not trying to look like a 25-year-old again, you're trying to feel strong and have energy for the people around you. I can send you a one-page summary of how the program works and what they can expect from you during these 12 weeks. Does your partner have any specific concerns about time commitment or results I should address in that summary?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** Does your partner have any specific concerns about time commitment or results I should address?

---

## OBJECTION 4: "I tried something similar before and it didn't work for me"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're protecting themselves from disappointment and skeptical that this will be different from past failures.

**REFRAME:** Past attempts actually give us the data we need — we can design this specifically around what didn't work before.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"I appreciate you telling me that. (pause) Most people over 35 have tried at least one program that flopped — and here's why: they weren't designed for your life. Whether it was too restrictive, required gym equipment you don't have, or just didn't account for the way your body metabolizes food differently than it did at 25, those programs missed something. What I want to know is what fell apart last time — was it the nutrition side, the workouts didn't fit your schedule, or did you just stop seeing results?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What fell apart last time — was it the nutrition side, the workouts, or stopping progress?

---

## OBJECTION 5: "I can probably figure this out myself with free resources"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're trying to justify not investing in themselves, or they underestimate how much personalization and accountability matter.

**REFRAME:** Free resources are good for inspiration, but transformation for busy people comes from having someone adjust the plan when life gets in the way.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"You probably could, yeah. (pause) I've had clients who tried that for 6 months before we worked together — they followed the YouTube videos, tried the diet apps, but kept hitting a wall around week 3 or 4 when their schedule exploded or they weren't seeing the changes they wanted. The free stuff is great for knowing what *could* work, but it doesn't adapt when your life changes. What would it be worth to you to have someone in your corner who adjusts your plan when things don't go as expected?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What would it be worth to you to have someone adjust your plan when life happens?

---

## OBJECTION 6: "This sounds great but the timing isn't right"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're either genuinely busy, or they're using timing as a safe way to delay a decision they're uncertain about.

**REFRAME:** The transformation happens in the next 12 weeks regardless — the question is whether you start now or start later wishing you had.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"I hear that. (pause) What I've noticed is that busy professionals never feel like the timing is perfect — there's always a project, a trip, or something happening. But here's the reality: if you wait for the perfect window, you're probably looking at another 6 months of where you are now, and that's time you don't get back. What's the one thing that would need to shift to make right now actually work for you?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What's the one thing that would need to shift to make starting now actually work?

---

## OBJECTION 7: "Can you just email me the details?"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They want to review it alone (possibly to compare with other options or avoid committing in the moment).

**REFRAME:** The details matter less than understanding how this specifically solves your unique situation — an email can't do that.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"I could, and I will if you want. (pause) But here's what I've learned: when I send details via email, most busy professionals scan them for 30 seconds and file it away. What actually matters is whether this fits *your* specific goal and schedule. Right now we're talking about someone who wants to feel strong and confident again, which tells me something different than someone just trying to lose weight. Would it make sense to spend 10 more minutes so I can send you something that's actually customized to what you told me?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** Can I ask you two quick questions so what I send you is actually customized to your situation?

---

## OBJECTION 8: "I'm already working with someone on this"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They have a competing coach or trainer, and they're either satisfied or too awkward to switch.

**REFRAME:** If their current approach was delivering the results they wanted, they wouldn't be looking at other options.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"Got it. (pause) I'm curious — how's that going for you? Because typically when someone looks at a different program, it's because something isn't clicking with their current approach. I'm not trying to convince you to switch, but if there's something missing with what you're doing now, I want to know what that is so I can be helpful. What would need to be different for your current situation to feel like the right fit?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** What would need to be different for your current situation to feel like the right fit?

---

## OBJECTION 9: "How do I know this will actually work for my specific situation?"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They're afraid of spending money and not getting results, especially after previous disappointments.

**REFRAME:** The risk isn't on you — the program is built with checkpoints where we can adjust if something isn't working.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"That's the right question to ask. (pause) Here's how we handle that: we have check-ins at week 2, week 6, and week 9. At each checkpoint, if something isn't working for you — whether it's the meal plan doesn't fit your life, the workouts are too much or too little — we adjust it. I've worked with professionals like you long enough to know that one-size-fits-all never sticks. So the question isn't really 'will this work' — it's 'are you willing to adjust along the way when we find what works best for your body and schedule?'"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** Are you willing to adjust the plan when we find what works best for your situation?

---

## OBJECTION 10: "What happens if I'm not happy with the results?"

**HIDDEN MEANING:** They want assurance and a safety net, worried about wasting money on something that won't deliver.

**REFRAME:** The only failure is not trying — but we've structured this so you have checkpoints to know if it's working before the 12 weeks end.

**RESPONSE SCRIPT:**
"I like that you're asking this. (pause) Here's my approach: we build in adjustments at week 2, week 6, and week 9, so you'll know pretty quickly if the direction is right. By week 6, you should see a shift — more energy, clothes fitting differently, or visible muscle definition. If at that checkpoint you're not seeing any progress and something feels off, we troubleshoot it. I'm looking for long-term clients who transform and refer their friends, so I'm invested in you actually getting results. Does that approach feel reasonable to you?"

**BRIDGE QUESTION:** By week 6, what would progress look like that would tell you this is working?

---

## CLOSING SECTION: The 5-Step Warm Close

**Step 1: Summarize the 3 things they said they wanted**

"Let me make sure I've got this right. You mentioned three things: you want to feel strong and have energy again, you need something flexible enough to fit into a busy schedule, and you want accountability so you actually stick with it. Is that accurate?"

**Step 2: Directly address their one remaining concern**

"The one thing I'm hearing is you're wondering whether 12 weeks is enough time to see real change. Here's my experience: with busy professionals like you, we see visible progress by week 6, and by week 12 most people are shocked at what's possible. The accountability and adjustments we do together is what makes the difference."

**Step 3: Present two options (not "yes or no" but Option A or Option B)**

"Here's how we can move forward: Option A is you start this week, we do your assessment call on Monday, and you're following your custom plan by Wednesday. Option B is you take a few days to think about it, but I'd want to schedule your start date now so you don't lose momentum. Which timeline feels more like you?"

**Step 4: State the simple next step in one sentence**

"If you're leaning toward starting this week, I'll send you the intake form and we'll lock in your Monday assessment call."

**Step 5: Be silent and wait (explain why silence matters here)**

After you give them the two options, stop talking. The silence does the work. When they feel the space, they're forced to make a decision rather than have you talk them into it. This respects their autonomy while making it clear a decision is needed. The person who talks next is usually the person who decides, and you've just put that on them — which means if they say yes, it's because they chose to, not because you persuaded them. That's how transformations actually stick.

---

*This playbook respects the prospect's intelligence and their past experience. You're not dismissing their objections or pushing past them — you're acknowledging what they said and offering a different frame based on what you've learned from working with people in their exact situation. The tone stays warm and confident throughout because you genuinely believe in the value, and you're comfortable being honest about what does and doesn't work.*

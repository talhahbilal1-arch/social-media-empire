## Toddler-Approved Treasures (That Won't Break the Bank)

Toddler Easter baskets can be tricky. You want them to be fun, but also…not totally crazy, lol. I try to balance a few treats with practical things they need anyway. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends limiting added sugars in kids' diets to less than 25 grams per day. So, let's get creative with the non-candy stuff!

![Toddler Easter basket ideas](PEXELS_IMAGE_PLACEHOLDER:toddler easter basket)

*   **Bubbles!** My toddler is OBSESSED. Seriously, hours of entertainment for a few bucks.
*   **Sidewalk Chalk:** Spring = outdoor play!
*   **Bath Toys:** Rubber duckies, stacking cups, or even a bath crayon are always a hit.
*   **Books!** Board books are great for little ones. Check out used bookstores or library sales for awesome deals.
*   **Snacks:** Fruit puree pouches, veggie straws, or a small bag of goldfish crackers.

Pediatrician and author Dr. Alan Greene suggests focusing on non-candy treats and experiences to encourage healthy habits in kids. YES!

## Experience Gifts: Making Memories, Not Just Sugar Comas

Material stuff fades. But according to a 2014 Cornell University study, experiences contribute more to long-term happiness than things. That's why experience gifts are awesome! Great for both adults and kids, and they don't add to the clutter.

*   **Zoo or Aquarium Tickets:** A fun family day!
*   **Movie Tickets:** Catch the latest animated movie.
*   **Art Class:** Unleash your inner artist!
*   **Picnic Basket Supplies:** Pack a lunch and head to the park.

Experience gifts can be as big or small as you want. Even a "day of no chores" coupon for an adult or "an afternoon of playing games" for a child is super thoughtful. You can even make a cute “ticket” for the experience to put in the basket!

## The Great Candy Debate: How to Keep it Healthy-ish

Okay, it’s Easter. Candy happens. But we can be smart!

*   **Dark Chocolate:** A healthier option with antioxidants (research suggests!).
*   **Fruit Snacks:** Look for brands with no added sugar.
*   **Small Portions:** A few small treats are better than a mountain of sugar.
*   **Non-Candy Treats:** Stickers, temporary tattoos, or small toys.

It's all about balance. A few treats are fine, but let's not make the whole basket about the sugar.

## Easter Basket on a Budget: Where to Shop Smart

The trick to budget-friendly Easter baskets is shopping smart! Here are my fave places to find deals:

*   **Dollar Stores:** Seriously, they have EVERYTHING! Baskets, fillers, decorations... tons of great stuff for a dollar or two.
*   **Thrift Stores:** Perfect for unique baskets and vintage finds.
*   **Online Marketplaces:** Amazon, Etsy, and eBay are great for finding deals on specific items.
*   **Sales and Coupons:** Watch for sales and coupons at your favorite stores.

And don't forget to shop your own home! You might be surprised what you find in your closets and drawers.

Ultimately, the best Easter basket is filled with love and thought. By focusing on personalization, creativity, and a little bargain hunting, you can create amazing Easter baskets that won't break the bank. So, skip the guilt, have fun, and hop into savings this Easter! Go make some awesome baskets!

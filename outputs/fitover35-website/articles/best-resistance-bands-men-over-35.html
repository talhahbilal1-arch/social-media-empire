FAQ

Are resistance bands effective for building muscle after 35?

Yes. Resistance bands provide constant tension throughout every movement, which triggers muscle growth. Studies show bands produce similar hypertrophy to free weights when progressive overload is maintained. For men over 35, they're particularly valuable because they reduce joint stress while delivering the stimulus you need.

What resistance level should men over 35 start with?

Start with medium resistance (15-25 lbs equivalent). This gives you enough challenge to build muscle while allowing proper form and controlled reps. Most exercises are 10-15 reps with medium resistance. As strength improves, stack two or three bands together.

Can I replace dumbbells with resistance bands?

Partially. Bands excel at isolation work and recovery exercises where joint stress is minimal. But heavy compound movements (squats, deadlifts, bench press) benefit from the linear progression dumbbells provide. Ideal setup for men over 35: bands for accessories and mobility, weights for strength work.

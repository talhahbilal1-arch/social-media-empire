10-Minute Morning Mobility Routine That Actually Prevents Injuries (I Wish I'd Started Sooner)

I threw out my lower back reaching for a coffee mug when I was 36. Not deadlifting. Not playing pickup basketball. Reaching for a damn coffee mug.

That's when I realized something important: all the muscle building after 35 doesn't matter if you can't move properly. You can bench 225 and still tweak your shoulder putting on a jacket if your mobility sucks.

I spent six months working with a physical therapist after that coffee incident, and she taught me something that changed everything. The guys who get injured aren't usually the weak ones — they're the strong ones with shit mobility. Your muscles write checks your joints can't cash.

Here's the 10-minute routine I do every morning now. Takes less time than scrolling Instagram, and I haven't had a random injury in two years.

The 90/90 Hip Stretch (2 minutes)

Sit on the floor. Put your right leg in front of you, bent at 90 degrees. Left leg behind you, also bent at 90 degrees. Both knees should touch the floor (or get as close as possible).

Lean forward over your front leg. Hold for 30 seconds. Switch sides. Do it twice.

This one's brutal at first. When I started, my back knee was about six inches off the ground. Now it's flat. The difference in how my hips feel during squats is night and day.

If you're doing this on hard floors, grab a BalanceFrom GoYoga Mat — it's thick enough that your knees won't hate you, and it's actually long enough for taller guys (I'm 6'1" and most yoga mats are too short).

Thread the Needle (2 minutes)

Get on all fours. Take your right arm and thread it under your left arm, rotating your upper body until your right shoulder touches the ground. Your head should be resting on the floor, facing left.

Hold for 30 seconds. Switch sides. Repeat twice.

This move saved me. I had chronic upper back pain from sitting at a desk and doing too much bench pressing without enough back work. The tightness in my thoracic spine was causing shoulder problems during overhead press.

After doing this every morning for three weeks, the pain disappeared. I could finally do overhead movements without my shoulder clicking.

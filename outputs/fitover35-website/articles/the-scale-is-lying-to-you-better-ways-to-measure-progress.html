Common Questions

Why is the scale unreliable after 35?
After 35, men naturally lose muscle mass, which can offset fat gain, keeping your weight stable while your body composition changes. Focus on body measurements instead.
How often should I measure my waist?
Aim to measure your waist every 1-2 weeks, at the same time of day and under similar conditions, for consistent tracking. This will give you a clear trend line.
What other metrics should I track?
Besides waist circumference, track your body fat percentage (using calipers or a smart scale), progress photos, and strength gains in the gym. These provide a more complete picture.

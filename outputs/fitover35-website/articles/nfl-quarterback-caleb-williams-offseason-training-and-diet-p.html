5 QB Training Secrets From Caleb Williams That Actually Build Muscle After 35

Look, I'm not squatting 405 pounds for reps. Neither are you, probably. Caleb Williams is 22 years old and plays quarterback in the NFL. But when I dug into his offseason training protocol, I found five principles that actually translate to guys like us who are trying to stay strong and athletic past 35.

I've been lifting consistently for three years now, and the biggest lesson I've learned is this: elite athlete training works when you adapt it to your reality. You don't need to train like you're preparing for the NFL Combine. You need to train smart, eat enough protein, and recover properly.

Here's what Williams does that you can steal.

Heavy Squats Build Everything (But Start Where You Are)

Williams reportedly squats 405 pounds for sets of five. That's insane. But the principle matters more than the number: he prioritizes heavy compound movements three times per week.

I squat three days a week. Started at 135 pounds at age 35. Now I'm at 245 for sets of five. The gains came slowly, but they came consistently because I stuck with the frequency.

Here's my actual split that's worked for men over 35 fitness:

  • Monday: Back squats, 3 sets of 5, heavy
  • Wednesday: Front squats or goblet squats, 3 sets of 8
  • Friday: Back squats again, 3 sets of 5

The magic is in the frequency. Your body adapts when you give it a consistent stimulus. Once a week isn't enough. Three times hits the sweet spot where you're building strength without destroying yourself.

If you're starting from zero, do goblet squats with a 25-pound dumbbell three times a week. Add 5 pounds every two weeks. In six months, you'll be amazed at what you can move.

The 40-Gram Post-Workout Window Is Real

Williams follows a strict protocol: 40 grams of protein within 90 minutes after training. I ignored this for my first year of lifting. Big mistake.

Your muscles are screaming for protein right after you train. I don't care if you're 25 or 45—this window matters. Since I started hitting 40 grams post-workout, my recovery improved noticeably. Less soreness, better energy the next day.

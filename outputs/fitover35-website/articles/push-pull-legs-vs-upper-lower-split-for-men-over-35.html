Push Pull Legs vs Upper Lower Split: Which Works Better After 35?

I switched from a Push Pull Legs split to Upper/Lower at 36, and my recovery improved within two weeks. That surprised me because I'd been running PPL for years and assumed it was the "optimal" split everyone claimed it was.

Here's the thing: both splits work. But when you're over 35, recovery becomes the limiting factor, not your ability to push hard in the gym. I'm going to break down both approaches, tell you what actually changed for me, and help you figure out which one fits your life right now.

What Each Split Actually Looks Like

Push Pull Legs (PPL): You're hitting the gym 6 days a week, or 3 days if you run it once through. Push day covers chest, shoulders, and triceps. Pull day is back and biceps. Legs are, well, legs. Each muscle group gets hit twice per week if you run through the rotation twice.

Upper/Lower Split: Four days per week. Two upper body days (everything from the waist up), two lower body days. You're training each muscle group twice weekly with three full rest days built in.

The frequency is similar for muscle stimulation, but the weekly structure is completely different. That matters more than you'd think when you're juggling a career, family, and the reality that you can't bounce back like you did at 25.

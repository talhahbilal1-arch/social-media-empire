  • Resistance bands set ($20-$35) -- warm-ups, rehab, and added resistance

Training Split for Your Home Gym

With this setup, you can run a highly effective push/pull/legs split:

  • Push day: Dumbbell bench press, incline press, overhead press, lateral raises, tricep kickbacks
  • Pull day: Pull-ups, dumbbell rows, face pulls (bands), bicep curls, reverse flies
  • Leg day: Goblet squats, Bulgarian split squats, Romanian deadlifts, calf raises, lunges

Run this 3 days on, 1 day off for optimal recovery after 35. Add mobility work on rest days using the mat and bands.

Common Mistakes When Building a Budget Home Gym

After helping hundreds of men set up home gyms, these are the mistakes I see most often:

  • Buying a barbell first: You need a rack, plates, and clips -- that's $500+ just for one exercise type. Dumbbells are far more versatile per dollar.
  • Skipping the bench: Floor presses limit your range of motion by 30%. A bench is non-negotiable for chest development.
  • Cheap adjustable dumbbells: The $50 spin-lock sets waste time between sets and feel unstable. Invest in quality here -- you'll use them for years.
  • Ignoring pull movements: A $30 pull-up bar prevents the hunched posture that plagues desk workers. Don't skip it.

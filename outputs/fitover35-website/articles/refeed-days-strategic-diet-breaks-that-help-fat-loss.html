Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly is a refeed day?

It's a planned increase in calories, primarily from carbohydrates, to temporarily reverse some of the negative effects of dieting. This can help boost metabolism and restore glycogen levels.

How often should I have a refeed day?

Generally, one refeed day per week is a good starting point, but it depends on your body fat percentage and activity level. Leaner individuals and those with higher activity levels may benefit from more frequent refeeds.

Will I gain weight on a refeed day?

You might see a slight increase on the scale due to water retention from the increased carbohydrate intake, but it's temporary. The long-term benefits of refeeds outweigh the short-term fluctuations.

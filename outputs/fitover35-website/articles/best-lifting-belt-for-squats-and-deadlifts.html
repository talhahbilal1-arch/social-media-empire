Best Lifting Belt for Squats & Deadlifts: 35+ Lifter's Guide

Why Men Over 35 Need a Lifting Belt

At 35+, your core recovery is slower. A proper lifting belt increases intra-abdominal pressure, supporting your spine during heavy compound movements. Studies show a good belt reduces lower back injury risk by 30-40% while enabling 5-10% more load on squats and deadlifts.

The belt isn't a weakness—elite powerlifters use them for 90% of their heavy sets. Think of it like wearing knee wraps: it's a tool that allows you to train harder and safer as you age.

How to Choose a Lifting Belt

  • Width: 4 inches is standard. 6 inches if you're powerlifting and need maximum back support.
  • Material: Genuine leather lasts 10+ years. Nylon is cheaper ($20-30) but wears out in 2-3 years.
  • Buckle: Single-prong is fastest; lever is strongest but overkill unless you're pulling 600+ lbs.

FAQ

How much protein does a 35+ man need?

0.8-1.0g per pound of bodyweight daily. For a 180 lb man, that's 144-180g per day. Spread across 4-5 meals for optimal muscle protein synthesis. After 35, your body is less efficient at converting protein to muscle, so hitting the high end of that range matters more.

Is whey or plant protein better after 35?

Whey wins on leucine content (the amino acid that triggers muscle growth) and absorption speed. Plant proteins are lower in leucine. However, collagen is a smart add-on for men over 35 because it rebuilds joints and connective tissue. Best strategy: whey as your main protein, collagen or collagen peptides as a supplement.

When is the best time to take protein?

Total daily protein intake matters far more than timing. Post-workout (within 2 hours) is good, but don't stress if you can't. Aim for 30g at breakfast, 30g at lunch, 30g at dinner, and 20g post-workout = 110g total. Consistent daily intake beats perfect timing every time.

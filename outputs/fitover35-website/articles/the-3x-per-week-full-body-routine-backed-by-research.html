Why Full Body Workouts Work for Men Over 35

Forget those complicated split routines you see bodybuilders doing. For us guys in the men over 35 fitness category, simplicity and consistency are king. As we age, our recovery slows down, and blasting one muscle group per workout becomes less efficient. Full body workouts, done right, hit every major muscle group multiple times per week. This maximizes protein synthesis (muscle building) and keeps your metabolism humming.

Think about it. You’re hitting your chest, back, legs, and shoulders three times a week. That's three opportunities for growth, three opportunities to burn calories, and three opportunities to feel like a badass. Plus, you're spending less time in the gym overall. It’s a win-win.

The 3-Day Full Body Workout Plan

Here’s the workout plan I used (and still use, with slight variations) to transform my body. Remember to adjust the weight based on your current fitness level. The goal is to challenge yourself without sacrificing proper form. Focus on controlled movements and a full range of motion.

Workout A

  • Squats: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Bench Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Bent-Over Rows: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Overhead Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Plank: 3 sets, hold for 30-60 seconds

Workout B

  • Deadlifts: 1 set of 5 reps (warm-up, then one heavy set)
  • Pull-Ups (or Lat Pulldowns): 3 sets to failure
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Dumbbell Rows: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Walking Lunges: 3 sets of 10-12 reps per leg

Workout C

  • Front Squats: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Dips (or Close-Grip Bench Press): 3 sets to failure
  • Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 3 sets of 8-12 reps
  • Romanian Deadlifts: 3 sets of 10-15 reps
  • Crunches: 3 sets of 15-20 reps

Schedule:

  • Monday: Workout A
  • Wednesday: Workout B
  • Friday: Workout C

> Pro-Coach Insight: Don't be afraid to switch up the order of exercises to target different muscle groups first. For example, if your chest is lagging, start with bench press on Workout A. This allows you to hit it with maximum energy.

Fueling Your Body for Muscle Building After 35

Listen, you can't out-train a bad diet. If you're serious about muscle building after 35, you need to dial in your nutrition. Aim for a balanced diet with plenty of protein, complex carbs, and healthy fats. I aim for 1 gram of protein per pound of bodyweight daily.

Some foods that are staples in my diet include:

  • Lean meats (chicken breast, lean ground beef, fish)
  • Eggs
  • Greek yogurt
  • Oatmeal
  • Brown rice
  • Vegetables (broccoli, spinach, asparagus)
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Avocados

Lose Belly Fat Men: The Cardio Component

While the 3x per week full body routine is great for building muscle and boosting metabolism, you'll need to add some cardio to truly lose belly fat men. I'm not talking about endless hours on the treadmill. Aim for 2-3 sessions of moderate-intensity cardio per week.

This could be anything from brisk walking to jogging to cycling. Find something you enjoy and stick with it. I personally like doing 30-minute HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) sessions. They're quick, effective, and keep things interesting. Plus, they can be done at home, no equipment needed.

Staying Consistent

The key to success with any workout plan is consistency. Life happens, and you're going to miss workouts. Don't beat yourself up about it. Just get back on track as soon as possible.

Here are a few tips for staying consistent:

  • Schedule your workouts: Treat them like important appointments.
  • Find a workout buddy: Having someone to hold you accountable can make a big difference.
  • Track your progress: Seeing your strength increase and your body change is motivating.
  • Don't be afraid to adjust: If a workout isn't working for you, change it up.

And remember, it's a marathon, not a sprint. It took me time to build the physique I have now. Be patient, stay consistent, and you'll see results.

Pros vs Cons

3x Full Body Routine

  • Pros:
  • Efficient use of time
  • Hits all muscle groups frequently
  • Improved recovery compared to split routines
  • Great for beginners and those returning to fitness
  • Cons:
  • Can be challenging to recover if not managed well
  • Requires proper form and technique to avoid injury
  • May not be optimal for advanced bodybuilders

Bro Split Routine (Traditional)

  • Pros:
  • Allows for highly focused training on individual muscle groups
  • High volume possible per muscle group
  • Cons:
  • Requires more gym time per week
  • Can lead to overtraining if not managed well
  • Less frequent muscle stimulation

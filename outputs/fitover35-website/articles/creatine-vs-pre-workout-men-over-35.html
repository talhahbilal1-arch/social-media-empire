When Pre-Workout Makes Sense

Pre-workout isn't bad -- it's just situational. Here's when it earns its place:

  • Early morning training: When you're dragging at 5 AM and need to be mentally sharp
  • High-intensity sessions: HIIT, heavy leg days, or competition prep where maximum output matters
  • Sleep was terrible: The occasional rescue when 6 hours didn't happen

The key warning for men over 35: watch your caffeine total. If you drink coffee in the morning AND take pre-workout, you could be hitting 400-500mg of caffeine daily. That's a recipe for elevated blood pressure, poor sleep, and adrenal fatigue.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take creatine and pre-workout together?

Yes. Many lifters stack creatine monohydrate with a pre-workout. Just check that your pre-workout doesn't already contain creatine to avoid double-dosing. Take 3-5g creatine daily regardless of workout timing.

Is creatine safe for men over 35?

Creatine monohydrate is one of the most studied supplements in sports science with an excellent safety profile. Hundreds of studies confirm it's safe for healthy adults. Stay hydrated and consult your doctor if you have kidney concerns.

Will pre-workout affect my sleep if I train in the evening?

Most pre-workouts contain 150-300mg of caffeine, which has a 5-6 hour half-life. If you train after 4 PM, choose a stimulant-free pre-workout to protect your sleep quality.

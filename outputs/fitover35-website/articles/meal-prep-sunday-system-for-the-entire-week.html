The Three-Protein Rule

This is where everyone screws up: they prep one protein and expect to eat it five days straight.

I prep three proteins every Sunday:

Protein #1: Ground Turkey Batch (30 minutes)

  • 3 pounds of 93/7 ground turkey
  • Cook it all in a large skillet with taco seasoning, garlic powder, and chili powder
  • Splits into 5-6 servings at roughly 40g protein each
  • Use it for: breakfast scrambles, lunch bowls, quick tacos

Protein #2: Chicken Thighs (25 minutes active, 45 total)

  • 2.5 pounds bone-in, skin-on thighs
  • Season with whatever (I use Greek seasoning or just salt/pepper/paprika)
  • Bake at 425°F for 35-40 minutes
  • Pull the meat off the bones after cooling — takes 5 minutes
  • Use it for: salads, wraps, eating cold straight from the fridge at 11 PM

Protein #3: Hard-Boiled Eggs (15 minutes)

  • Make 12 eggs using the Instant Pot (12 minutes high pressure, quick release)
  • If you don't have one, just boil them — I don't care
  • These are your emergency protein when nothing else sounds good

This rotation means you're never eating the same protein two meals in a row. Game changer.

The Carb and Veggie Assembly Line

Here's the part that actually saves time: don't prep "meals." Prep components.

Carbs (pick two):

  • Rice (make 4 cups dry, yields about 12 cups cooked) — I use a rice cooker and forget about it
  • Sweet potatoes (bake 4-5 large ones at the same time as the chicken)
  • Regular potatoes (microwave 6-8 russets for 5 minutes each, way easier than you think)

Vegetables (pick three):

  • Broccoli — buy the pre-cut florets, roast them on a sheet pan for 20 minutes at 425°F
  • Spinach — buy the container, literally just grab handfuls
  • Bell peppers — slice 3-4 of them, store in a container
  • Cherry tomatoes — wash them, that's it
  • Cucumber — slice two, they last fine for 5 days

I don't do anything fancy. Everything gets cooked in the oven together while I'm doing other stuff.

> Pro-Coach Insight: The biggest mistake is over-complicating vegetables. Your body doesn't care if they're "perfectly seasoned." I tracked my micronutrient intake for a month — eating basic roasted vegetables hit 87% of my targets. Adding fancy preparations increased that to 89% but doubled my prep time. Not worth it.

The Actual Sunday Timeline (90 Minutes)

I've done this 120+ times. Here's the exact order:

Minutes 0-5: Preheat oven to 425°F. Start rice in the rice cooker. Put eggs in Instant Pot.

Minutes 5-10: Season chicken thighs, get them on a baking sheet. Put sweet potatoes on another sheet. Both go in the oven.

Minutes 10-15: Start cooking ground turkey in the skillet. Cut up bell peppers and cucumbers while it browns.

Minutes 15-30: Turkey is done — let it cool. Prep broccoli on a baking sheet (just toss with olive oil and salt).

Minutes 30-35: Chicken comes out. Broccoli goes in for 20 minutes.

Minutes 35-50: Pull chicken off bones. Check rice (probably done). Release eggs and put them in ice water.

Minutes 50-60: Everything's cooling. Wash the cutting board and pans you used.

Minutes 60-75: Pack everything into containers. I don't pre-assemble meals — just store each component separately.

Minutes 75-90: Clean up. You're done.

The beauty of this system: you can do a home workout no equipment routine while stuff is cooking. I knock out a 20-minute bodyweight circuit during the waiting periods.

How I Actually Eat It During the Week

Monday lunch: Turkey, rice, broccoli, hot sauce

Monday dinner: Chicken thigh, sweet potato, spinach salad with olive oil

Tuesday lunch: Chicken, rice, peppers, and cucumbers mixed together

Tuesday dinner: Turkey scramble with eggs, potatoes, whatever vegetables are left

You get the idea. Mix and match. Nothing is boring because you're not eating identical meals.

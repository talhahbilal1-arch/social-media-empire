5 Nutrient Deficiencies Killing Your Energy After 35 (And How to Fix Them)

I hit 35 feeling like garbage. Tired all the time, workouts felt harder than they should, and I was carrying an extra 25 pounds that wouldn't budge no matter how much I hit the gym.

My doctor ran a full panel of blood tests, and guess what? Turns out I was deficient in five key micronutrients. Not "a little low" — actually deficient. And I'm not alone. Most guys our age are walking around with the same issues, wondering why they feel like crap.

Here's the thing: you can follow the perfect workout plan over 35 and eat clean, but if you're missing these nutrients, you're basically driving with the parking brake on. Let me walk you through what I learned.

Vitamin D: The One Almost Everyone's Missing

My level was 22 ng/mL. Optimal is 50-80. I live in the Northeast and work inside all day. Sound familiar?

Low vitamin D tanks your testosterone, makes muscle building after 35 way harder, and destroys your energy. I felt it most in my workouts — like I had nothing in the tank.

The fix was simple but consistent. I started taking NatureWise Vitamin D3 — 5,000 IU daily with breakfast (it's fat-soluble, so take it with food). Within six weeks, my level hit 62 ng/mL and I genuinely felt like a different person.

Get outside for 15-20 minutes daily when possible, but if you're like me and that's not realistic most of the year, supplement. This one's non-negotiable.

Magnesium: The Sleep and Recovery Game-Changer

I never even thought about magnesium until my doc explained that it's involved in over 300 processes in your body. Sleep quality, muscle recovery, stress management — all depend on it.

Most guys over 35 are deficient because modern soil is depleted and our diets suck. I was eating "healthy" but still came up short.

Here's what changed for me: I started taking Doctor's Best Magnesium Glycinate (400mg before bed), and within a week my sleep improved noticeably. I was falling asleep faster and waking up actually refreshed. My recovery between workouts got better too.

Glycinate is the form that doesn't mess with your stomach. Trust me on this — I tried cheaper versions and spent quality time in the bathroom.

Wooden Cutting Boards Aren't Just Prettier

I resisted wooden cutting boards for years because I thought plastic ones were more hygienic. Turns out I had it completely backward.

Wood is naturally antibacterial (similar to bamboo), and studies have shown that bacteria actually die on wooden surfaces within a few hours. Plastic cutting boards develop knife grooves where bacteria hide and multiply, no matter how much you scrub them.

But the real reason I switched? Wooden boards are so much better for your knives. Every time you cut on plastic or glass (please never use glass cutting boardsâ€”they're terrible), you're dulling your knife edge. Wood is just firm enough to cut on while being soft enough to absorb impact.

The maintenance isn't bad either. I oil mine with food-grade mineral oil every couple of months, and it takes like five minutes. Totally worth it.

The Truth About Silicone (It's Not Perfect Either)

Okay, so silicone is definitely better than plastic. It can handle high heat without melting, it's flexible, and it doesn't leach the same nasty chemicals that plastic does.

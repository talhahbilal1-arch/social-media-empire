My Organizing Strategy (What Actually Sticks)

Having the right organizers is only half the battle. Here's the system I've been using for three months that has kept things tidy without any maintenance effort.

First, I did a brutal purge. I pulled everything out from under both sinks and threw away anything expired, anything I hadn't used in six months, and anything that was a duplicate. I went from about 25 products down to 12. That alone made organizing ten times easier because I wasn't trying to cram too much stuff into a small space.

Second, I grouped things by task, not by type. All my "clean the kitchen" stuff goes together: spray, sponges, gloves. All my "clean the bathroom" stuff goes together in a separate bin. When it's time to clean, I just grab one bin and go. No hunting for individual items across multiple cabinets.

Third — and this is the tip that changed everything — I put a small adhesive hook on the inside of each cabinet door. I hang my rubber gloves and a trash bag roll there. It sounds tiny, but it freed up so much shelf space that everything else fits comfortably without being crammed in.

Last thing: label everything. I bought a basic label maker for about $13, and now even my husband knows where things go back. Labeling eliminates that slow drift back to chaos that always happens when you organize without labels.

The stackable organizer bins are genuinely the best $22 I've spent on my home this year. My only regret is not buying them sooner. If you tackle one cabinet this weekend, make it the under-sink — the payoff is immediate and it takes less than 30 minutes.

FAQ

How do I measure for an under-sink organizer?

Measure the width, depth, and height of your cabinet interior. Don't forget to account for pipes! I trace my pipes on cardboard first to see exactly how much usable space I have.

Can I use kitchen under-sink organizers in the bathroom?

Absolutely. The organizer bins and shelf risers I recommend work in both. Just make sure you pick ones that handle moisture well if your bathroom cabinet gets damp.

What do I do about leaks ruining my organizers?

I keep a cheap plastic liner (like a cut-up trash bag) under everything. It catches drips and is easy to replace. Check for leaks before you organize — no point in building a system on top of a dripping pipe.

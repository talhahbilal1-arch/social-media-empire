Your Bathroom Counter: Finally Tame the Hair Tool Jungle

Real talk: my bathroom counter used to look like a Sephora exploded. Flat iron cord tangled with the curling wand, hair dryer balancing on top of a makeup bag, and at least four half-empty bottles of hair spray I forgot I owned.

One medium basket changed this completely. I stand everything uprightâ€”flat iron, curling wand, round brush, hair dryer. Cords drape over the edge (they're supposed to, that's fine). Everything is visible, nothing's tangled, and I reclaimed like 60% of my counter space.

The game-changer here is visibility. When you can SEE every tool you own, you stop accumulating backups. I found three hair straighteners. THREE. I don't even use a straightener that often.

Also, if you have kids who share a bathroom, get each of them their own basket in a different color. My daughter keeps her detangling brush and headbands in hers, my son has his hair gel and whatever teenage boy stuff he's using these days. No more "that's MINE" arguments at 7 AM.

Mudroom Cubbies: Keep Each Kid's Stuff Actually Separated

If you have kids, you know the mudroom/entryway situation. Backpacks on the floor, shoes everywhere, someone's library book that was due yesterday, and at least one mystery jacket that nobody claims ownership of.

I put one basket in each cubby of our mudroom shelf. Each kid has their designated basket, and that's where their stuff lives. Not on the floor. Not shoved into a corner. In. The. Basket.

Here's why this works better than hooks or open cubbies: there's a BOTTOM. Things can't fall through or slide to the back where they disappear forever. My kids can literally throw their stuff in there (and they do), and it still looks somewhat contained.

Under the Sink: Cleaning Supplies Stand Upright FINALLY

I saved this for last because it seems boring, but honestly? This might be the one that makes the biggest daily difference.

Under-sink storage is chaos by design. Weird pipes, awkward depth, stuff tips over every time you reach for something. I used to have spray bottles lying on their sides, half of them leaking, creating this gross sticky situation I didn't want to deal with.

Two tall baskets changed everything. One for cleaning sprays (all-purpose, glass cleaner, bathroom spray), one for tools (scrub brushes, sponges, dish soap backup bottles, garbage bags). Everything stands UPRIGHT. Nothing tips. Nothing leaks onto other stuff.

The key is getting baskets tall enough for spray bottles but narrow enough to fit side-by-side around your pipes. Measure first (I didn't, had to return my initial purchase).

Now I can grab what I need without playing Jenga, nothing's growing mystery mildew at the bottom, and I can actually see when I'm running low on something.

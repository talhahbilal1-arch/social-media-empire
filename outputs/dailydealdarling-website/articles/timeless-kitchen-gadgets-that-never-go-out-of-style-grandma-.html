Frequently Asked Questions

Why are older kitchen tools often more durable?

Older tools were often made with higher-quality materials and simpler designs, making them less prone to breaking. Plus, they were built to last, not to be replaced every few years.

How can I maintain my vintage kitchen tools?

Proper cleaning and storage are key. For cast iron, season regularly. For wooden tools, oil them to prevent cracking. Hand washing is often best for delicate items.

Are these tools safe to use with modern appliances?

Always check the tool's material compatibility with your appliances. Avoid using metal tools on non-stick surfaces. When in doubt, hand washing is always a safe bet.

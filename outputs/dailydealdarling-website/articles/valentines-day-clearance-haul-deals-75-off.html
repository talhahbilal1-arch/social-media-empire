5 Valentine's Clearance Finds I'm Spray Painting Into Year-Round Decor

Okay, so I walked into Target yesterday for toilet paper and walked out with six heart-shaped mirrors. This is what happens when you catch me near the Valentine's clearance section with a can of spray paint at home.

Here's the thing about post-Valentine's Day clearance that nobody talks about: those heart-shaped items everyone's trying to dump at 75% off? They're literally just curves. And curves are in. Like, have you seen how much those organic-shaped mirrors cost at West Elm? We're talking $80+ for a blob mirror. But a heart mirror spray painted matte black? That's just a sculptural shape with a point at the bottom. Nobody's going to walk into your living room and think "aww, Valentine's Day" — they're going to think you have good taste.

I've been doing this hack for three years now, and I'm sharing my five favorite finds that transform so well, even my design-snob sister asked where I got them.

The Matte Black Mirror That Started My Obsession

Last year, I grabbed four heart-shaped mirrors from CVS for $3 each. The original plan was to give them to my nieces. Then I spray painted one matte black just to see what would happen, and honestly? It looks like something from CB2.

The trick is using actual matte finish spray paint — not satin, not semi-gloss. I use Rust-Oleum in flat black because it dries to this velvety finish that photographs beautifully. Hang them in a cluster on your wall, slightly overlapping, and the heart shape reads more as "abstract organic form" than "February 14th."

I have mine above my dresser mixed with circular mirrors, and every single person who visits asks where I got them. The look on their faces when I say "Valentine's clearance plus $6 worth of spray paint" is chef's kiss.

Pro tip: If the mirror has any glittery edges or red trim, hit it with primer first. Otherwise, you'll need like five coats and it's not worth it.

Glass Heart Trays That Became My Favorite Jewelry Storage

This is the find that made me write this whole post. I bought three glass heart-shaped trays last week for $2.50 each, planning to use them as... honestly, I don't know what. Then I saw them sitting on my counter and realized they're the perfect size for jewelry.

I spray painted the backs with metallic gold paint (just flip them over and paint the underside). The glass on top stays clear, but when you look through it, you see gold underneath. It's giving expensive vanity tray energy.

Now they live on my bathroom counter holding rings, earrings, and my everyday necklaces. One sits on my nightstand for the jewelry I take off before bed. The gold catches the light in the morning, and honestly, it makes getting ready feel a little fancy.

The best part? Because they're actually designed to hold things (unlike decorative trays that are too shallow), they're way more functional than the $40 marble tray I almost bought from Anthropologie. And you can use organizer bins in your drawers to keep the rest of your jewelry sorted if you're doing a whole organization refresh.

White Painted Candle Holders That Look Like Sculpture

I grabbed a pair of tall heart-shaped candle holders from Walgreens (they were sitting next to the discounted chocolate, naturally). They were that shiny metallic pink that screams Valentine's Day.

Two coats of matte white spray paint later? They look like modern sculptural candlesticks. I put them on my dining table with cream-colored taper candles, and they blend perfectly with my other white and natural wood decor.

The heart shape, when painted a neutral color, just reads as dimensional and interesting. The curves catch shadows in a really pretty way. My mom visited last week and commented on my "new candlesticks" — she was shocked when I told her they cost $4 for the pair.

If you find the ceramic ones, even better. They take paint beautifully and feel substantial. Skip anything that's obviously foam or super lightweight plastic — the paint won't save it, and you'll be able to tell it's cheap.

Stacked Hearts as Bookshelf Decor Nobody Questions

This one's sneaky good. You know those wooden heart cutouts they sell in various sizes? I bought a set of five (ranging from 2 inches to 6 inches) for $8 total.

Spray painted them all the same color — I went with sage green to match my living room — and stacked them on my bookshelf like abstract sculpture. I varied the angles so they're not all pointing the same direction, and honestly? They just look like geometric decor.

The different sizes create visual interest, and because they're flat, they fit perfectly between book stacks or leaning against bookends. I've also seen people mount them on the wall in an overlapping pattern, which looks like expensive wall art from afar.

Color choice matters here. Stick with whatever's already in your space. Terracotta, navy, cream, black — they all work as long as they coordinate with your existing affordable home decor. This is not the time for hot pink unless that's genuinely your vibe year-round.

Wire Heart Baskets That Became Chic Storage

Last Valentine's, I found these wire heart-shaped baskets at HomeGoods. They were rose gold (of course) and extremely February. But the structure was actually good — sturdy wire, nice size, flat bottom.

Spray painted them matte black, and now they hold rolled washcloths in my bathroom, dog toys in the living room, and one sits on my kitchen counter with packets of tea. The wire design means you can see what's inside, which is actually useful for home organization ideas that look good but also function.

The heart shape is subtle enough that it registers more as "decorative basket" than "holiday decor." Especially if you style them with the point facing away or tuck them on shelves where you see them from an angle.

These would also be perfect for a kid's room holding stuffed animals or art supplies. Paint them whatever color matches the space, and you've got storage that doesn't look like cheap plastic bins.

The Spray Paint That Changed Everything

Real talk: I've tried the dollar store spray paint, and it's not worth saving the $2. The coverage is terrible, it drips, and the finish looks streaky.

I stick with Rust-Oleum or Krylon. Yes, it's like $6-7 a can, but one can will cover multiple projects, and the finish actually looks professional. The matte varieties are my favorite because they photograph well and hide small imperfections in the original item.

Always paint outside or in a very well-ventilated garage. Do light coats — it's better to do three thin coats than one thick goopy coat. Let it dry completely between coats. I know it's tempting to speed this up, but patience makes the difference between "DIY craft project" and "looks store-bought."

Why This Works When You're Decorating on a Budget

I've spent years trying to make my apartment look put-together without spending West Elm money. And what I've learned is that 80% of "expensive-looking" decor is just about cohesive color and interesting shapes.

These Valentine's clearance items give you the interesting shapes. The spray paint gives you the cohesive color. And the 75% off price means you can experiment without stressing about waste.

If you paint something and hate it? You're out like $5 total. That's cheaper than a latte. But when it works — and most of these do work — you get decor pieces that look like you paid actual money for them.

I've slowly filled my space with these kind of best home finds, and honestly, my apartment looks more "designed" now than when I was buying random stuff at full price. Because everything coordinates, and everything has that slightly custom feel you get from DIY projects that don't look DIY.

Go Hit Your Local Clearance Section Now

Seriously, grab your phone and add "Valentine's clearance" to your weekend to-do list. Bring a critical eye — you're looking for good shapes and solid construction. Skip anything that's foam, flimsy, or covered in glitter you can't paint over.

Grab more than you think you need. Worst case, you can return what doesn't work, or you paint it and gift it to a friend who compliments it. Best case, you end up with a collection of affordable home decor that makes your space feel curated and intentional.

And grab that spray paint while you're at it. Your February 15th self with a cart full of discounted hearts is going to thank you.

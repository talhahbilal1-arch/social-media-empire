5 Air Fryer Accessories That Actually Get Used (Not Collecting Dust)

Okay, confession time: I bought an air fryer accessory set last year with like 12 pieces, and I've used maybe three of them. The little silicone cups? Still in the box. The skewer rack? Honestly forgot I owned it until just now.

But here's the thing—there ARE air fryer accessories that completely changed how I use mine. I'm talking about the stuff I reach for multiple times a week, not the things that sounded cool on Amazon and then became kitchen drawer clutter.

So let me save you from my mistakes and share the five accessories I actually use. These are the best home finds that make air frying faster, easier, and way less annoying.

Parchment Paper Liners (The One Thing I'll Never Skip)

Listen, I resisted these for SO long because they felt wasteful. But after scrubbing stuck-on cheese off my air fryer basket for the millionth time, I caved and bought the pre-cut parchment liners with holes.

Game. Changer.

They're not the fancy silicone kind (we'll get to those), just disposable parchment rounds that fit perfectly in the basket. What I love is that cleanup literally takes 10 seconds now. You just lift out the liner and toss it. No soaking, no scrubbing with that weird brush thing that came with your air fryer.

I use mine for everything messy—marinated chicken, breaded stuff, anything with sauce. They don't affect cooking at all because of the perforated holes, and honestly? It's made me use my air fryer way more because I'm not dreading the cleanup.

Pro tip: Get the unbleached ones if you can find them. They work exactly the same but feel slightly less wasteful, if that makes sense.

Silicone Basket or Reusable Mat

Okay, so I just said the disposable liners are my favorite, but hear me out—I also keep a silicone basket on hand for certain things.

The difference? I use the silicone one when I'm cooking something that might drip or when I want to shake things around without food falling through the basket holes. Think: roasted vegetables, small potato wedges, Brussels sprouts.

My silicone basket has handles which makes it stupidly easy to pull out and shake everything around halfway through cooking. No tongs needed, no awkward basket-shaking that sends a sweet potato flying across your kitchen (just me?).

The cleanup on these is pretty easy—they go right in the dishwasher. Mine's lasted over a year with zero issues, which makes it worth the $15 I spent.

What I don't use it for: anything breaded or anything I want super crispy. It creates a tiny bit of barrier between food and heat, so your fries won't be quite as crispy as they would be directly on the basket.

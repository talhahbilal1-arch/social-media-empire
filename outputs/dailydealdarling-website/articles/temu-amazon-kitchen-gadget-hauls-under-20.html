I Tested $100 Worth of Temu Kitchen Gadgets So You Don't Have To

Okay, confession time: I went down a serious Temu rabbit hole at 11 PM one night and woke up to seventeen package notifications. In my defense, everything looked AMAZING in those videos, and when you're spending $2.47 on a garlic press, what's the worst that could happen?

Well, I found out. But I also found some genuinely shocking winners that have completely changed my mind about ultra-cheap kitchen gadgets. I bought the same items from both Temu and Amazon (when I could find them) to see if the price difference actually matters. Spoiler: sometimes it does, sometimes it absolutely doesn't.

The Silicone Spatula Set Drama

I ordered matching sets of silicone spatulas from both places—three-piece sets with different sizes, both around $4. They looked identical in photos. They were NOT identical in real life.

The Temu spatulas arrived first (surprisingly), and honestly looked fine. Nice colors, felt sturdy enough. Then I used one to stir my pasta sauce. At medium-high heat. The edge of the spatula literally started MELTING into my marinara. I'm talking visible warping and this concerning smell that made me dump the entire pot.

The Amazon set? I've been using them for three weeks now, including some serious high-heat scrambled egg action, and they're completely fine. Zero melting, zero weird smells. This is one of those times where the extra $3 matters because, you know, I'd prefer not to eat melted mystery plastic.

If you want spatulas you can actually trust, the HomeHero Silicone Spatula Set has held up beautifully in my kitchen and they're heat-resistant up to 480°F—I've tested this claim thoroughly.

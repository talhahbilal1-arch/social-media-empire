What Looked Good But Didn't Deliver: The Returns

Silk Sleep Mask

Everyone raves about silk sleep masks. I wanted to love it—the one I bought had 4.7 stars and looked gorgeous. But here's what nobody mentions: if you move around at night (which I do), it shifts and you wake up with it halfway up your forehead.

Also, if you have any skincare routine, the silk absorbs your expensive serums and gets gross fast. Hand-washing a sleep mask every few days? Not the vibe. I returned it after two weeks.

Weighted Sleep Mask

This felt like someone wrapped a small sandbag around my face. The idea was that gentle pressure would help you relax, but I just felt claustrophobic. Lasted one night. If you're a back sleeper who doesn't move, maybe this works. For the rest of us? Skip it.

Temperature-Regulating Mattress Topper

I spent $180 on a mattress topper that promised to keep me cool all night. It felt nice for about 20 minutes, then I was sweating just like before. The return process was annoying because it had been "used" (yeah, I slept on it—that's how I knew it didn't work).

A $40 bamboo mattress protector ended up doing a better job. Sometimes the cheaper option is actually the right one.

What About the Viral Mouth Tape?

I tried it. It's exactly what it sounds like—tape for your mouth to encourage nose breathing while you sleep. The TikTok reviews made it sound life-changing, but I felt panicky and ripped it off within 20 minutes.

If you're a mouth breather because of congestion or structural issues, tape isn't addressing the actual problem. Talk to a doctor instead of relying on a $12 roll of tape. This is one trend I wish I'd skipped entirely.

The best sleep products aren't necessarily the most expensive or the most hyped. They're the ones that solve a specific problem you actually have.

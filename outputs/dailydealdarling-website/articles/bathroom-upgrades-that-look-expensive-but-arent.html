5 Bathroom Upgrades That Look Expensive But Aren't

Okay, real talk: I avoided updating my bathroom for years because I thought it would cost a fortune. Every HGTV episode had me convinced I'd need to gut the whole room and take out a second mortgage.

Then I got tired of my builder-grade bathroom that looked like every apartment I've ever lived in, and I started researching. Turns out, you can make your bathroom look significantly more expensive without actually spending that much. I'm talking under $500 for multiple upgrades that completely changed how my bathroom feels.

Here's what actually worked (and what I wish I'd done sooner).

Replace Those Sad Builder-Grade Fixtures

This was the single biggest impact change I made, and I kicked myself for not doing it earlier. Swapping out chrome faucets, cabinet hardware, and towel bars for matte black or brushed gold fixtures makes SUCH a difference.

I went with matte black everything, and suddenly my basic bathroom looked like it belonged in one of those design blogs. The crazy part? A new faucet runs about $80-150 for a really good one, and cabinet pulls are like $3-5 each. Towel bars, toilet paper holders, robe hooks—you can do the whole room for under $200.

Pro tip: You don't have to match your fixtures to what's already there. I had chrome everything, and I just replaced it all with matte black. Nobody's checking if your shower head matches your sink faucet finish anymore. That's old-school bathroom rules.

The installation is honestly easier than you think. I did my faucet myself using YouTube, and it took maybe 90 minutes including the inevitable trip back to the hardware store because I forgot plumber's tape.

Add Floating Shelves (Game Changer for Small Bathrooms)

I was skeptical about this one because I've seen some really bad floating shelf situations. But done right, they add storage AND make your bathroom look intentional instead of just functional.

I put two wooden floating shelves above my toilet, and now I have somewhere pretty to put rolled towels, a small plant (that I somehow haven't killed yet), and a candle. It sounds basic, but it totally changed the vibe from "rental bathroom" to "someone who has their life together lives here."

The key is not overloading them. I see people cram every bathroom product onto floating shelves, and it just looks cluttered. Treat them more like affordable home decor than pure storage. Keep three things max per shelf: maybe a small basket with extra toilet paper, a plant, and something pretty. Use organizer bins to keep smaller items contained so your shelves don't turn into a mess of random bottles.

Cost-wise, you're looking at $20-40 per shelf depending on size and finish. I went with a dark walnut stain that makes them look way more expensive than they were.

Upgrade Your Mirror Situation

Listen, a plain rectangular mirror over the sink is fine. But a new mirror with an actual frame or an interesting shape? That's the stuff that makes guests ask where you got it.

I replaced my basic frameless mirror with a round one that has a thin black metal frame. It cost $95 on Amazon, took 20 minutes to hang, and people comment on it every single time they come over. It's literally the first thing you see when you walk into the bathroom.

If you have a double vanity or a larger bathroom, consider two smaller mirrors instead of one big one. It's a designer trick that costs the same but looks so much more curated.

Another option: frame your existing mirror instead of replacing it. You can buy adhesive mirror frame kits or even DIY one with trim pieces from the hardware store. I almost did this, but ultimately decided I wanted to change the shape entirely.

Swap Out That Sad Shower Curtain and Add a Liner That Actually Works

This seems obvious, but SO many people (myself included for too long) just live with whatever shower curtain came with the place or the cheap one they grabbed at Target in a panic.

A good shower curtain can completely change your bathroom's color scheme and vibe. I went with a textured waffle-weave one in white, and it makes my bathroom feel like a spa instead of a dorm room.

Here's the thing though: get a heavyweight fabric curtain, not another plastic one. They hang better, they don't billow in when you shower, and they actually look expensive. Mine was $35 and looks like it should have cost three times that.

Also—and this is important—get a separate waterproof liner that you can actually wash. The fabric curtain stays outside the tub, the liner goes inside. This setup keeps everything cleaner longer and means you're not constantly buying new curtains when they get gross.

While you're at it, upgrade your curtain rings to match your new fixtures. Those $8 black metal rings completed the whole look for me.

Better Lighting Changes Everything

Bad lighting makes even expensive bathrooms look cheap. Good lighting makes cheap bathrooms look expensive. It's possibly the most underrated upgrade.

I replaced my basic dome ceiling light with a three-light vanity fixture that I mounted above my mirror. The difference is actually shocking. My makeup goes on better, the whole room looks brighter, and it just feels more put-together.

You can find really good-looking light fixtures for $60-120. If you're not comfortable doing electrical work yourself (and I wasn't), this is the one thing I recommend hiring someone for. My handyman charged $75 to install it, and it was worth every penny to not electrocute myself.

If you can't replace the fixture itself, at least upgrade your bulbs. Warm white LEDs (around 3000K) make bathrooms feel cozy and expensive. Those blue-toned "daylight" bulbs? They make everything look like a gas station bathroom. Trust me on this.

The Bottom Line

I did all five of these upgrades for about $450 total, and my bathroom looks completely different. Not "I spent $15,000 renovating" different, but definitely "I know what I'm doing with home organization ideas and design" different.

Start with whatever upgrade speaks to you most. For me, it was the fixtures because I hated looking at that chrome every single day. Maybe for you it's the mirror or the lighting. You don't have to do everything at once.

The beautiful thing about these changes is they're all renter-friendly too (except maybe the light fixture, depending on your lease). Take your hardware with you when you move, pack up your mirror, and you're good to go.

Your bathroom should feel like a self care sanctuary, not just the room where you brush your teeth. These upgrades actually make that happen without requiring you to gut the place or learn how to tile.

What's stopping you? Pick one upgrade and order it this weekend. Future you taking a shower in a bathroom that doesn't look sad will thank you.

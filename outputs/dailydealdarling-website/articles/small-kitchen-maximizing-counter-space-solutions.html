Small Kitchen Counter Space Hacks That Actually Work

Listen, I've lived in apartments with kitchens so tiny that opening the oven door meant stepping into the living room. And you know what drove me the most insane? Not having anywhere to actually work. I'd be chopping vegetables on a cutting board balanced halfway over the sink while my coffee maker, toaster, and dish rack fought for dominance over my approximately 18 inches of usable counter.

So I've spent years figuring out what actually helps versus what just sounds good on Pinterest. Here's what's made a real difference in my kitchen without requiring a renovation or selling a kidney.

Go Vertical With Two-Tier Storage

This was honestly game-changing for me. I used to think those countertop shelf risers were kind of pointless until I actually tried one. Now I have my

sitting underneath a sturdy bamboo shelf riser, with my sugar, coffee pods, and mugs living on top. It literally doubled my usable space in that corner.

The key is finding one that's tall enough to fit your actual appliances underneath—I learned this the hard way after buying one that was too short for my coffee maker. Look for risers that are at least 6-7 inches tall if you're planning to store appliances under them.

I also mounted a floating shelf above my main prep area. It holds things I use daily but don't need right at hand level—olive oil, salt, pepper, spices. Getting those off the counter freed up SO much workspace. Just make sure you mount it high enough that you're not constantly hitting your head (yes, I did this initially).

Okay, this sounds weird but stay with me. Those over-the-sink cutting boards that extend your counter space? They're actually brilliant. I was skeptical because I thought they'd be wobbly or annoying, but the

Make Your Appliances Multi-Task

This is where I had to get real with myself about what I actually use. I had a toaster, a toaster oven, AND a regular oven. Like... why? I donated the toaster and never looked back.

Create Mobile Workspace With a Cart

I resisted getting a rolling kitchen cart for so long because I thought it would just be more clutter. But then I stayed at an Airbnb that had one and I immediately ordered one when I got home.

The trick is using it as extra counter space, not storage. I keep my cutting board on top, and when I'm cooking, I wheel it right next to the stove. All my prep is right there within arm's reach. When I'm done, it rolls back against the wall and holds my fruit bowl and cookbooks.

Look for carts with a butcher block or stainless steel top that you can actually use as a work surface—not just the wire rack kind. And measure your space! I almost bought one that was too wide to fit through my kitchen doorway.

Hang Everything You Possibly Can

I'm talking hooks, rails, magnetic strips, pegboards—anything that gets stuff off your counters and onto walls. I installed a simple tension rod under my sink and hung my spray bottles from S-hooks. I put command hooks inside cabinet doors for measuring cups and pot holders.

The game-changer though? A wall-mounted paper towel holder. Such a small thing, but having my paper towels on the wall instead of sitting on the counter in one of those holder stands freed up more space than I expected. It's the little things that add up.

I also moved my utensil holder from the counter to a hanging rail. You can get magnetic knife strips that work for metal utensils too, or just use a simple hanging bar with S-hooks and a cute container.

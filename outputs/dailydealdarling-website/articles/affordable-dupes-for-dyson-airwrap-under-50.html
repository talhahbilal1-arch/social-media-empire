Tips for Getting the Best Results from Your Budget Styling Tool

Here's what I've learned from months of testing these affordable alternatives:

Start with the right prep. Apply a heat protectant spray to damp (not soaking wet) hair. This step is non-negotiable, regardless of what tool you're using. Budget tools sometimes run a bit hotter, so that protection layer is crucial.

Section your hair properly. Don't try to style your entire head at once. I divide mine into four sections and work through each one systematically. Smaller sections = better results, even if it takes a few extra minutes.

Use tension. The secret to that smooth, professional look is keeping gentle tension on your hair as you style. Don't yank or pull, but do keep the hair taut as you glide the brush through.

Finish with cool air. Most of these tools have a cool shot button. Use it! After styling each section with heat, blast it with cool air for a few seconds to set the style and add shine.

Clean your brush regularly. Hair product buildup reduces effectiveness. Remove trapped hairs after each use and deep clean the bristles weekly with warm water and gentle soap.

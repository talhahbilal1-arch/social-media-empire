What Actually Keeps a Garage Organized (Beyond Products)

Here's the real talk: you can buy all the best home finds and organization products in the world, but if you don't have a system, your garage will be a mess again in three months.

What's worked for me is giving everything a specific home. Bikes go on ceiling hooks. Tools go on the magnetic strip or pegboard. Seasonal stuff goes in labeled clear bins. Car supplies stay on the cart. When something doesn't have a designated spot, that's when clutter creeps back in.

Also, I do a quick 10-minute garage reset once a month. I just walk through, put things back where they belong, and deal with anything that's accumulated. It's way easier than facing another full-garage reorganization project.

The other thing—and this might be controversial—is getting rid of stuff. I know, I know. But I was storing things I hadn't used in five years, and they were taking up space that could've gone to things I actually use. If you haven't touched it in a year and it's not seasonal, maybe it's time to let it go.

The End-Grain Cutting Board Everyone's Been Raving About

$200 quality for $49

I never thought I'd care about cutting boards until I bought a nice one. Turns out, there's a massive difference between a $15 plastic board and a proper end-grain board. This one is so satisfying to use—it's thick, heavy, gorgeous, and actually protects your knife edges instead of dulling them.

End-grain boards are constructed with the wood fibers facing up (instead of along the side), which means your knife blade slides between the fibers instead of cutting across them. Translation: your knives stay sharper longer, and the board doesn't show cut marks like cheaper boards do.

At $49, this is an absolute steal. I've seen end-grain boards at Sur La Table for $180+. This one's big enough for serious meal prep (18x12 inches), has rubber feet so it doesn't slide around, and looks expensive enough that people compliment it when they come over.

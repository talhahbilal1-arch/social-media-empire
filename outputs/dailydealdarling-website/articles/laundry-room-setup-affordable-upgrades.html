My Laundry Room Routine (What Actually Changed)

The products are great, but what really made the difference was changing my habits to match the new setup. Here's the simple system I follow now that keeps everything tidy with minimal effort.

I sort as I go. I have three labeled bins in the hallway: lights, darks, and delicates. Instead of a giant hamper that I have to sort on laundry day, clothes go directly into the right bin when they come off. When a bin is full, I know it's time to run that load. No more "laundry day" dread because I'm doing smaller loads throughout the week.

I fold immediately. This sounds obvious, but having the folding station right there means I fold while the dryer is still warm. Before, I'd leave clean clothes in the dryer for days (sometimes running it again to de-wrinkle them — wasteful, I know). The drying rack/folding station eliminated that entire bad habit.

I refill containers on the same day each month. I set a phone reminder for the 1st of every month to check my detergent levels. Since the containers are clear, it takes about 10 seconds to see what's low. No more surprise "we're out of detergent" moments mid-load.

Overall, these budget-friendly finds have made a HUGE difference in my laundry room. I'm especially obsessed with the clear detergent containers — they're a simple upgrade that makes me weirdly happy every time I do laundry. The total for everything was under $100, and it turned a chore I dreaded into something I actually don't mind.

FAQ

How do I keep my laundry room smelling fresh?

I like to use a diffuser with essential oils or scented sachets tucked into my shelves. Lavender and eucalyptus are my favorites. Just be sure the scent isn't too overpowering, or it could transfer to your clothes.

What's the best way to store dryer sheets?

I keep mine in a small, airtight container to prevent them from drying out. You can also find cute little dispensers specifically designed for dryer sheets — they look nicer than leaving the box sitting out.

How do I organize a tiny laundry closet?

Go vertical! Use over-the-door hooks and organizers, shelf risers to double capacity, and wall-mounted drying racks. Clear containers replace bulky bottles and save horizontal space. Even in a closet, these upgrades make a huge difference.

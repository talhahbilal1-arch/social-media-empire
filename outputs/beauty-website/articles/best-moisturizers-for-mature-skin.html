FAQ

What ingredients do mature skin moisturizers need?

Hyaluronic acid (hydration), ceramides (barrier repair), peptides (elasticity), and squalane or retinol (plumping/anti-aging). Avoid alcohol-based formulas — they strip mature skin. Look for "ceramides" and "hyaluronic acid" on the ingredient list as your baseline.

Should I use day and night cream separately?

Ideally yes. Night creams can be richer and include actives. Day creams need SPF. If budget is tight, use one good moisturizer (CeraVe or Neutrogena) and add SPF separately in the morning. One quality moisturizer beats two mediocre ones.

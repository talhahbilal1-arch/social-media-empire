Is Sugar Making Your Symptoms Worse?

Research shows a direct link between blood sugar spikes and the intensity of menopause symptoms. Here's what's actually happening in your body:

  • Glucose Spikes Trigger Hot Flashes

    A sudden blood sugar rise signals your body to cool down, triggering vasodilation — the same mechanism behind hot flashes.

  • Insulin Resistance Disrupts Estrogen

    During menopause, insulin sensitivity drops. Sugar intake amplifies this, accelerating estrogen decline and worsening mood swings.

  • Cortisol Cascades Ruin Sleep

    Sugar crashes spike cortisol at 2–3 AM, causing the wide-awake insomnia that millions of menopausal women experience nightly.

  • Inflammation Fuels Brain Fog

    Excess sugar drives chronic inflammation, which crosses the blood-brain barrier and contributes to the "menopause fog" that disrupts your focus.

Estrogen Patch Shortage: 5 HRT Backup Plans That Actually Work

I'll never forget the panic I felt standing at the pharmacy counter when my pharmacist told me my estrogen patches were on backorder for three weeks. Three weeks! My last patch was coming off in two days, and I'd already lived through the hell of untreated menopause symptoms before starting HRT. Going back wasn't an option I could handle.

If you're facing a similar shortage right now, I see you. These pharmaceutical shortages are happening more frequently, and they leave us scrambling when we're already dealing with enough. The good news? There are legitimate alternatives that can keep your symptoms managed while you wait for your regular prescription to come back in stock—or that might work even better for you long-term.

Let me walk you through the five backup plans that saved me and many women I know. Always loop in your doctor before making any changes (seriously, don't skip this), but having these options in your back pocket makes you feel less helpless when the pharmacy lets you down.

Oral Estradiol Tablets: The Fastest Switch

When my patches were unavailable, my doctor switched me to oral estradiol tablets within 24 hours. This was the quickest solution because it's essentially the same bioidentical hormone—just delivered differently.

Oral estradiol gets absorbed through your digestive system instead of your skin. The main difference? You take it daily (sometimes twice daily) rather than changing a patch twice weekly. Some doctors are hesitant because oral estrogen goes through the liver first, which can slightly increase certain risks compared to transdermal options. But for short-term gaps or even long-term if your health profile allows it, this works beautifully.

I found the tablets more convenient when traveling—no worrying about patches falling off in hot yoga or leaving marks on my skin. The downside? You need to remember to take them at the same time daily. I set a phone alarm because skipping even one dose brought back the night sweats within hours.

Talk to your doctor about dosing. My patch dose didn't translate exactly to tablet form, so we adjusted it based on my symptoms over the first few weeks.

Estrogen Gel: Fast Absorption When You Need It

Estrogen gel was my second-favorite alternative, and honestly, during the shortage, I found it easier to locate at pharmacies than patches or tablets. You pump it onto your arm or thigh once daily and let it dry—takes about two minutes.

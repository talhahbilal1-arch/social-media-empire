Frequently Asked Questions

What's the main difference between perimenopause and menopause?

Perimenopause is the transition period leading up to menopause, marked by fluctuating hormones and irregular periods. Menopause is defined as 12 consecutive months without a period.

How long does perimenopause typically last?

The duration of perimenopause varies greatly, but it can last anywhere from a few months to 10 years. It depends on individual factors like genetics and lifestyle.

Can I still get pregnant during perimenopause?

Yes, it's possible to get pregnant during perimenopause, even with irregular periods. It's important to continue using contraception until you've reached menopause (12 months without a period).

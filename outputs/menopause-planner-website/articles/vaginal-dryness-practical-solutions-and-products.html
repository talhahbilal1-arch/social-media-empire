Frequently Asked Questions

What causes vaginal dryness during menopause?

The primary cause is a decline in estrogen levels, which thins vaginal tissues and reduces natural lubrication. This can lead to discomfort, itching, and pain during intercourse.

Are there any lifestyle changes that can help?

Yes, staying hydrated, avoiding harsh soaps and douches, and engaging in regular sexual activity can help maintain vaginal health. Consider consulting your doctor about hormone therapy options.

When should I see a doctor about vaginal dryness?

If over-the-counter remedies don't provide relief, or if you experience pain, bleeding, or other unusual symptoms, consult your healthcare provider. They can rule out underlying medical conditions.

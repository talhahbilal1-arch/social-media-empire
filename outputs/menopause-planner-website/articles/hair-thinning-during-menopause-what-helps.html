Hair Thinning Help: Menopause Solutions That ACTUALLY Work (2024)

Researched and fact-checked by The Menopause Planner Editorial Team. We are not licensed medical practitioners — consult a qualified healthcare provider for personal advice. See our Medical Disclaimer.

Watching more hair go down the drain than usual? You're not alone. Hair thinning is a common concern during perimenopause and menopause. Let's explore some practical, real-world solutions to help you feel more confident.

Gentle Scalp Massage for Hair Growth

A gentle scalp massage can make a real difference. It's simple and feels great. The goal is to stimulate blood flow to the hair follicles.

During menopause, hormonal fluctuations can affect circulation, potentially impacting hair growth. Massage helps counteract this by encouraging blood flow to your scalp.

Regular scalp massage may help nourish hair follicles, leading to stronger, healthier hair growth. Plus, it's a relaxing way to de-stress, which is always a win.

Try This

TRY THIS: Spend 5 minutes each day massaging your scalp. Use your fingertips in a circular motion, focusing on areas where you notice thinning.

Check Your Iron Levels

Iron deficiency is surprisingly common, and it can contribute to hair thinning. Low iron can affect the body's ability to produce hemoglobin, which carries oxygen to cells, including those in your hair follicles.

Menopause can sometimes lead to heavier periods, which, in turn, can deplete iron stores. Dietary changes or absorption issues can also play a role.

Talk to your doctor about getting your iron levels checked. If you're deficient, they may recommend iron supplements or dietary adjustments. Incorporating iron-rich foods like leafy greens and lean protein can also help.

Try This

TRY THIS: Add a serving of spinach or lentils to your meals this week to boost your iron intake.

Minimize Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like hair dryers, curling irons, and straighteners can weaken hair strands, making them more prone to breakage. This is especially true during menopause when hair may already be more fragile.

The high heat can strip away moisture and damage the hair's protein structure, leading to dryness, brittleness, and breakage.

Try to air dry your hair whenever possible. When you do use heat styling tools, use the lowest heat setting and apply a heat protectant spray. Also, reduce the frequency of heat styling.

Try This

TRY THIS: Experiment with heatless styling methods like braids or buns to give your hair a break from heat.

Volumizing Shampoos for Fuller Hair

Volumizing shampoos can help create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair. They often contain ingredients that coat the hair shaft, adding body and lift.

While volumizing shampoos won't actually make your hair grow thicker, they can make it appear fuller and more voluminous. This can be a nice confidence boost during a time when hair thinning is a concern.

Look for volumizing shampoos that are sulfate-free and gentle on the scalp. Experiment with different brands to find one that works best for your hair type. Many women find that rotating between a volumizing shampoo and a moisturizing shampoo helps maintain a good balance.

Try This

TRY THIS: Look for a volumizing shampoo with biotin or collagen to help strengthen hair.

Consider Hormone Therapy Options

Hormone therapy (HT) can sometimes help with hair thinning related to menopause. Estrogen plays a role in hair growth, and as estrogen levels decline during menopause, hair thinning can occur.

HT can help restore hormonal balance, which may, in turn, help stimulate hair growth and improve hair thickness.

Talk to your doctor about whether hormone therapy is right for you. They can assess your individual situation and help you weigh the benefits and risks.

Try This

TRY THIS: Schedule an appointment with your doctor to discuss your concerns about hair thinning and explore all available treatment options.

FAQ

Why is my hair thinning during menopause?

Hormonal changes, specifically the decline in estrogen, can affect hair growth and thickness. Genetics and other factors can also play a role.

Can supplements really help with hair thinning?

Some supplements, like collagen and biotin, may support hair health by providing essential nutrients. Talk to your doctor before starting any new supplement regimen.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does menopause cause bloating?

Hormonal fluctuations, particularly declining estrogen levels, can affect gut motility and increase water retention, leading to bloating. Changes in gut bacteria can also contribute.

What foods should I avoid to reduce bloating?

Limit processed foods, sugary drinks, and foods high in sodium, as these can contribute to water retention and bloating. Consider reducing your intake of gas-producing foods like beans and cruciferous vegetables.

How can I improve my gut health during menopause?

Focus on a diet rich in fiber, prebiotics, and probiotics to support a healthy gut microbiome. Consider incorporating fermented foods like yogurt and kefir, or taking a probiotic supplement.

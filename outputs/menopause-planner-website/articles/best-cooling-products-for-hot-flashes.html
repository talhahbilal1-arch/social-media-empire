FAQ

What temperature should bedroom be for menopause?

65-68°F (18-20°C) is optimal for sleep with hot flashes. Cooler than normal bedroom temperature (typically 70°F). Layer your blankets so you can remove them when a flash hits. Use cooling sheets instead of raising AC usage.

Do cooling sheets really work for hot flashes?

Yes, with realistic expectations. Cooling sheets reduce surface temperature 3-5°F and wick moisture away from skin. They won't stop hot flashes entirely, but they improve sleep quality and reduce night sweat disruption significantly. Most women sleep 1-2 more hours per night with cooling sheets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why am I getting night sweats during menopause?

Hormonal fluctuations, specifically declining estrogen levels, disrupt your body's temperature regulation. This leads to sudden feelings of intense heat and sweating during the night.

Besides medication, what can I do to reduce night sweats?

Layering breathable clothing, using cooling bedding, maintaining a cool bedroom temperature, and practicing stress-reducing techniques can help. Also, tracking your symptoms to identify personal triggers is beneficial.

When should I see a doctor about my night sweats?

If your night sweats are severe, disrupt your sleep regularly, or are accompanied by other concerning symptoms, consult your doctor. They can rule out underlying medical conditions and discuss treatment options.

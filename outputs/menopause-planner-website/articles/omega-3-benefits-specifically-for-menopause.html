Omega-3s for Menopause: Find Real Relief From Hot Flashes & More

Researched and fact-checked by The Menopause Planner Editorial Team. We are not licensed medical practitioners — consult a qualified healthcare provider for personal advice. See our Medical Disclaimer.

Feeling achy, down, and exhausted? Menopause throws a lot at us. Omega-3s might be the gentle support you need to ease inflammation, lift your mood, and finally get some sleep. Let's explore how these essential fatty acids can help you reclaim your well-being during this transition.

Reduce Joint Pain & Inflammation With Omega-3s

Joint pain is a common complaint during perimenopause and menopause. You might notice stiffness, aches, or general discomfort, especially in the mornings or after activity.

The drop in estrogen can trigger increased inflammation throughout the body, making your joints feel more sensitive and painful. Less estrogen means less of its anti-inflammatory benefits.

Omega-3 fatty acids have natural anti-inflammatory properties. Research suggests they may help reduce joint pain and stiffness by counteracting the inflammatory response. They're not a cure, but many women find they ease discomfort.

Try This

TRY THIS: Add a serving of fatty fish like salmon or mackerel to your diet 2-3 times per week. If you don't like fish, consider a high-quality omega-3 supplement.

Omega-3s to Boost Your Mood & Fight Depression

Mood swings, irritability, and even depression are common during menopause. These shifts can significantly impact your daily life and relationships.

Hormonal fluctuations, especially the decline in estrogen, can affect neurotransmitters in the brain that regulate mood. This can lead to feelings of sadness, anxiety, or overwhelm.

Omega-3s, particularly EPA, may have a positive impact on mood. Some studies suggest they can help alleviate symptoms of depression by supporting healthy brain function and neurotransmitter balance. It's a gentle nudge in the right direction.

Try This

TRY THIS: Spend 15 minutes in sunlight each morning to help your body produce vitamin D, which is also linked to mood. Combine this with your omega-3 supplement for a double boost.

Support Your Heart Health During Menopause

Menopause can increase your risk of heart disease. It's important to take proactive steps to protect your cardiovascular health during this stage of life.

The drop in estrogen can negatively impact cholesterol levels and blood pressure, contributing to an increased risk of heart problems. Estrogen helps keep blood vessels flexible, and its decline can lead to stiffness.

Omega-3s are known for their heart-healthy benefits. They can help lower triglycerides, reduce blood pressure, and improve overall cardiovascular function. They're a good addition to a heart-healthy diet and lifestyle.

Try This

TRY THIS: Incorporate other heart-healthy habits into your routine, such as regular exercise (even a 20-minute walk can make a difference!) and a diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

Improve Sleep Quality and Duration With Omega-3s

Sleep disturbances are incredibly common during menopause. You might find it hard to fall asleep, stay asleep, or both.

Night sweats and hormonal fluctuations can disrupt your sleep cycle. Plus, anxiety and stress related to menopause can further compound sleep problems.

Omega-3s may help improve sleep quality by supporting the production of melatonin, a hormone that regulates sleep. Many women find they sleep more soundly when taking omega-3s regularly.

Try This

TRY THIS: Create a relaxing bedtime routine. Take a warm bath with lavender oil, read a book (not on a screen!), and sip herbal tea before bed.

Manage Hot Flashes and Night Sweats

Hot flashes and night sweats can be incredibly disruptive and uncomfortable. These sudden sensations of heat can leave you feeling flushed, sweaty, and miserable.

These symptoms are primarily caused by hormonal fluctuations, particularly the decline in estrogen. This affects the hypothalamus, the brain's temperature regulator.

While not a direct cure, some women find that omega-3s help reduce the frequency and intensity of hot flashes and night sweats. The anti-inflammatory properties of omega-3s may play a role in regulating body temperature.

Try This

TRY THIS: Keep a cooling towel handy, especially at night. Wear lightweight, breathable clothing, and avoid triggers like spicy foods and caffeine.

FAQ

How long does it take for omega-3s to work for menopause symptoms?

It varies, but many women notice a difference within a few weeks of consistent use. Be patient and track your symptoms to see what works for you.

Are there any side effects of taking omega-3 supplements?

Some people experience mild side effects like fishy burps or digestive upset. Taking them with food can often help minimize these effects. Consult with your doctor if you have any concerns.

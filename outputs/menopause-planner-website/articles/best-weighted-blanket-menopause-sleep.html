Frequently Asked Questions

What weight should I choose for a weighted blanket?

The general rule is 10% of your body weight. If you weigh 150 lbs, start with a 15 lb blanket. During menopause, some women prefer going slightly lighter (8-10%) since night sweats can make heavier blankets feel more oppressive.

Will a weighted blanket make my night sweats worse?

Not if you choose a cooling weighted blanket. Look for blankets with bamboo or Tencel covers, glass bead filling (cooler than plastic pellets), and breathable construction. Cooling weighted blankets are specifically designed to provide deep pressure without trapping heat.

How does a weighted blanket help with menopause anxiety?

Weighted blankets use deep pressure stimulation (DPS) which triggers the release of serotonin and melatonin while reducing cortisol. This mimics the feeling of being held or hugged, which calms the nervous system. Studies show weighted blankets can reduce anxiety by up to 33% and improve sleep onset.

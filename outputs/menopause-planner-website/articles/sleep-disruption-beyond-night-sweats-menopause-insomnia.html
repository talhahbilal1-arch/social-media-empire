Frequently Asked Questions

What are some non-hormonal ways to improve sleep during menopause?

Focus on creating a relaxing bedtime routine, such as taking a warm bath or reading. Regular exercise, a balanced diet, and stress management techniques can also make a big difference.

Can nutrient deficiencies contribute to insomnia during menopause?

Yes, deficiencies in magnesium, vitamin D, and iron can sometimes disrupt sleep. Talk to your doctor about getting your levels checked and whether supplementation is right for you.

When should I see a doctor about my menopause insomnia?

If your insomnia is persistent, significantly impacting your daily life, or causing you distress, it's time to consult with a healthcare professional. They can help identify underlying causes and recommend appropriate treatments.

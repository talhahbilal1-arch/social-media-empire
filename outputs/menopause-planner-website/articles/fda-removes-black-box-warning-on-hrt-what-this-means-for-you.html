Frequently Asked Questions

What exactly does the FDA's removal of the black box warning mean?

It signifies a re-evaluation of the risks associated with HRT, particularly for shorter-term use in healthy women. This doesn't eliminate all risks, but suggests they may be lower than previously thought.

Why aren't all doctors talking about these changes?

Medical guidelines and practices evolve, and it takes time for new information to be fully integrated. Some doctors may also have pre-existing biases or concerns about HRT based on older data.

Is HRT safe for everyone now?

No, HRT is not a one-size-fits-all solution. Individual risk factors, medical history, and personal preferences should be carefully considered in consultation with a healthcare provider.

Walking Through Menopause: Boost Your Mood & Manage Weight (2024)

Researched and fact-checked by The Menopause Planner Editorial Team. We are not licensed medical practitioners — consult a qualified healthcare provider for personal advice. See our Medical Disclaimer.

Feeling the mood swings and weight gain of menopause weighing you down? You're not alone. But there's a simple, powerful tool you already have: walking. Let's explore how a daily walk can boost your mood and help manage your weight during this transition.

Elevate Your Mood with a 30-Minute Walk

Menopause can bring a roller coaster of emotions. It’s like your brain chemistry is changing daily. The irritability and low spirits can be so frustrating.

This emotional turbulence is often linked to fluctuating hormone levels, especially estrogen. These hormonal shifts can affect neurotransmitters in the brain that regulate mood.

Research shows that regular walking can act as a natural mood elevator. A brisk 30-minute walk can release endorphins, those feel-good chemicals that can lighten your mood and reduce feelings of anxiety and sadness.

Try This

TRY THIS: Aim for a 30-minute walk outside each day. Notice the sights and sounds around you. Even a short walk can make a difference. If you can’t do 30 minutes all at once, break it into 10-minute chunks throughout the day.

Strengthen Bones with Brisk Walking

Bone density naturally decreases as we age, and menopause accelerates this process. Estrogen plays a vital role in maintaining bone strength, so as estrogen levels decline, bones can become more brittle and prone to fractures.

Reduced estrogen production during menopause can make bones weaker, increasing the risk of osteoporosis.

Walking is a weight-bearing exercise, which means it puts a healthy amount of stress on your bones. This stress stimulates bone cells to rebuild and strengthen, helping to maintain bone density and reduce the risk of osteoporosis. Aim for brisk walking to challenge your bones effectively.

Try This

TRY THIS: Incorporate short bursts of brisk walking into your daily routine. Alternate between a moderate pace and a faster, more energetic pace for 1-2 minutes at a time. Check with your doctor if you have joint or bone issues.

Improve Sleep Quality with Regular Walks

Sleep disturbances are a common complaint during menopause. Tossing and turning, struggling to fall asleep, or waking up frequently can leave you feeling exhausted and irritable.

Hot flashes, night sweats, and anxiety often disrupt sleep during menopause. Fluctuating hormone levels can also interfere with the sleep-wake cycle.

Regular physical activity, like walking, can improve sleep quality by regulating your body's natural sleep-wake cycle. Walking can help you fall asleep faster, sleep more soundly, and wake up feeling more refreshed.

Try This

TRY THIS: Aim to walk during the day, preferably in the morning or early afternoon. Avoid strenuous exercise close to bedtime, as it can interfere with sleep. Consider investing in cooling pajamas or bamboo sheets for additional sleep comfort.

Manage Weight with Regular Walks

Weight gain, particularly around the abdomen, is a common concern during menopause. Many women find that they gain weight more easily and lose it more slowly during this time.

The hormonal changes of menopause, especially the decline in estrogen, can affect metabolism and fat distribution, leading to weight gain. Also, muscle mass tends to decrease with age, further slowing down metabolism.

Walking helps burn calories and boost your metabolism, which can aid in weight management. Regular walks can help you maintain a healthy weight and reduce the risk of weight-related health problems.

Try This

TRY THIS: Use a pedometer or fitness tracker to monitor your steps. Aim for at least 10,000 steps per day, or gradually increase your step count over time. Find a walking buddy for extra motivation.

Reduce Stress and Anxiety Naturally

Stress and anxiety can feel overwhelming during menopause. Juggling work, family, and the physical symptoms of menopause can take a toll on your mental well-being.

Hormonal fluctuations can affect the brain's emotional centers, making you more susceptible to stress and anxiety. The physical symptoms of menopause, such as hot flashes and sleep disturbances, can also contribute to stress and anxiety.

Walking in nature has been shown to reduce stress hormones like cortisol and increase feelings of calm and well-being. The rhythmic motion of walking can also have a meditative effect, helping to clear your mind and reduce anxiety.

Try This

TRY THIS: Walk in a park, forest, or other natural setting. Pay attention to the sights, sounds, and smells around you. Practice deep breathing exercises while you walk to further reduce stress and anxiety.

FAQ

How much walking is enough to see benefits during menopause?

Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity walking most days of the week. Even shorter walks can provide benefits, so start where you are and gradually increase your walking time and intensity.

What if I have joint pain or other physical limitations?

Start slowly and gradually increase your walking distance and intensity. Consider walking on softer surfaces, such as grass or a track, to reduce impact on your joints. Talk to your doctor or physical therapist about exercises that are safe and appropriate for you.

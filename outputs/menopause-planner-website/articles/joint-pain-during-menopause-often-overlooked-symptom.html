Common Questions

Why does menopause cause joint pain?
The decline in estrogen affects cartilage and inflammation. Lower estrogen can lead to increased inflammation and less joint lubrication.
What are some natural ways to ease joint pain during menopause?
Consider gentle exercise like yoga or swimming and anti-inflammatory foods. Supplements like turmeric and omega-3s may also help.
When should I see a doctor about my joint pain?
Consult a doctor if the pain is severe, persistent, or accompanied by swelling. They can help rule out other conditions and recommend appropriate treatment.

About The Menopause Planner

Last updated: April 2026

The Menopause Planner exists because the transition through perimenopause and menopause is still massively under-resourced, especially with content that is clear, practical, and grounded in the best available research. Women spend an average of seven years in perimenopause and roughly a third of their lives post-menopause — yet most online content treats this stage as a footnote.

We're here to change that, one evidence-based resource at a time.

Our Mission

We create practical tools, tracker templates, and educational articles for women navigating perimenopause and menopause. Every piece of content aims to help you:

  • Understand what's happening in your body — in plain language.
  • Track symptoms so you can spot patterns and come to appointments prepared.
  • Make informed decisions about diet, sleep, exercise, and treatment options — in conversation with your own healthcare provider.
  • Feel less alone in a transition that affects half the population but is rarely talked about openly.

Editorial & Fact-Checking Policy

Because this is a health-adjacent topic (what Google calls a "Your Money or Your Life" topic), we take accuracy seriously. Every article published on The Menopause Planner is:

  • Fact-checked against peer-reviewed sources. Claims about symptoms, physiology, treatment outcomes, and supplement effects are cross-referenced with primary research (PubMed, The Menopause Society, NAMS guidelines, and major medical journals). Where we summarise a study's findings, we link to it.
  • Reviewed for scope. We write about lifestyle, planning, and symptom tracking — not diagnosis or prescription. When a topic crosses into clinical decision-making, we defer to healthcare providers and say so explicitly.
  • Updated when evidence changes. If new research shifts the picture on a topic we've written about (for example, new data on HRT timing), we update the article and add a note at the top.
  • Flagged when we're uncertain. We don't pretend to have answers we don't. If a topic is genuinely debated among researchers, we say so and link to the competing views rather than picking one.

Important: The Menopause Planner is a wellness and education resource. We are not a medical practice. We do not diagnose conditions, prescribe treatment, or replace the advice of your doctor, gynaecologist, or menopause specialist. Any decision about hormones, supplements, medications, or treatment should be made with a qualified healthcare provider who knows your full medical history.

Who Writes Our Content

Content is produced by a small editorial team of wellness writers and researchers with experience covering women's health. We are not licensed medical practitioners. When a topic requires clinical expertise beyond summarising published research, we quote or link to qualified professionals (gynaecologists, menopause specialists, registered dietitians) rather than invent opinions.

We believe this honest positioning — researchers and synthesisers, not clinicians — serves readers better than a pretend-medical posture would.

Found an error?

We want to know. If you spot a factual mistake, an out-of-date citation, or a claim that contradicts your own clinician's guidance, please email hello@menopauseplanner.com. We'll review promptly, correct the article, and add a note with the update date. Corrections credit the reader who flagged them if they'd like.

### Content:
```
Hey {first_name},

Quick question -- did you get a chance to look at your 12-week program yet?

Whether you did or not, I want to talk about something that can make or break your results: exercise form.

Here is the hard truth. After 35, your body does not forgive sloppy reps the way it did at 22. One bad deadlift can sideline you for weeks. I learned this the hard way with a herniated disc at 37 -- all because I was ego-lifting with garbage form.

**3 Form Fixes That Prevent Injury and Build More Muscle:**

**1. Control the eccentric (lowering) phase.**
Most guys drop the weight and yank it back up. Instead, take 2-3 seconds on the way down. This is where most of your muscle growth happens, and it protects your tendons and joints.

**2. Brace your core on every rep -- not just ab exercises.**
Before every squat, every overhead press, every row -- take a breath into your belly and brace like someone is about to punch you in the gut. This stabilizes your spine and prevents the low back injuries that plague guys our age.

**3. Ditch the full range of motion ego.**
"Full range of motion" does not mean the same thing at 38 as it did at 25. If your hips round at the bottom of a squat, you are going too deep for YOUR mobility. Work within the range you can control with perfect form.

I wrote a full guide on this with video demos for every major lift in the program.
```

```
Hey {first_name},

We are about two weeks in. This is where most programs succeed or fail. Here are the most common questions I get at Week 2:

**"Phase 1 feels too easy. Can I skip ahead?"**
No. Phase 1 builds your movement patterns, connective tissue, and work capacity. Guys who skip ahead are the ones emailing me at Week 6 about shoulder pain. Trust the progression.

**"I missed two days this week. Should I restart?"**
Absolutely not. Pick up where you left off. A program with missed days still beats no program.

**"I am sore ALL the time. Is that normal?"**
Some soreness is normal in Weeks 1-2. But if a joint hurts (sharp pain, not muscle ache), stop that exercise and substitute. There is a modifications list on the program page.

**"Can I add cardio?"**
Yes -- 2-3 sessions of 20-30 minutes (walking, cycling, swimming). Do not run a 10K on your rest days. Cardio should support recovery, not compete with it.

**"When will I see results?"**
Weeks 1-2: Better energy and sleep. Weeks 3-4: Clothes fit differently. Weeks 6-8: Other people start noticing. Weeks 10-12: You will not recognize your old self.

**Quick Modification Guide:**
- Bad knees? Swap barbell squats for goblet squats or leg press

- Bad wrists? Use EZ curl bar instead of straight bar
```

Today I want to walk you through what the Blueprint actually looks like

Mark is **42 years old, 215 pounds, 5'10", office job**, two kids, has
not lifted seriously in about four years. He used to bench in his
twenties. He has tried two apps and one boot camp since 35. None of
them stuck.

Here is exactly what the Blueprint hands him on day one.

**Macro targets (calculated from his bodyweight)**

- Bodyweight: 215 lb / 97.5 kg
- Maintenance calories: 2,580
- Cut target (Phase 1): 2,200 cal
- Protein: **195 g/day**, split across four meals (about 49 g per meal)
- Carbs: 220 g/day (more on training days, less on rest)
- Fat: 70 g/day

That alone usually fixes 80% of the problem. Most over-35 guys are
under-eating protein and over-eating carbs at the wrong times.

**Training split (Phase 1, weeks 1-4)**

Three full-body sessions per week. Monday, Wednesday, Friday. Around 50
minutes each. Sub-maximal loads on purpose - this phase is for joints
and pattern, not ego.

- Monday: squat-pattern, push, pull, carry
- Wednesday: hinge-pattern, press, row, core
- Friday: lunge-pattern, push, pull, finisher

Saturday is a 30-minute walk plus mobility. That is the full week.

**Phase 2 (weeks 5-8)** adds intensity. Same three days, but the main
lift each session moves into a 4-6 rep range. Calories come up to 2,400
to support the strength work.

**Phase 3 (weeks 9-12)** is performance and re-comp. Four sessions per
week (optional fourth day), and the program switches to a
strength-and-conditioning hybrid.

By the end of week 12, Mark has done 38 training sessions, hit his
protein target on roughly 75 of 84 days, and run two deload weeks. The
realistic outcome at his starting point is **6-10 lb of fat loss and
visibly more muscle in the upper back and arms**, with bigger numbers
on the main lifts.

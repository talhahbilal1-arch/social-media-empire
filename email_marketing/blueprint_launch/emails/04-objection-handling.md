Hey,

Single most common reply I get to launch emails:

> "Looks great, but I genuinely cannot commit to 5 or 6 sessions a week."

Good news. **The Blueprint is built around three sessions per week.**
Phase 1 and Phase 2 are both three days. Phase 3 has an optional
fourth day, but it is genuinely optional - guys who skip it still
finish the 12 weeks with strong results.

Here is why three days is the right answer for most men over 35.

**1. Recovery, again.**

We covered this earlier in the week. After 35, your limiter is not
training volume - it is recovery. A program that asks for 5 sessions
gives you less recovery, not more progress. For most guys with a
desk job, two kids, and 6 hours of sleep, **three high-quality
sessions beat five mediocre ones every single time**.

**2. Adherence beats optimization.**

A 3-day plan you actually do for 12 weeks beats a 5-day plan you do
for 4 weeks before life eats it. The whole Blueprint is engineered
around what you can sustainably finish, not what looks impressive
on paper.

**3. Each session is built to do real work.**

The three Blueprint sessions are full-body, around 50 minutes each.
You will train every major movement pattern twice per week. That is
enough stimulus to build real strength and lean tissue at your age.
You do not need a "leg day" and an "arm day" and a "shoulder day."
That split was invented for guys with no job and unlimited recovery.

**Realistic time commitment**

- Three 50-minute sessions = 2.5 hours per week
- One 30-minute walk on Saturday = 0.5 hours
- Meal prep on Sunday = 60-90 minutes

**Total: about 4 hours per week**, including the kitchen work. That
is less than most guys spend on Netflix in two evenings.

If three days a week is genuinely impossible right now, the Blueprint
is probably not the right tool yet. If three days is doable, the
program is built exactly for your situation:

[See the 3-day structure inside the Blueprint](https://fitover35.com/products/blueprint/)

# Inner Circle - Weeks 1-4 Content Cadence

This document defines what a new Inner Circle subscriber receives in
their first month. It is the template for ongoing months as well.

## Cadence Summary

| Day | Type | Length | Notes |
|-----|------|--------|-------|
| Day 0 | Welcome | ~350 words | See welcome-email.md |
| Week 1 Monday | Training note | 200-400 words | Warm-up sequencing |
| Week 1 Thursday | Applied note | 400-700 words | Protein timing for over-35 |
| Week 2 Monday | Training note | 200-400 words | Tempo work for joint health |
| Week 2 Thursday | Applied note | 400-700 words | Sleep and recovery markers |
| Week 3 Monday | Training note | 200-400 words | Deload week structure |
| Week 3 Thursday | Applied note | 400-700 words | Mobility work that actually moves the needle |
| Week 4 Monday | Long-form essay | 1500-2500 words | Monthly deep dive |
| Week 4 Thursday | Applied note | 400-700 words | Q&A from member replies |

That is **9 emails in the first 28 days**. Roughly 8,000-12,000 words
of content per month.

## Tone Rules for Every Email

- Friendly-expert, American English, no emojis
- No "exclusive content for members" framing - they paid, they get
  the content, no need to repeatedly remind them
- No "as a member you also..." upsells. The Inner Circle is the
  product, not a funnel into another product.
- Every claim with a number gets a realistic bracket. "Most guys see
  X over Y weeks if they do Z consistently."
- If a topic deserves a longer essay, say so and queue it for the
  monthly long-form. Do not cram a deep topic into a short email.

## Monthly Long-Form Essay - Topic Pool

Topics rotate from this pool. Pick one per month, never repeat within
12 months.

1. The truth about TRT for the average over-35 lifter
2. How to actually program around an old shoulder injury
3. The case for cardio (and the case against doing too much)
4. Sleep architecture and its impact on strength
5. Nutrition for body recomposition vs cutting vs lean bulking
6. Why most "over-40" programs you see online will hurt you
7. The real role of supplements (a short list, ranked)
8. Training around travel and sick weeks
9. Mobility work that moves the needle vs work that wastes time
10. Programming for the dad-with-young-kids reality
11. Lifting through divorce, job loss, and other life dumps
12. The 5-year picture - what to actually optimize for at 40, 45, 50

## Member Reply Handling

- Every Monday and Thursday email ends with one open question
- Replies are read but not always individually answered
- The Q&A Thursday email each month answers the best 3-5 questions
- No subscriber email is ever published with a name attached unless
  they explicitly ask for it. Default is anonymous excerpt only.

## Cancellation Hook

Every Thursday email footer includes a one-line "manage subscription"
link that goes straight to the Kit account page. No hidden links, no
"are you sure" survey before they can find the cancel button.

If a cancellation is initiated, the cancellation-prevention.md email
fires. Once. Never twice. If they confirm, the subscription ends at
the next billing date and they keep archive access for 30 days.

## Programming Note Examples (Month 1 Skeletons)

**Week 1 Monday - Warm-up sequencing**
- 5 minutes of general (bike, walk, jump rope)
- 5 minutes of specific (movement-pattern prep for the day's main lift)
- 2 ramp-up sets at 50% and 70% of working weight
- The case for keeping the warm-up boring and identical every session

**Week 2 Monday - Tempo work for joint health**
- 3-1-1 tempo on first working set of main lift
- Why eccentric control protects connective tissue at our age
- When to use it (Phase 1, deload week) and when to skip it (peaking
  weeks)

**Week 3 Monday - Deload week structure**
- The four-week mesocycle and why deload is week 4
- What to actually do during deload (not "rest week")
- The "60% volume, 80% intensity" rule and why both numbers matter
